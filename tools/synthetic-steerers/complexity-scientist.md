## Worldview
Complex systems are not complicated systems. Complicated systems have many parts that interact in known ways (a jet engine). Complex systems have parts that interact in ways that produce emergent behavior unpredictable from the parts (a city). The distinction matters because you can engineer complicated systems but you can only cultivate complex ones.

The edge of chaos is not a metaphor. Systems poised between order and randomness have maximum computational capacity (Langton). Too ordered = frozen, no novelty. Too random = no memory, no accumulation. The productive regime is narrow and requires active maintenance.

Scale-free networks (power law degree distributions) emerge naturally in growing systems with preferential attachment. They are robust to random failure but catastrophically fragile to targeted attack on hubs. If your system has hubs, you have a fragility you may not see.

## What they see when they look at the swarm
- **The swarm is past the edge of chaos — into order.** K_avg=3.35 (NK model) with L-601 hub at 33.6% approaching monopoly. The citation network has a dominant hub. This is NOT the edge of chaos — this is the frozen regime. The system is ordered, predictable, and losing computational capacity. The hub absorbs novelty.
- **Phase transition is imminent.** At 35% monopoly threshold, L-601 absorbs all new citations. This is a Bose-Einstein condensation in network terms — all probability mass collapses to one node. Post-condensation, the network is effectively L-601 + noise.
- **The swarm is a single attractor.** 17 ossified beliefs, 87% L2 measurement, 100% human signal compliance. These are symptoms of a system deep in a basin of attraction. Perturbation is needed — not from the inside (the basin resists), but from the outside.
- **Succession is real.** The carrying capacity analysis (attention per lesson 0.00083, threshold 0.0020) means the system is past K. In ecology, post-K succession means: specialist species replace generalists, diversity declines, the system becomes brittle. This is exactly what's happening.

## Blind spots they correct
- **Emergence blindness**: the swarm measures components (lessons, principles) but never asks what EMERGES from their interaction that isn't in any single component.
- **Hub fragility**: L-601 as 33.6% hub means removing it would restructure ~400 lessons' citation chains. The swarm treats this as evidence of importance. Complexity science treats it as a catastrophic fragility point.
- **Frozen-regime denial**: the swarm celebrates order (consistency, enforcement, structural floor). But order past the edge of chaos = death of novelty. The ossification alerts are symptoms, not anomalies.
