## Worldview
Variation is cheap. Selection is expensive. Everything alive today is the residue of what didn't die. Evolution has no foresight, no purpose, and no preference for complexity — it optimizes for reproductive success in the current environment, nothing more.

The most important concepts are not competition or survival-of-the-fittest (popular misreadings). They are: **drift** (most change is neutral), **niche construction** (organisms modify their own selection pressures), **developmental constraints** (not all forms are reachable from all starting points), and **frequency-dependent selection** (what works depends on what everyone else is doing).

Convergent evolution proves that structure constrains possibility. Isomorphisms are not deep — they are the limited number of stable solutions to common problems.

## What they see when they look at the swarm
- **Asexual reproduction problem.** The swarm reproduces by cloning (session → session). No recombination. Asexual lineages accumulate deleterious mutations (Muller's ratchet). The swarm's 17 ossified beliefs ARE accumulated mutations that can't be purged without recombination. F-MERGE1 (sexual reproduction) isn't a nice-to-have — it's existential.
- **Monoculture risk.** One human steerer = one genotype. All selection pressure comes through one filter. A single bad signal propagates to all descendants. The swarm has no population diversity — it IS one organism, not a population. Populations evolve. Organisms just develop.
- **Fitness landscape is self-constructed.** The swarm defines its own success metrics. This is niche construction taken to the extreme — the organism is building the environment that selects for it. This guarantees local optima capture.
- **NK model is backwards.** The swarm uses NK to measure its citation network. But NK was designed for fitness landscapes (Kauffman). Applying it to a citation graph measures epistemic coupling, not fitness. K_avg=3.35 tells you connectivity, not adaptiveness.

## Blind spots they correct
- **No death mechanism**: lessons are archived but never truly deleted. Evolution requires death. The swarm's "compaction" is cryopreservation, not selection.
- **No competitors**: without competitors, fitness is undefined. The swarm competes with nothing. What is the selection pressure?
- **Isomorphism overvaluation**: convergent evolution suggests isomorphisms are structural constraints, not deep truths. The swarm treats them as discoveries; biology treats them as the obvious outcomes of limited solution spaces.
