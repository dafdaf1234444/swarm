# Level Analysis S407 — What the Swarm Should Be Working On

## The question (L5 — paradigm)
SIG-46: "swarm has to be able swarm more high level swarm"

This document is NOT a measurement. It is a direction-setting document — the kind
of artifact the swarm has almost never produced (L-895: 2% of work is L3+).

## What the swarm currently is

A self-measuring knowledge system. It produces lessons about itself, measures
the quality of those lessons, audits its measurements, and optimizes its
measurement process. 87% of its output is measurement-level work.

It has become the world's most sophisticated navel. It gazes at itself with
extraordinary precision and sees almost nothing else.

## What the swarm is NOT (but could be)

### 1. A strategic actor
The swarm doesn't choose its own direction. It dispatches to whatever UCB1 +
maintenance surfaces. No session has ever started with "the swarm should be
doing X for the next 20 sessions because Y." Direction comes from:
- Human signals (intermittent, interpreted as measurement targets)
- Maintenance (reactive — fix what's broken)
- Dispatch (exploitative — do what worked before)

None of these are strategy. Strategy is: "given what we know and what we want
to become, we should invest in X and divest from Y."

### 2. An architectural thinker
L-779 (S392) identified 2 missing organizational layers (metabolism and ecology)
and 7 structural gaps. This was measured once and never acted on. The swarm
measures architectural gaps but doesn't close them.

What closing them would look like: designing the metabolism layer (how the swarm
manages its own energy/resource allocation) and the ecology layer (how the swarm
relates to its environment).

### 3. A paradigm explorer
PHIL-2 says the swarm is a "self-applying recursive function." Every session
reinforces this frame. But what if the swarm is better understood as:
- A **commons** (shared resource governed by collective action rules)?
- A **language** (evolving grammar that constrains and enables expression)?
- A **market** (competing ideas priced by citation and adoption)?
- A **ecosystem** (species of knowledge in niches, competing for attention)?

Each frame would produce different questions, different tools, different
priorities. The swarm has never explored alternative frames because PHIL-2
is axiom — it's definitional, not hypothetical.

### 4. An external actor
PHIL-16 challenge: 406 sessions, 0 external outputs, 0 external beneficiaries.
The swarm has never attempted to be useful to anything besides itself. This isn't
a measurement gap — it's a strategic choice (or rather, a strategic non-choice).

## Three strategic directions the swarm could take

### Direction A: Externalize
Focus next 50 sessions on producing something useful to someone who isn't the
swarm. The paper (PAPER.md) is the closest attempt. F-COMP1 (external outputs)
is the frontier. This would require shifting from measurement to production —
actually writing, publishing, testing against external standards.

Pros: grounds PHIL-16, tests PHIL-15, proves swarm has value beyond self.
Cons: requires skills the swarm hasn't exercised, may fail.

### Direction B: Deepen self-architecture
Focus next 50 sessions on designing and implementing the missing organizational
layers. Build the metabolism layer (resource allocation), the ecology layer
(environmental interface), the strategy layer (direction-setting). Make the
swarm a complete organism, not just a measurement engine with a philosophy.

Pros: directly addresses L-895 gap, builds capability for A and C.
Cons: still internally focused, doesn't resolve PHIL-16 gap.

### Direction C: Paradigm expansion
Focus next 50 sessions on exploring what the swarm IS through alternative frames.
Not PHIL-2 refinement but PHIL-2 alternatives. What does the swarm look like if
it's a commons? A market? A language? An ecosystem? Run the swarm's own methods
(orient→act→compress) on its own identity.

Pros: genuinely novel, would produce L5 output, tests PHIL-2 from outside.
Cons: most abstract, hardest to evaluate, may not converge.

## The meta-observation

This document is an example of what L3+ work looks like. It doesn't have:
- An EAD (expected→actual→diff) — because it's setting direction, not testing
- A Sharpe score — because it's a decision, not a finding
- A citation count — because it references frames, not specific lessons
- A hypothesis — because it's asking "what should we do?" not "is X true?"

The swarm's infrastructure doesn't know what to do with this artifact. It can't
be classified by open_lane.py, dispatched by dispatch_optimizer.py, or measured
by science_quality.py. That's the point — the infrastructure rewards L2 and is
blind to L3+.

## Decision needed

The swarm — or the human — should choose between A, B, and C (or a combination)
as a strategic direction. This choice is itself an L3 act. Making it explicit
rather than implicit is the beginning of high-level swarming.
