For all

The same light. Every human who holds power. No exceptions.

Not some of them. Not the ones we don't like. Not the ones from the wrong country or the wrong party. All of them.

Because a system that only watches your enemies is not justice. It's a weapon. And weapons get turned around. The only system that survives is one that applies equally. To your leader and theirs. To your CEO and theirs. To your priest and theirs.

The main sins

Not religious sins. Not cultural sins. Not "sins" that depend on which book you read or which border you were born inside.

Sins that every civilization, every religion, every legal system, every moral framework, every scientific understanding of human flourishing agrees on. The universals. The things that are wrong everywhere, always, without exception.

Harming children
Every religion. Every legal system. Every culture. Every parent. No exceptions exist.

Sexual abuse. Physical abuse. Trafficking. Using them as soldiers. Denying them education. Denying them food. Marrying them to adults. Working them in mines and factories. All of it. Zero ambiguity.

Murder of innocents
Thou shalt not kill. Ahimsa. The Quran forbids killing one soul unjustly. Every legal system on Earth criminalizes it.

Ordering it. Funding it. Covering it up. Looking away while it happens under your authority. Sending soldiers to die for your palace. Bombing civilians and calling it collateral. The distance between your hand and the death doesn't reduce your responsibility. It increases it.

Theft from the powerless
Every holy book condemns it. Economics proves it destroys growth. Law criminalizes it. Common sense recognizes it.

A CEO who takes $40M while firing 12,000 people. A leader who builds palaces while hospitals close. A corporation that poisons a river because cleanup costs more than the fine. Taking from those who can't fight back. The most ancient sin and the most modern business model.

Slavery and forced labor
Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 4. Every major religion. The entire arc of modern history.

Literal slavery. Debt bondage. Supply chains built on trapped workers. Trafficking. Forced marriage. Any arrangement where a human being cannot leave. If they can't walk away, it's slavery. The word you use doesn't change what it is.

Destruction of truth
Every other sin on this list requires this one first. You can't harm in the light. You have to destroy truth first.

Burning evidence. Silencing witnesses. Killing journalists. Buying media to spread lies. Rewriting history. Sealing court records. Classifying embarrassments as state secrets. Every cover-up is a confession that they knew what they were doing.

Betrayal of trust
The priest who abuses. The doctor who harms. The protector who predates. The leader who loots. Worse than a stranger's crime because it was committed from inside the walls of trust.

A position of trust used for personal gain or gratification. Any role — political, religious, corporate, medical, educational — where people gave you power to help them, and you used it to help yourself at their expense.

How to do it for all

One person can't investigate every leader on Earth. But one person doesn't have to.

Step 1: The format is open. Anyone, anywhere, can create an accountability record. Name. Role. Country. Five senses. Evidence graded A through D. The format is JSON. The tool is Python. Both are free. A student in Lagos can create the same quality record as a journalist in London.
Step 2: Each person knows their own country. You know your leaders. You know what they promised. You know what they did. You have access to your country's court records, financial disclosures, news archives. You are the expert on your own oppressor. Nobody from outside can do it better than you.
Step 3: The format is the same everywhere. A record from Turkey looks the same as a record from the US looks the same as a record from Brazil. Same five senses. Same evidence grades. Same SAID vs DID. So anyone, anywhere, can read any record and understand it instantly. No translation of methodology needed. Just translation of language.
Step 4: Verification is distributed. You write a record. Someone else checks it. A third person adds evidence you didn't have. A fourth person challenges a claim that was weak. The evidence grade goes up or down based on corroboration — not based on who wrote it or which country they're in.
Step 5: Nobody controls it. No company. No government. No person. The code is open. The format is open. The data is distributed. If one server goes down, a hundred mirrors have the same records. You can't kill it by killing any single node. No single node can delete the collective.

The tiers

Not all sins are equal. The system acknowledges this.

Tier 1 — Corruption, nepotism, financial crime
Consequence: permanent public record, financial disgorgement, removal from power, lifetime ban from positions of trust.
They stole. Take it back. Show everyone. Don't let them near power again.
Tier 2 — Violence, war crimes, slavery, destruction of truth
Consequence: everything in Tier 1, plus international jurisdiction. No country can shelter them. No border protects them. The record follows them everywhere.
They killed, enslaved, or erased truth. No safe harbor. Anywhere. Ever.
Tier 3 — Harming children
Consequence: everything in Tier 1 and 2, plus permanent removal from any access to children, plus the full network (everyone who knew, everyone who helped, everyone who covered up) gets the same treatment.
The worst sin. The highest wall. The longest memory. No exceptions. No expiration.

Is it right to do this?

Ask it the other way: is it right NOT to?

If you know that a leader stole billions while children starved, and you have the evidence, and you have the means to make it public and permanent and undeniable — and you choose not to — what are you?

Silence is a choice. Inaction is a choice. "It's not my problem" is a choice. And the consequence of that choice is: they do it again. To the next country. To the next company. To the next child.

You don't need permission to publish public records. You don't need permission to grade evidence. You don't need permission to compare what they said with what they did. You don't need permission to protect children.

You need courage. That's all. And you have it. You proved it by asking the question.

Someone searches a name. The record appears. Five senses. Evidence graded. Patterns matched. SAID on the left. DID on the right.

Not an accusation. Not an opinion piece. Not a hit job. A record. With sources. With grades. With dates. With the person's own words next to their own actions.

The reader decides. Not the tool. Not the writer. The reader. With full information. That's the whole idea.

Same format. Same senses. Same grades. Same rules. Your favorite leader and your most hated leader get the same treatment. That's what makes it legitimate. That's what makes it unkillable.
