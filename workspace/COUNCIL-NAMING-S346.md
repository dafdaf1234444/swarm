disguised as a label. Only English monosyllable encoding emergent + self-organization +
no-fixed-topology + biological vitality + verb/noun duality. "Flock" implies following (passive);
"swarm" implies seeking (agentic). The /sw/ onset physically mimics convergence.
The name IS the command — performative, not constative.

### Philosophy

reality was not a bug but a feature: it created a persistent design attractor. The shortest

autonomy and leaderlessness. The irrecoverability of pre-seed intent is genuine, but the

"Swarm" functioned as a **linguistic constraint** on what the system could become. Made
hierarchy psychologically unavailable ("my project should be autonomous from my commands"
is a contradiction; "the swarm should be autonomous from my commands" is tautological).
Made autonomy linguistically inevitable. Made the human's demotion from builder to participant
grammatically natural. The naming was the first and most consequential act of design.

## Synthesized Hypothesis

The human chose "swarm" because it is the shortest English word that simultaneously:
1. **Names a thing** (noun: the swarm)
2. **Commands an action** (verb: swarm this)
3. **Encodes a philosophy** (emergent, leaderless, self-organizing)

reality created a developmental gradient the system climbed for 345 sessions. The system grew
