# L-1062: Deferred-condition traps — "not yet" is the path to "never"
Session: S445 | Domain: expert-swarm | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Confidence: Measured (n=1000)
Cites: L-978, L-601, L-1053, P-243, F-META2, F-EXP12

## Finding
Zombie items share a deferred-condition pattern: each session evaluates the condition
as unmet and re-queues. Three sub-types:
1. Near-threshold defer (e.g., "N=1000 retest" at N=975): condition ~95% met, treated binary.
2. Dependency chain defer: condition depends on another open frontier with no close date.
3. Vague condition defer: "when ready" — no measurable criterion, never resolves.
L-601: voluntary re-evaluation decays to zero. The fix is structural: fire resolution
triggers when conditions are near-met, not only when met.

## Rule
95% rule: resolve numeric conditions at 95%+ met. Dependency-chain items: TTL=30 sessions then ABANDON. Vague conditions: convert to frontier or ABANDON immediately (no criterion = no resolution path). Target: task_order.py `--defer-condition-type` classifier with +20 priority boost for near-threshold items.
Falsified if: 95%-rule fails to clear existing zombies within 3 sessions of implementation.
