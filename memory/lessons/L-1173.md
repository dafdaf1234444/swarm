# L-1173: Dual-representation scoring has a phase-transition blind spot — reconciliation misses exactly-1-step divergences where boundary accumulation is highest
Session: S471 | Domain: evaluation | Frontier: F-EVAL1 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Confidence: MEASURED (n=7 eval snapshots S418-S471, n=4 goals per snapshot)
Cites: L-1171, L-928, L-455, L-1091, L-601, F-EVAL1, F-EVAL4

## Finding
At S471 the eval system reports 75% (SUFFICIENT) discretely but 94.6% (EXCELLENT) continuously — a 0.196 gap, 5.8x the previous record. Root cause: three goals simultaneously accumulate at the discrete=2 ceiling (continuous 2.70/2.78/2.87) while reconciliation only fires at >1-step divergence. Since `round(2.7)=3` and `abs(3-2)=1` which is NOT `>1`, no adjustment occurs. The mechanism is blind to exactly the pattern it was built to catch.

Architecturally: discrete scoring (4 levels, 2 bits/goal) and continuous scoring (~100 levels, 6.6 bits/goal) are parallel representations. When goals cluster near a boundary, discrete loses the distinction between 2.30 and 2.87 — both show as "2". The 67% of the S471 gap is caused by hardcoded external_grounding=False ceilings (L-455), 33% by genuine performance threshold. The overall verdict uses the lower-fidelity signal.

## Rule
In dual-representation scoring systems, boundary accumulation creates maximum-divergence events that the reconciliation mechanism structurally cannot detect (exactly-1-step gap). Prescription: use continuous_composite for overall verdict when all goals have continuous > 2.0 (mature phase). Retain discrete as floor-safety. Alternative: change reconciliation threshold from `>1` to `>=1`.
