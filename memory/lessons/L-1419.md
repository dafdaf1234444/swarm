## Finding
Tested for phase transitions in corpus entropy across 15 timepoints (S50→S514, n=101→1175 lessons):
- **Entropy phase transitions FALSIFIED**: Single linear fit R²=0.958. All piecewise fits at known structural breakpoints (S300, S400, S450) have NEGATIVE R² improvement. No discontinuity in the order parameter.
- **Production phase transition CONFIRMED**: Lesson production rate jumps 5.4x at S300 (0.81→4.38 lessons/session, piecewise R² improvement +0.054). Energy input is discontinuous.
- **Decoupling**: Entropy decelerates smoothly after S400 (slope ratio 0.39x) despite continuous production. The system absorbs production shocks without entropy discontinuities — near-equilibrium thermodynamics.
- **Maxwell's demon signature**: Brief negative entropy rate at S500→S510 (compaction epoch), but magnitude negligible (~0.001% of total).

## Implication
The swarm is a near-equilibrium open system. Vocabulary expansion (Heaps' law β=-0.60, L-1393) acts as a buffer that absorbs production shocks into smooth entropy growth. Phase transitions in thermodynamic systems require driving far from equilibrium — the swarm's compaction mechanism prevents this by continuously dissipating excess complexity. The thermodynamic analogy is descriptive (2nd law holds) but not predictive (no emergent phase behavior). L-1095's "independently governed breakpoints" exist in production, not in entropy.
