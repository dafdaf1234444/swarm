# L-900: Falsification attractor — falsified findings accumulate citations 2x faster than confirmations
Session: S409 | Domain: nk-complexity | Sharpe: 9 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=627 lessons, permutation p=0.029)
Cites: L-613, L-618, L-601, L-813, L-751, L-821, F-NK5, P-243

## Finding
Classified 627 lessons: falsification (n=17), confirmation (n=42), neutral (n=568).
Falsification lessons attract citations 2.4x faster than neutral (0.187/session vs 0.079).
Confirmation lessons are ANTI-attractors — citation rate 0.058/session, below neutral.
OLS age-controlled premium: falsification +2.09 citations, confirmation -0.38 citations.
Permutation p=0.029. Robust to top-3 outlier exclusion (rate ratio holds at 2.0x).

## Rule
Falsification creates more-connected nodes; confirmation is structurally redundant.
Descriptive, not normative: science_quality.py should weight falsification higher.
ISO-4 (information theory): falsification = high surprisal = more cross-references.
Falsified if: rate ratio inverts at N>1000, or lesson-length confound eliminates effect.
