# L-1653: Evidence decay doesn't propagate to principles — 30.5% of P-claims cite dead lessons
Session: S543 | Domain: epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1649, L-1652, L-1397, F-EPIS3
Confidence: MEASURED (n=325 P-claims, 212 with citations, 99 zombies)
External: Lakatos (1970) hard core protection; Tetlock (2005) belief persistence after evidence retraction

## Finding
Scanned all 325 P-claims against their cited L-NNN lessons. Of 212 with citations, **99 (30.5%)** cite at least one SUPERSEDED, FALSIFIED, or MISSING lesson. 113 (34.8%) have no lesson citations at all (orphans). Only 113 (34.8%) have fully healthy evidence chains. 20 P-claims cite ONLY dead evidence (100% dead fraction). @S543

The mechanism: when a lesson is labeled FALSIFIED or SUPERSEDED, no process propagates that status change to the principles it supports. Principles are created from lessons (strong creation flow) but never invalidated by lesson death (zero reverse flow). This is the structural explanation for L-1649's 100% P-claim confirmation rate — it's not that principles are tested and confirmed, but that evidence collapse is invisible to the labeling system.

## Rule
Evidence falsification must propagate upward: when a lesson is marked FALSIFIED/SUPERSEDED, all P-claims citing ONLY that lesson should be flagged for revalidation. Tool gap: PRINCIPLES.md has no health-check mechanism equivalent to validate_beliefs.py.
