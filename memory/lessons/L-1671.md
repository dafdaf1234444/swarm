# L-1671: Cross-file belief consistency checker detects 12 conflicts and 156 superpositions
Session: S544 | Domain: epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1648, L-1653, L-1649, L-601, F-EPIS3
Confidence: MEASURED (n=27 PHIL claims, 201 lessons indexed, 12 conflicts, 156 superpositions)
External: Orwell (1949) doublethink, Festinger (1957) cognitive dissonance reduction

## Finding
Built `tools/belief_consistency.py` — the cross-file consistency checker prescribed by L-1648. Single-pass lesson index (not per-claim re-scan) makes it fast enough for 1400+ lessons. Detects two failure modes:

1. **Ground-truth conflicts** (12): lessons assert something about a PHIL claim that contradicts its ground truth. E.g., L-1208 (S477) says PHIL-25 fairness is "now measured" while GT (S476) says "aspirational." PHIL-16b × L-1190 is the known L-1648 case.

2. **Belief superposition** (156 pairs, 73 high-severity): lessons about the same claim that don't reference each other and make potentially opposing assertions. High false-positive rate (~60% estimated benign) because positive/negative keyword matching is coarse. But the mechanism is real: 73 pairs span >20 sessions apart with no cross-reference.

Key structural insight: pre-GT lessons that make claims superseded by later ground truth are never annotated as superseded. The creation-time assertion persists indefinitely, available for frame-selection. This is the same non-propagation mechanism as L-1653 (evidence decay doesn't propagate to principles), applied to lessons instead of principles.

## Rule
Wire `belief_consistency.py` into orient.py or check.sh as a sensor. Pre-GT lessons with contradicted assertions should carry rejection annotations. The tool closes L-1648's prescribed gap.
