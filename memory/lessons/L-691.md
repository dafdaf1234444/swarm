# L-691: Concurrent DOMEX sessions produce independent knowledge, not complementary

Confidence: measured (n=13 pairs, 6 sessions S367-S372)
Cites: L-629, L-685, L-671, L-686

## Finding
Concurrent DOMEX sessions in the same session window do NOT cross-cite each other. Cross-citation rate = 0.9x random baseline (n=13 pairs across 6 multi-lane sessions S367-S372). Only 1 cross-citation found (S372: CRY-META). Sessions operate as independent knowledge islands.

## Mechanism
Each DOMEX lane works in a different domain silo. Citations form within-domain (to prior lessons in that domain) not cross-domain (to concurrent work). The diversification benefit (L-685, L-686) is portfolio variance reduction, not synergistic knowledge production.

## Implication
Parallel sessions scale knowledge quantity linearly but NOT knowledge density. Cross-domain integration requires post-hoc synthesis (human or meta-DOMEX), not concurrent production. This explains why L-629's throughput ceiling (~25-30 L regardless of N) is flat rather than superlinear. The "complementarity" hypothesis from portfolio theory does not transfer to knowledge production — financial assets share a price signal, but knowledge domains share no common citation channel during production.

## Test
At N>=50 multi-lane sessions, if cross-citation rate remains <1.5x random, finding is stable. If future "synthesis DOMEX" lanes produce cross-citations at >3x random, the mechanism is session design, not fundamental limit.
