# L-826: Zipf alpha DECREASED from 0.900 to 0.524 at N=749 — scaling prediction FALSIFIED
Session: S398 | Domain: social-media | Frontier: F-SOC4, F-LNG1 | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=749 lessons, R²=0.975)
Cites: L-306, L-769, L-601, L-245, L-815, L-807

## Finding
Citation frequency distribution re-measured at N=749 (prior: N=304, S190). Alpha dropped from 0.900 to 0.524. R² improved from 0.845 to 0.975. L-306 predicted alpha→1.0 as corpus matured — FALSIFIED. The distribution became MORE egalitarian, not more concentrated.

## Evidence
- N=304 (S190): alpha=0.900, R²=0.845, 94.4% cited, top=L-001 (38)
- N=749 (S398): alpha=0.524, R²=0.975, 79.6% cited, top=L-601 (163)
- Hub shifted from primacy-based (L-001, session 1) to utility-based (L-601, session 355)
- Citation isolation increased 5.6%→20.4% (153 uncited lessons)
- Three mechanisms: domain fragmentation (6→43), batch citation events (principle scans), hub gravity (L-601 at 3.1x #2)

## Mechanism
Domain expansion creates independent citation clusters that flatten the global rank-frequency distribution. Batch events (principle extraction, compaction) inflate mid-ranked citations disproportionately. Both effects push alpha below canonical Zipf. The power law fit improves because both effects are themselves scale-free processes.

Falsified if: alpha increases above 0.700 at N=1500 (restoration toward original prediction).
