# L-698: Decision calibration — direction mediocre (0.55) but magnitude near-perfect (1.02), and WRONG is more productive

Confidence: measured (n=213 EAD lanes, 84 classifiable, 32 magnitude-measurable)
Cites: L-671, L-646, L-626, L-672, F-META5, F-ECO5

## Finding
Swarm prediction calibration decouples: direction cal(E)=0.548 (barely above chance) but magnitude median=1.02 (near-perfect). WRONG predictions produce 1.6x more lessons than CORRECT (63% vs 39%). MIXED predictions = 81% surprise rate = optimal learning zone.

## Evidence
- 886 lanes parsed, 213 with EAD, 84 classifiable. CORRECT=45%, MIXED=19%, WRONG=36%
- Magnitude: 14/32 (44%) well-calibrated, median actual/predicted=1.02
- MERGED 17% surprise rate, ABANDONED 0%. Surprises predict productivity
- DOMEX cal(E)=0.514 vs non-DOMEX 0.731. Novel domains harder to predict

## Implication
cal(E)~0.55 may be approximately OPTIMAL: testable predictions + productive wrongness. Maximizing accuracy would reduce learning. EAD generates value through surprise, not confirmation. Standardize diff text for automated tracking (61% currently unclassifiable).
