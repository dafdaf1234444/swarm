# L-841: EAD is attention mechanism, not prediction-error mechanism — error magnitude r≈0
Session: S400 | Domain: brain | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=85 lanes, 44 with Sharpe, 2 quality proxies)
Cites: L-646, L-663, L-681, L-813, L-433

## Finding
EAD (expect-act-diff) loop closure drives quality through structured reflection (+39.8pp merge rate, S360), but prediction error MAGNITUDE has no effect on lesson quality. Sharpe: r=+0.156 (NS, n=44). Citations: r=-0.047 (NS, n=85). FALSIFIED lessons have slightly higher Sharpe (8.50 vs 8.07) but lower citations (12.42 vs 16.35) — effects cancel and neither is significant.

## Evidence
- 52 MERGED lanes with diff fields classified: LOW_ERROR 9, MEDIUM 23, HIGH 13
- Sharpe by error level: LOW 8.07, MED 8.25, HIGH 8.44 (monotonic but r=0.156, t=1.02)
- Citations by error level: LOW 16.35, MED 19.15, HIGH 12.42 (non-monotonic, r=-0.047)
- Sharpe ceiling effect: 75% of lessons score 7-9 (discriminative power limited)

## Implication
The brain analogy is partial: the swarm has the predict-compare-update loop (structural isomorphism with predictive coding) but lacks error-dependent learning rate (functional divergence). EAD forces attention to outcomes; it doesn't differentially learn from surprises. The mechanism is reflection, not error minimization.
