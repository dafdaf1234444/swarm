Systematic audit of 4 human-originated PHIL claims (PHIL-18, PHIL-25, PHIL-26, B20): 4/4 are AUTHORITY-CREATED — no evidence existed before the human signal. Pattern: signal→claim→evidence infrastructure, not observation→hypothesis→test.

Contrast with challenge resolution (S530, L-1543): evidence quality predicts survival with OR=8.5x (n=92, p<0.005). The epistemic mechanism works for evaluating claims. It does not govern creating them.

## Rule
PHIL-13 conflates two pathways: (1) challenge resolution routes through evidence (confirmed), (2) belief creation routes through authority (4/4 cases). The claim describes only pathway 1. Pathway 2 is invisible because human signals are treated as novel directions, not truth claims — but creating a PHIL entry IS a truth claim.

## Prescription
Update PHIL-13 to acknowledge dual-pathway structure. Creation authority is not epistemic failure — it's agenda-setting (McCombs & Shaw 1972). But claiming "truth routes through evidence" while authority routes creation is the motte-and-bailey L-1532 diagnosed.
