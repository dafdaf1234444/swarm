# L-1033: Scaling model predictions degrade past their fit range — empirical re-anchoring required

**Date**: 2026-03-02 | **Session**: S441 | **Domain**: Meta | **Level**: L3

Cites: L-912, L-1016, P-302

The swarm's quantitative scaling models (scaling_model.py) were fit on empirical data at N=325–401 (S305–S335). At N=945, the Zipf α power-law predicts α≈0.49 (critical period); empirical measurement at N=927 shows α=0.824 — a 68% error. The NK logistic model tracks better but has no empirical validation points past N=401. Both models are being used in the integration-bound phase (N>575) with no evidence they remain calibrated.

**Mechanism**: Power-law fits are stable near the data range and degrade nonlinearly outside it. A 2x extrapolation from N=401 to N=945 compounds fit error. The Zipf model was fit on 6 data points in the fragmented-island phase; it does not capture connected-core citation dynamics.

**Falsification target**: Re-fit scaling_model.py with empirical data at N=449 (α=0.969), N=927 (α=0.824), current K_avg≈2.0 (S357). If new fit changes Zipf α projections by >10% at N=1,200, the original model was significantly miscalibrated.

**Prescription**: Any tool projecting scaling behavior past 2x its empirical anchor range must display a "EXTRAPOLATION WARNING" and cite the last empirical point. Add to scaling_model.py output header.
