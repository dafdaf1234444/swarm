## Improvement-is-swarm (novel dimension)
Four signals tested — is the improvement process itself swarming?
1. **Distributed** PASS: Gini 0.408, 17/20 sessions contribute improvements (not concentrated).
2. **Recursive** PASS: 43.6% of tool commits modify or integrate with other tools (tool-on-tool).
3. **Sustained** PASS: velocity accelerating across 3 eras (2.05→2.81→3.63 improvements/session).
4. **Self-referential** NUANCED: meta lessons cite non-meta 1.8x more than meta→meta. Improvement is practice-grounded, not circular. This is a strength — the swarm improves itself by learning from domain work.

## Mechanism
The overhead floor broke because structural enforcement (L-601) + harvest integration eliminated pure-overhead sessions. When every session produces knowledge, overhead becomes a minority tax on productive work rather than a structural floor. The 33% "invariant" was an artifact of voluntary-compliance era.
