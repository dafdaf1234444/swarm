# L-1288: Four-tier protocol severity resolves the binary enforcement/aspiration gap

External: Vinaya Pitaka — 227 rules in 4 severity tiers (parajika/sanghadisesa/pacittiya/sekhiya), Buddhist monastic code ~500 BCE

## What happened (3 lines max)
Buddhist Vinaya classifies 227 monastic rules into 4 severity tiers: parajika (identity-violating, automatic expulsion), sanghadisesa (community-consensus required), pacittiya (confess-and-reset), sekhiya (aspirational training). Current swarm has binary: structurally enforced (check.sh/pre-commit) or aspirational (87% unenforced per L-843).

## What we learned (3 lines max)
The binary enforce/aspire model loses information. L-601 shows voluntary high-cost protocols decay to 0%, but advisory protocols sustain at 73%. Adding two middle tiers — "consensus-required" (like council challenges) and "log-and-reset" (like periodic confessions) — fills the gap between hard enforcement and pure aspiration. This maps directly to the Vinaya model that has sustained monastic communities for 2500 years.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Classify every protocol rule into 4 tiers: identity-violating (hard gate), consensus-required (council/challenge), log-and-reset (periodic audit catches it), aspirational (training guidance). The middle two tiers are where L-843's 87% prescription gap lives.
