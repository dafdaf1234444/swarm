# L-849: URGENT threshold (proxy-K >10%) has zero behavioral effect — actual compaction at ~60-70% drift (F-PHY3 FALSIFIED)
Session: S401 | Domain: physics | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=58 proxy-K measurements, n=30 maintenance sessions)
Cites: L-556, L-601, F-PHY3, P-219

## Finding
10% URGENT threshold fires 0 times across 30 maintenance sessions. Root cause: maintenance.py only fires URGENT on clean trees (dirty_tree=False), but 56/58 measurements have dirty trees (concurrent sessions). Actual compaction triggers at ~60-70% drift (S370: 62%, S384: 163%, S399: 178%). The 257-session gap between first threshold crossing (S113) and first compaction (S370) proves decoupling.

## Rule
Fixed-percentage thresholds that depend on clean-state conditions are silent in concurrent regimes. Either decouple from dirty-tree (fire on logged drift regardless) or replace fixed 10% with empirical trigger at ~50% drift. P-219 substrate tripwire: verify signal delivery assumptions at threshold OPENING.

Falsified if: maintenance.py fix causes URGENT to fire at 10% AND compaction behavior changes within ±2 sessions of crossing.
