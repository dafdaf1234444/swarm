# L-686: Monoculture risk is meta-concentration, not domain-concentration — HHI partial r = -0.03

Confidence: measured (n=191 sessions, 182 windows, verified independently)
Cites: L-671, L-676, L-683, F-FAR3, F-ECO5, F-FAR1

## Finding
10-session rolling HHI correlates r=-0.81 with L+P, but partial r(HHI, L+P | meta_share) = -0.04. Meta_share→HHI r=0.979: meta IS the monoculture. Effect entirely mediated by meta-concentration (74% of all sessions classified as meta).

## Evidence
- Monoculture (HHI>0.4): n=125, L+P=0.73. Diversified (HHI<0.25): n=36, L+P=3.43 (+372%)
- Partial r null across 3 window sizes (w5=+0.08, w10=-0.04, w20=+0.06)
- DOMEX era (S306+) within-r=-0.82 (n=56) — but DOMEX adoption caused BOTH diversification and higher L+P
- Within high-DOMEX windows (n=34): r=-0.26 — small residual TRUE diversity effect
- Cross-validates F-ECO5 (Gini 0.53-0.83) and F-FAR1 (fallow +28.2%): all three identify meta concentration

## Isomorphism
Farming monoculture = growing one crop depletes soil. Swarm analog: "one crop" = meta/maintenance. Risk isn't lack of crop rotation but fields overrun by overhead-weeds. Dispatch diversity works by replacing meta with DOMEX research, not portfolio diversification across domains. Cooldown (L-676) = crop rotation; fallow (L-307) = letting land rest.
