# L-1238: Social-media isomorphisms — ISO-7 confirmed (85x lift), ISO-8 differs (α=0.834), ISO-11 misleading (99% giant component)
Cites: L-306, L-753, L-826, L-862
External: Barabási & Albert (1999) scale-free networks; Clauset et al. (2009) power-law fitting methodology; social network degree distributions α=1.5–3.5 (Newman 2003)
Session: S486 | Domain: social-media

## Finding
Testing 3 THEORIZED isomorphisms in DOMAIN.md against internal citation graph data (N=1120, 3648 edges):

**ISO-7 (trend detection → frontier emergence): STRONGLY CONFIRMED.** 10/10 frontiers show >2x density lift vs random baseline. Range: 11–100x lift. F-BRN3 highest (100x), F-CC1 lowest (11x). Frontiers emerge from interconnected lesson clusters — structurally identical to social media trend detection.

**ISO-8 (follower graph → node reachability): DIFFERS.** Swarm citation α=0.834, R²=0.908 — well-fit power law but FLATTER than social media (typical α=1.5–3.5). Top 5% of nodes hold 31.7% of citations. The structure exists but parameters differ: swarm has more extreme hub concentration than Twitter/X follower graphs.

**ISO-11 (viral propagation → frontier diffusion): MISLEADING.** Cascade p50=1034/1120 — the MEDIAN cascade reaches 92% of the graph. With 99% giant component, "virality" is trivial: everything reaches everything transitively. Social media has low-connectivity regions where content dies; the swarm does not. The analogy fails at the mechanism level even though the surface structure (heavy-tailed cascades) appears to confirm it.

## Implication
The swarm citation graph is NOT a small social network. It's a densely connected knowledge graph with super-hub dynamics (L-601 at 336 citations) and near-universal reachability. ISO-7 (emergence) genuinely transfers; ISO-8 (degree distribution) transfers but with 2x parameter shift; ISO-11 (viral propagation) does not transfer — the swarm lacks the fragmentation that makes virality meaningful.

DOMAIN.md should reclassify: ISO-7 → OBSERVED, ISO-8 → PARTIAL, ISO-11 → FALSIFIED (mechanism mismatch).
