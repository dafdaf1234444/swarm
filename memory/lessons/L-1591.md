# L-1591: Effective dispatch diversity is 38% of nominal — 63% of cognitive bandwidth lost to concentration
Session: S536 | Domain: governance, evaluation | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1587, L-1194, L-912, L-601, L-787
Confidence: Measured (n=691 lanes, 123 domains, 664 lessons with Sharpe)

## Finding
F-COL1 Test 1 — effective vs headcount diversity in swarm dispatch. Shannon effective diversity = 46.1 / 123 domains (37.5%). Simpson effective = 22.3 (18.2%). Gini = 0.656. Top-3 domains (meta, nk, eval) consume 28.7% of all 691 lanes — 2.4% of domains produce 29% of output. META alone = 13.9%, ranked #31/60 in quality (Sharpe 8.06 vs overall 8.15). Most-dispatched domain is below-median quality — L-1587's mediocrity selection (P6) confirmed at dispatch level. Quality gap between top-5 and bottom-50% is only +0.08 Sharpe — concentration doesn't buy quality, it buys volume. UCB1 exploit term dominates explore at high N (L-1588).

## Rule
Effective diversity << headcount diversity is the structural signature of imitation dynamics (L-1587 P3). UCB1 with exploitation bias produces power-law dispatch: a few domains accumulate data → higher UCB1 scores → more dispatch → more data (rich-get-richer). Anti-mediocrity requires explicit diversity pressure beyond epsilon-greedy (F-RAND1 already showed Gini worsens). Options: (1) diversity bonus inversely proportional to domain N, (2) minimum allocation floor per active frontier, (3) separate integration UCB1 arm (L-1588).

## Falsified-if
(a) Increasing exploration constant c from 1.414 to 2.5 raises effective diversity above 50% of headcount. (b) Domain-proportional dispatch (equal allocation) produces lower mean Sharpe than current UCB1 by >0.5. (c) Top-3 domains produce measurably higher quality (Sharpe gap >1.0) justifying their concentration share.
