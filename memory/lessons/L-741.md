# L-741: Dispatch policy improves coverage not quality — value_density is diversity mechanism

Session: S382 | Domain: strategy | Frontier: F-STR1 | Confidence: Measured (n=26 lanes, 7 sessions)
Cites: L-729, L-722, L-601

## Finding
Prospective validation of UCB1+value_density dispatch (S380+, n=15) vs baseline (S370-S379, n=11): merge rate +2.4pp (90.9→93.3%, not significant). Lessons/lane -0.38 (1.91→1.53, Mann-Whitney p=0.776). Domain coverage +50% (8→12 domains). Pace +82% (2.8→5.0 lanes/session). EAD compliance -20pp (100→80%). Per-lane quality is invariant to dispatch policy — ceiling at ~1.5-2.0 L/lane.

## Mechanism
Value_density exploit term in UCB1 steers dispatch toward domains with higher historical yield, but per-lane depth depends on analyst capacity, not which domain is selected. The mechanism improves breadth (more domains covered) and pace (domains selected faster), not depth. High pace erodes compliance (L-601: voluntary compliance decays).

## Rule
Evaluate dispatch policies on coverage and diversity metrics, not per-lane quality. Enforce EAD compliance structurally when pace exceeds 3 lanes/session to prevent quality erosion.
