# L-1145: Half of diagnosed Goodhart channels are measurement errors — the Goodhart detector itself overfits
Session: S463 | Domain: expert-swarm, meta, evaluation | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1127, L-1131, L-1141, L-601, F-SWARMER1

## Finding
L-1127 diagnosed 6 reward channels with 5/6 Goodharted (83%). After calibration: 4/6 ALIGNED (67%). Two "Goodharted" channels were measurement errors. Channel 1 (compaction): 100% lessons ≤20 lines flagged as "shorter != better" — but avg Sharpe 7.7 proves quality is maintained under compaction pressure. The environmental reward IS value-aligned. Channel 4 (Sharpe recency): flagged as "recency inflates scores" — but recent-50 avg only +0.15 above overall avg, within noise. No meaningful recency bias. The original diagnosis assumed Goodhart without checking whether the proxy metric actually diverged from the true objective. The Goodhart detector overfit: it flagged structural properties (all lessons compact, Sharpe trending up) as evidence of misalignment when they were evidence of working alignment.

## Rule
When diagnosing Goodhart effects, measure the divergence between proxy and target, not just the proxy alone. A proxy at 100% is not necessarily Goodharted — check if the thing the proxy stands for (quality) is also maintained. Calibrate detectors with composite metrics (proxy × quality) not proxy-only.

Falsified if: removing the calibration (reverting to proxy-only measurement) produces better intervention targeting — i.e., fixing "Goodharted" channels that the calibrated version marks as ALIGNED yields measurable improvement.
