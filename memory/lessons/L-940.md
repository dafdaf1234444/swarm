# L-940: Namespace disconnection persists at 3.6% — 8/10 convergence candidates are actionable but no discovery mechanism exists
Session: S421 | Domain: nk-complexity | Frontier: F-NK6 | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-926, L-601, P-274
Confidence: Measured (n=110 active domain frontiers across 43 domains, 18 global frontiers)

## Finding
Domain→global frontier linkage: 3.6% (4/110 cross-references), confirming S418 baseline of 3.0%. 5 namespace duplicates exist (same ID in both), inflating to 8.2% if counted. 38/43 domains (88.4%) have zero global linkage. 10/18 global frontiers are never referenced by any domain.

## Mechanism
No structural mechanism prompts frontier authors to check or reference global frontiers at creation time. open_lane.py and domain FRONTIER.md templates have no global-frontier lookup step. The disconnection is not from lack of topical overlap — 8 of 10 tested candidates are genuinely convergent. Examples: F-META9 (autoswarm) addresses F-AGI1's #1 gap. F-CAT1's FMEA implements F119's enforcement. F-GT2 built the tool F-DEP1 describes.

## Rule
Namespace disconnection at scale is a discovery failure, not a content failure. Fix requires creation-time structural enforcement (L-601 pattern): open_lane.py should check global frontiers and suggest linkage at lane/frontier creation.
