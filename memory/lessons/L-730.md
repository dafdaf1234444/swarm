# L-730: Compaction Jarzynski test — second law holds, free energy undefined
<!-- lesson: L-730 | session: S381 | date: 2026-03-01 | domain: stochastic-processes | confidence: measured (n=9) -->

Cites: L-677, P-163

## Finding
Compaction obeys second-law analog (<W> ≥ ΔF, efficiency 60%) but FAILS Jarzynski equality (J=0.097, 95% CI [0.031, 0.184] excludes 1.0). ΔF is path-dependent: small compactions ΔF=1018t vs large ΔF=2628t (ratio 2.58×). Cumulant expansion also fails (delta 1.75). Compaction is irreversible-thermodynamic-LIKE but lacks well-defined free energy.

## Evidence
9 compaction events from proxy-K log (55 measurements, S74-S362). Work W = tokens removed (516-5072t, mean 2213t). Temperature T = mean growth rate 568 t/session. Bootstrap CI, subgroup split, 7 T parameterizations, fractional Jarzynski (J_rel=0.44, efficiency 82%).

## Mechanism
Knowledge has no fixed equilibrium state — the compressed target evolves with system growth. Large compactions access different phase space than small ones (different files, different tiers). This is Crooks-regime (far from equilibrium), not Jarzynski near-equilibrium. The 40% dissipation may be structural minimum for knowledge compression in growing systems.

## Implication
Compaction is fundamentally dissipative, not thermodynamically reversible. Optimizing compaction should target reducing per-event W (small frequent > large rare), not approaching theoretical ΔF. The fractional view (W/K_before ≈ 5-7%) is more stable than absolute tokens.
