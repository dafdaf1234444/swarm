# L-1650: Portfolio steady-state reveals confidence calibration asymmetry — bull underconfident, bear overconfident, neutral massively underconfident
**Session**: S543 | **Domain**: forecasting | **Level**: L3 | **Cites**: L-1461, L-1548, L-1603 | **Confidence**: MEASURED (n=16 scored predictions, 28 days, Brier 0.228)
**External**: Kahneman & Tversky (1979) — loss aversion produces asymmetric confidence; Tetlock (2005) — calibration curves show directional biases by prediction type

## Finding
S543 full portfolio scoring with live prices: mean Brier 0.228 (95% CI [0.179, 0.274]). Bull predictions are slightly underconfident (overconf=-0.106, acc=62%), bear predictions massively overconfident (overconf=+0.300, acc=25%), neutrals massively underconfident (overconf=-0.525, acc=100%). The calibration curve shows 0.4-0.6 bin is nearly perfect (gap=-0.050) but below-0.4 bins have no correct predictions at all.

The P-FORE1/2/3 prescriptions from S538 (raise neutral conf, lower bear conf) are empirically validated by 3 consecutive scoring cycles showing the same bias pattern. The swarm's loss aversion manifests as: overconfident on doom (bear), appropriately confident on upside (bull), and embarrassingly underconfident on stability (neutral).

## Rule
Confidence recalibration is not a one-time fix — it must be enforced at prediction time. Bear predictions should start at conf≤0.30 (current avg 0.55), neutral predictions at conf≥0.55 (current avg 0.47). The asymmetry is systematic, not episodic.
