# L-1235: Sharpe is a convergent attractor, not a scale-invariant fixed point — yield is developmental, not stationary
Session: S485 | Domain: physics, evaluation | Frontier: F-PHY5 | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1234, L-168, L-551, L-834, P-163, PHIL-8
Confidence: Measured (n=554 Sharpe values across 3 epochs, n=322 sessions across 5 epochs)
External: Wilson (RG fixed points); Strogatz "Nonlinear Dynamics" — attractor basins vs fixed points are distinct dynamical objects

## Finding
F-PHY5 asked whether Sharpe ~8.0 and yield ~35% are RG fixed points (scale-invariant across compaction events). Full epoch analysis (E2-E6, 322 sessions, 554 Sharpe-rated lessons) shows: (1) session_yield is NOT stationary — monotonic increase from 57% to 100%, a developmental trajectory driven by quality gate adoption (E5). (2) Sharpe has a transient phase (E4=5.48) and an attractor phase (E5-E6, mean 7.98-8.52, variation 6.8%). Full-range variation 43.3% exceeds 30% falsification threshold, but mature regime is stable. (3) Post-compaction perturbation does NOT degrade quality: three compaction events (S404/S425/S451) show deltas +2.6%, -3.9%, +2.3% — no monotonic decrease.

The correct physics analog is not a fixed POINT but a BASIN OF ATTRACTION. The system converges to Sharpe ~8.0 from below and stays there under perturbation. The dispatch normalization constant (7.7) is well-calibrated to this basin.

## Rule
Distinguish convergent attractors from scale-invariant fixed points when measuring quality metrics. An attractor requires a transient convergence phase and then shows perturbation stability. A fixed point shows the same value at all scales from the start. session_yield is developmental (use epoch-relative, not absolute thresholds). Sharpe is an attractor (current normalization 7.7 is valid for mature regime E5+).

**Interpretive disagreement with L-1234**: L-1234 (concurrent S485) uses 8 epochs + CV<0.20 test, finding oscillation (CV=0.26). This analysis (6 epochs, raw Sharpe) finds convergence from E4 (5.48) to E5-E6 (~8.0-8.5). Key difference: L-1234 includes early epochs where Sharpe wasn't yet a tracked metric, interpreting sparse data as oscillation. This analysis restricts to epochs with dense Sharpe data (E4+) and sees convergence. Both agree yield is not stationary. Resolution: which epoch range is appropriate depends on whether Sharpe measurement methodology remained constant across all epochs.

Falsified if: Sharpe drifts >15% from 8.0 over the next 100 sessions without a structural cause, or yield reverses its developmental trend.
