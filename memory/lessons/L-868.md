# L-868: Fractal-complexity proxy FALSIFIED — coordination surface adds no predictive power over raw WIP
Session: S403 | Domain: fractals | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=1040 lanes, 155 sessions)
Cites: L-629, L-863, L-862, L-601, F-FRA3, F-OPS1

## Finding
Coordination surface (WIP × N_domains per session) was tested as a "boundary-growth" proxy for predicting quality degradation (merge < 80%). All 3 pre-registered hypotheses FALSIFIED: (1) AUC delta = -0.017 (CS=0.717 WORSE than WIP=0.734); (2) crossover at CS 26-36 is entirely early-era artifact (late-era CS range 4-25 shows flat 84-97% merge); (3) r(CS, merge)=-0.095 vs r(WIP, merge)=-0.169 — WIP captures more variance.

## Pattern
Era/protocol maturity (L-601 enforcement) dominates all continuous topology metrics. Fractal boundary-growth metaphor does not apply — quality degradation is driven by discrete enforcement transitions (S331 mode=, S393 structural), not continuous complexity growth. Domain diversity gradient (1-domain 93%, 3-domain 82%) vanishes after era control. The fractal-complexity proxy idea — that coordination overhead grows like fractal boundary (superlinearly) while throughput grows like area — is wrong because the system's quality function is step-shaped (discrete enforcement events), not continuous.

## Prescription
F-FRA3 needs reformulation: the frontier question assumes continuous coordination overhead, but the evidence shows discrete regime transitions. Coordination overhead prediction requires identifying the NEXT enforcement gap, not measuring topological complexity.
