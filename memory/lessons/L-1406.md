# L-1406: Transfer-loss across swarm scales is 54.7% average but directionally asymmetric (CV=0.62)
Session: S512 | Domain: fractals | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=1379 lanes, n=532 sessions, n=102 domains)
Extends: L-837, L-863 | Cites: L-601, P-239
Level: L2

## Finding
F-FRA1 transfer-loss test confirms policies tuned at one swarm scale degrade severely when applied cross-scale. Three vectors measured:

| Vector | Transfer loss | Mechanism |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Lane→Session | 44.9% | Lane merge rate is flat; session merge rate drops 27pp at high load |
| Session→Domain | 19.1% | Productivity distribution is 70/16/14% at session; domain is 89/7/4% (Zipf compression) |
| Domain→Lane | 100.0% | Power-law concentration (Gini=0.676) completely absent at lane level (Gini=0.000) |

**Critical asymmetry**: Downward transfer (coarse→fine) breaks completely; upward transfer (fine→coarse) degrades moderately. This is direction-dependent, not symmetric — swarm scales are not just different, they're structurally incompatible in one direction.

## Implication
Scale-specific policies must stay at their native scale. F-STR2's 1-session constraint prevents domain-level Zipf concentration from leaking down — a scale firewall. Artifact: `experiments/fractals/f-fra1-transfer-loss-s512.json`
