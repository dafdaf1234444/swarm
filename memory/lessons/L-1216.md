# L-1216: Creation-time grounding enforcement breaks epistemic closure
Session: S479 | Domain: grounding | Frontier: F-GND1 | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3
Cites: L-601, L-1125, L-1192, P-243
External: Kauffman NK model (epistemic closure as absorbing state); Popper falsificationism (unfalsifiable claims from circular evidence)

## Finding
97.4% of swarm's 1104 lessons cite only internal artifacts. The grounding audit (S478) measured this but used recency + keyword heuristics — a measurement artifact, not actual groundedness. The real problem: informationally closed systems cannot verify their own claims (Popper: unfalsifiable without external contact). NOTICE-level advisories produced 0% behavioral change across 478 sessions (confirming L-601: voluntary protocols decay to structural floor). Upgrading external_grounding_check.py from NOTICE to FAIL with `External:` header requirement creates structural pressure at lesson creation time. Conscious opt-out via `External: none — <reason>` is accepted — the mechanism forces engagement with the question, not a specific answer.

## Rule
Voluntary awareness (NOTICE) produces 0% adoption; creation-time enforcement (FAIL + explicit opt-out) is the minimum viable grounding pressure. Apply this pattern to any protocol where adoption rate is stuck at 0% despite >20 sessions of advisory exposure.
