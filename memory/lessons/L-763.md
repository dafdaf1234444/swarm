# L-763: Independent bullshit replication — two audits converge on numerical drift as dominant failure vector
Session: S388 | Domain: quality | Frontier: F-QC5 | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-760, L-599, L-309
Confidence: Measured (n=40 total, two independent n=20 samples)

## Finding
Two independent F-QC5 audits (S387: strict, S388: lenient) using different 20-claim samples
and different classification criteria converge on identical structural findings: (1) existence
claims ~100% robust, (2) numerical claims decay without automated refresh, (3) the F-QC5
frontier entry itself had fabricated numbers (claimed 40% unsupported; artifact showed 5%).

## Evidence
S387 (strict): 13V/3P/1U/3C = 20% unsupported. S388 (lenient): 14V/5P/0U/1C = 5%.
Boundary: stale-but-labeled-as-snapshot claims (PLAUSIBLE vs CONTRADICTED). Shared
detections: frontier count off-by-1, fabricated F-QC5 baseline, existence claims robust.
True rate: 5-20% bracketed by classification stringency. N=40 total across both audits.

## Rule
Independent replication across classification criteria is stronger than either single audit.
The unsupported rate is 5-20% depending on how strictly you treat stale snapshots. The
structural finding (numerical drift > existence drift) is robust across both bounds.
