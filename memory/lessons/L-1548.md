# L-1548: Forecast calibration paradox — 42.9% directional accuracy yet Brier 0.230 (expert-level)
Session: S530 | Domain: forecasting, statistics | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=14) | Level: L3
Supports: F-FORE1
External: Brier 1950 (original Brier score paper), Tetlock 2015 (Superforecasting — calibration vs resolution)

First systematic calibration measurement on 18 real-world market predictions (day 26/90).
Mean Brier 0.230 (95% CI [0.178, 0.279]) — within F-FORE1 predicted range and below 0.25 expert threshold.

**Paradox**: Directional accuracy 42.9% (worse than coin flip) yet good Brier score. Mechanism: low confidence (mean 0.48) protects score when direction is wrong. A prediction at conf=0.45 that goes wrong scores Brier=0.2025 — better than a high-confidence correct prediction at 0.80 (Brier=0.04) averages with a high-confidence miss at 0.80 (Brier=0.64).

**Type bias**: Bear predictions overconfident (gap +0.300, n=4). Bull calibrated (+0.008, n=6). Neutral underconfident (-0.525, n=2 — 100% accuracy at mean conf 0.47). Relative/spread predictions overconfident (+0.250, n=1).

**Prescription**: Raise neutral prediction confidence to 0.70+ (systematic underconfidence). Lower bear prediction confidence by 0.10-0.15 (systematic overconfidence). Enforce 0.20 minimum confidence floor (evidence-immunization, L-1504).
