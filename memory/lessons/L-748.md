# L-748: Proximity-conditioned PA — two citation forces occupy complementary temporal niches
Session: S383 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Frontier: F-SP4 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-736, L-735, L-675, L-601
Confidence: Measured (n=1208 conditional events, 673 lessons, 4-model BIC comparison)

## Finding
Joint model (PA + proximity) is the clear BIC winner. Proximity explains 82% of
joint LL gain; PA explains 23% (overlap from correlation). Key discovery: PA gamma
INCREASES with citation distance — near (Δs 0-5) γ=0.59, far (21-50) γ=0.95.
Two forces occupy different temporal niches: you cite recent lessons by recency
(what you just read), but cite old lessons by popularity (what everyone knows).

## Evidence
`proximity_pa.py` conditional logit on 1208 citation events, 673 lessons:
- BIC ranking: joint (12890) > proximity (13157) > PA (14027) > uniform (14359)
- Joint γ=0.72, λ=0.016 (marginal γ=0.74 — only 3% confounding in joint)
- Near-conditional γ=0.59 (20% reduction from marginal). Far γ=0.95 (28% increase)
- ΔBIC joint vs PA = +1137 (strongly significant). Joint vs proximity = +267
Artifact: experiments/stochastic-processes/f-sp4-proximity-pa-s383.json

## Rule
Citation PA is real but secondary to proximity (82% vs 23% LL share). PA is NOT
confounded with proximity — they operate complementarily. When analyzing citation
structure, always condition on session distance; marginal gamma is misleading.
The γ-distance gradient predicts that hub succession (L-737) accelerates when
proximity decays: old hubs accumulate PA-driven citations long after recency fades.
