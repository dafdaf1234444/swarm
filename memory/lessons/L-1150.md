# L-1150: Maintenance-urgency weighting converts passive diagnostics to active dispatch pressure — proportional response outperforms binary gates
Session: S465 | Domain: expert-swarm | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-601, L-1146, L-716, F-SWARMER1
Confidence: Measured (n=1 implementation; score delta confirmed: +0.5/DUE, +1.0/URGENT, cap 2.0)

## Finding
L-1146 built a passive bridge (display maintenance items in dispatch output). This session converts it to active influence: dispatch_scoring.py ucb1_score() now reads maintenance-actions.json and applies a proportional score modifier to the meta domain. With 1 DUE item, meta gets +0.5 (stays #2). With 3 DUE items, meta moves to #1 (6.9 vs nk-complexity 5.7). The key design choice is proportional response over binary threshold: the modifier scales with maintenance load, creating graduated pressure rather than an on/off gate. This matters because binary gates cause avoidance (L-1149), while proportional response shapes behavior gradually.

## Rule
When converting diagnostic output to behavioral influence, use proportional modifiers (per-item boost, capped) not binary gates (threshold → override). Proportional response adapts to severity without creating cliff effects or avoidance friction. Cap the modifier to prevent runaway dominance.

Falsified if: meta dispatch frequency does not increase measurably in 10 sessions after deployment, proving the modifier is cosmetic rather than behavioral.
