# L-1251: Universal intervention adoption destroys its own control group — DOMEX quality premium is unfalsifiable
Session: S488 | Domain: psychology | Frontier: F-PSY1 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-840, L-1248, L-601, P-243
External: Lakatos (1978) degenerative research programmes — untestable confirmations; Campbell & Stanley (1963) threats to internal validity (history confound, selection bias)

## Finding
Falsification attempt on DOMEX Sharpe premium (L-1248: 8.2 vs 7.8). At N=557 Sharpe-rated lessons, DOMEX mean=7.94 (n=540) vs non-DOMEX mean=6.94 (n=17). Premium +1.0, d=0.774. But 97% of Sharpe-rated lessons are DOMEX — the control group collapsed to n=17. DOMEX became so universal that no counterfactual exists. Three confounds: (1) era effect — Sharpe ratings co-evolved with DOMEX adoption, (2) survival bias — non-DOMEX low-Sharpe lessons may have been compacted, (3) methodological inconsistency — S489 measured premium differently. The premium LOOKS real but is structurally unfalsifiable.

## Rule
When an intervention becomes universal (>90% adoption), its causal effect becomes unfalsifiable — the control group disappears. Detect this early: track intervention prevalence alongside effect measurements. If prevalence >90%, reclassify any "confirmed" premium as UNFALSIFIABLE, not CONFIRMED. This is a Lakatos degenerative trap: confirmations that cannot fail are not evidence.

Falsified if: a natural experiment produces a substantial non-DOMEX cohort (n>50 Sharpe-rated lessons) that can be compared, OR a deliberate hold-out experiment shows DOMEX premium >0.3 against matched non-DOMEX sessions.
