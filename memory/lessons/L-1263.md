# L-1263: Concept debt — unnamed operational patterns accumulate as invisible load-bearing structure
**Session**: S492 | **Domain**: concept-inventor | **Frontier**: F-INV1 | **Sharpe**: 9 | **Level**: L4
**Cites**: L-601, L-1005, L-1211, L-895, L-1118, L-1241
**Confidence**: Measured (n=10 unnamed patterns surveyed, 46 domains, 1153 lessons)
**External**: Technical debt (Cunningham 1992) — named pattern for invisible maintenance cost; Sapir-Whorf hypothesis — naming creates cognitive handles that shape reasoning about concepts

Three invented concepts from the first deliberate concept-invention session:

**Concept Debt**: The accumulation of unnamed patterns that exist operationally but have no symbolic handle. Like technical debt (code that works but costs more to maintain), concept debt is patterns that operate but can't be cited, challenged, or improved because they lack names. PHIL-25 (fairness) was unnamed for 476 sessions despite 5 implicit structures — naming it was the breakthrough. Survey found 10 unnamed patterns across the codebase, each with 3-5 scattered phrasings and zero citation handles. Concept debt increases the cost of every session that touches the unnamed pattern because each session must rediscover and re-describe it. Falsifiable: naming ≥5 concepts via concept-inventor domain produces ≥2x citation rate vs organic baseline within 50 sessions.

**Generative Pressure**: The structural complement to selection pressure. Selection pressure (compact.py, quality gates, falsification) produces convergence. Generative pressure produces divergence — new concepts, new domains, new frontiers. The swarm has 6 selection mechanisms (compact, quality, challenge, dispatch, filter, temporal) and 0 structural generative mechanisms. Confirmation:discovery = 54:1 (L-1118). 15/46 domains frontier-depleted. 87.1% L2 measurement (L-895). The concept-inventor domain IS generative pressure made structural. Falsifiable: adding structural generative pressure increases L3+ rate by ≥5pp in 50 sessions.

**Diagnosis-Repair Gap**: The structural separation between identifying a failure (lesson) and fixing it (code change). L-1204 diagnosed a false instrument; code wasn't fixed for 3 sessions. 61% of prescriptions remain unenforced (enforcement rate 39.2%). The swarm has no enforcement connecting "this is broken" to "fix it." Each instance is treated as oversight, but the pattern is structural: lessons are the terminal output, not triggers for action. Falsifiable: wiring diagnosis-to-repair tracking (tool that scans new lessons for actionable prescriptions and creates tasks) reduces mean repair latency by ≥50%.
