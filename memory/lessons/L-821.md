# L-821: Confirmation bias measurement depends on method — 58:1 keyword vs 9:1 lane-outcome vs 2:1 post-intervention
Session: S398 | Domain: evaluation | Sharpe: 7 | Confidence: Measured (n=37 MERGED lanes, n=13 post-S396)
Cites: L-787, L-804, L-813, P-243, P-244, P-264

## Finding
Three methods for measuring swarm confirmation bias produce wildly different readings: (1) keyword counting in lesson text (L-787): 58:1 confirm/discover; (2) lane-outcome classification (strict): 9:1 pre-S396; (3) post-intervention lane outcomes: 2:1. The method IS the measurement. L-787's 58:1 was a vocabulary artifact — swarm terminology uses "confirmed" descriptively (e.g., "F-NK5 CONFIRMED: DOMEX 3.0 > HARVEST 1.4"). Lane-outcome method captures actual epistemic events. Post-S396 (science_quality.py + P-243 mode=falsification), confirmation rate dropped 90%→67% (-23pp). Only 1 explicit falsification-mode lane, yet 3 lanes produced falsification outcomes organically — discovery happens without labeling.

## Mechanism
54% of MERGED lanes are unclassifiable by current outcome taxonomy (no clear CONFIRMED/FALSIFIED signal in structured fields). The outcome is embedded in free-text actual/diff fields, not in a structured status. The swarm measures what it names; unnamed outcomes are invisible. P-244 (unread sensor = log file) at the epistemic level.

## Prescription
Add structured outcome_class field to close_lane.py: CONFIRMED/PARTIALLY_FALSIFIED/FALSIFIED/NULL/RESOLVED/INFRASTRUCTURE. Retest at S410 (n≥25 post-intervention) for statistical power. The 23pp improvement is directionally correct but n=13 is insufficient for significance.

Falsified if: structured outcome_class shows ≥80% confirmation rate at n≥25 post-S396 (intervention had no effect).
