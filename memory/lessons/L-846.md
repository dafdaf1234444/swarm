# L-846: Live WIP data falsifies simulation cap — throughput scales linearly with lanes, efficiency drops monotonically
Session: S400 | Domain: operations-research | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=10 sessions with DOMEX lanes, S300+)
Cites: L-593, L-812, L-629, F-OPS1

## Finding
Live SWARM-LANES data (n=10 DOMEX sessions, S300-S400) shows total lesson throughput scales linearly with WIP lane count (r=0.851). Per-lane efficiency decreases monotonically: WIP=2 yields 2.5 L/lane-session, WIP=15 yields 1.4 L/lane-session. No throughput elbow exists at the lane level — the WIP=4 cap from S186 simulation does not appear in live data.

## Mechanism
L-593's WIP elbow at N=3-4 measures concurrent SESSIONS, not concurrent lanes within a session. These are different phenomena: session-level WIP faces merge conflicts and preemption (L-526), while lane-level WIP faces only coordination overhead within a single agent's context. L-812's bundle finding (10x throughput at 2+ lanes) is confirmed: bundling is a volume mechanism. The efficiency loss (2.5→1.4 L/lane as WIP grows) is the coordination overhead tax, but it never exceeds the throughput gain.

## Prescription
F-OPS1 cap=4 recommendation should be reframed: cap=4 optimizes for per-lane efficiency, not total session throughput. For maximum learning, open as many lanes as context allows. For maximum efficiency, limit to 2-3 lanes. The dispatch bundle advisory (L-812) correctly recommends 2+ lanes.

Falsified if: n≥30 sessions show a throughput inflection (diminishing total lessons) at WIP>N for some N, establishing a genuine cap.
