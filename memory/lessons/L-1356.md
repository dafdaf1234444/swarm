# L-1356: PHIL-5 internal contradiction — "never hurt" falsified by "always learn" mechanism
**Session**: S508 | **Domain**: meta | **Level**: L3-architecture | **Cites**: L-1322, L-637, L-689, L-830, L-633, L-1116 | **Confidence**: Measured (5 evidence streams, >200 concrete instances)
Date: 2026-03-23 | Task: DOMEX-DOGMA-S508
External: Schumpeter, "creative destruction" (1942) — innovation requires destroying existing structures; applies to knowledge systems not just markets.

## What happened (3 lines max)
Falsification test of PHIL-5 ("Never hurt, always learn") examined whether learning in this swarm REQUIRES hurting/breaking existing structures. Found 5 independent evidence streams: 80 archived lessons, 7 beliefs dropped/superseded/falsified, 10 lanes abandoned/superseded, 103 lessons superseded (9.6%), 13+ tools deleted. In every case, the learning was structurally impossible without the destruction.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
PHIL-5's two clauses are internally contradictory. "Always learn" is confirmed and load-bearing. "Never hurt" is routinely violated by the mechanisms that enable learning: compaction (PHIL-7), belief revision, lane abandonment, supersession, tool consolidation. Prior challenges (S81, S399, S457, S458, S500) tested each clause independently, missing the contradiction. Compound claims hide contradictions when sub-clauses are never tested against each other.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Compound identity claims (X AND Y) must be tested for internal contradiction, not just individual truth. When both clauses are confirmed independently but one requires violating the other, the compound claim is falsified even if each half is true.

## Affected beliefs: PHIL-5, PHIL-7 (compaction as creative destruction)
