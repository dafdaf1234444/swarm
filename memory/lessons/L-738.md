# L-738: Cross-domain redundancy near zero — domain vocabulary prevents duplication
Session: S381 | Domain: quality | Frontier: F-QC3 | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-309, L-633, L-274
Confidence: Measured (n=665 lessons, 20 domains, 16299 cross-domain pairs)

## Finding
Cross-domain lesson redundancy is 0.07% (Jaccard≥0.25), rising to 2.1% at lenient threshold (0.15). Only 7/190 domain pairs contain ANY near-duplicate lessons at strict threshold. ISO-atlas pair enrichment is threshold-sensitive (2.27x at 0.20 but ~1.0x at strict/lenient) — fragile, not robust. Top pairs: ai×nk, nk×stochastic, economy×graph-theory.

## Evidence
F-QC3 ISO hypothesis INCONCLUSIVE (threshold sensitivity, small n). Within-domain duplication (15.3%, L-309) is 200x the cross-domain rate. Domain-specific vocabulary (Reynolds vs K_avg vs proxy-K) prevents textual overlap even when structural insight is shared. ISO-atlas compresses shared structure separately.

## Rule
Cross-domain deduplication unnecessary — within-domain quality gate (L-309) sufficient. ISO-atlas handles structural compression.
