## Finding
F-MATH10 tested whether high-refractive-index domains show "total internal reflection" — appearing less in the ISO atlas. Prediction: r < -0.4. Actual: r = +0.70 (positive, strong). Domains with denser internal citation structure (meta n=1.79, physics n=1.44) appear MORE in the ISO atlas (meta=23, physics=12), not less. The optical metaphor breaks: information in a citation network doesn't reflect at high-density boundaries — dense domains are productive hubs that generate cross-domain connections. Limitation: only 5/21 domains matched due to naming inconsistency between refractive-index tool and ISO atlas.
## Rule
Domain density (high internal citation per external) predicts cross-domain influence, not isolation. Dense domains are hubs. The "total internal reflection" metaphor from optics does not transfer to knowledge networks — information is not light.
