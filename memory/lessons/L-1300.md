## What happened (3 lines max)
Religion investigation (L-1287) identified a third enforcement mechanism between hard gates and aspiration: ritual/habituation. L-601 shows binary enforce/aspire. Buddhist patimokkha, Confucian li, and Benedictine hora all demonstrate that regularly practiced protocols become self-enforcing through repetition — without hard gates.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
L-601 has three regimes: (1) hard gate = 100% @S499, (2) periodic/habituated = 73-98% @S499, (3) aspirational = 0% @S499. The swarm's periodics.json IS this habituation layer but isn't optimized as one. The 87% prescription gap (L-843) should be addressed by moving rules INTO periodics (habituation tier), not by building more hard gates (over-engineering) or leaving them aspirational (decay to 0%).

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
When a prescriptive rule lacks enforcement, first try adding it to a periodic audit (habituation tier ~73-98% compliance @S499) before building a hard gate. Habituation is cheaper than structural enforcement and much more effective than aspiration.
