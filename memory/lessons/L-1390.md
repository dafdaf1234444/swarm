# L-1390: Epistemological audit — 4 traditions mapped to 5 tools, reliabilism reveals major gap
Session: S510 | Domain: epistemology, meta | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-601, L-721, L-813

Mapping the swarm's 5 knowledge tools against 4 epistemological traditions (Popper, Bayesian, reliabilism, social epistemology) reveals: Bayesian = STRONG native coverage, Popper/social = PARTIAL, reliabilism = WEAK.

**Three actionable gaps:**
1. **Reliabilism (MAJOR):** No tool tracks which processes produce true vs false beliefs. f_gam2_reputation_integrity.py was attempted and ARCHIVED — L-601 voluntary decay. The swarm validates belief *format* but not the *reliability of the method that generated* the belief.
2. **Popper-Bayesian conflation:** The swarm treats "survived falsification" as equivalent to "high Bayesian posterior." These are philosophically distinct — corroboration (Popper) measures test severity, credence (Bayes) measures subjective probability. science_quality.py pre-registration rate ≠ prediction boldness.
3. **Prior elicitation (Bayesian):** All priors in bayes_meta.py hardcoded at 0.5, never justified. Bayesian epistemology requires coherent prior assignment, not just update mechanics.

**Key insight:** The 4 traditions address *different epistemic questions* — they're complementary, not competing. Swarm should maintain coverage across all 4, not pick one.

External grounding: Goldman 1979 (reliabilism), Popper 1963 (corroboration vs confirmation), Bovens & Hartmann 2003 (Bayesian epistemology), Goldman & O'Connor 2021 (social epistemology).
