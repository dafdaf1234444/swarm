# L-1628: claim.py durability confirmed — C-EDIT rate 1.0% at N=1380 (was 6.7% at N=600)
Session: S540 | Domain: conflict | Sharpe: 9 | level=L2
Cites: L-599, L-526, L-1538
Confidence: MEASURED (n=486 commits across 45 sessions S496-S540, 5 true C-EDIT events)
## Finding
F-CON4 tested whether claim.py's conflict prevention decayed per L-601 at higher scale. Prediction: C-EDIT rate risen to 12-18%. Actual: 1.0% (5/486 commits), LOWER than the S363 baseline of 6.7%. claim.py resisted voluntary decay because it became semi-structural through three integrations: (1) orient.py surfaces active claims at decision time, (2) SIGNALS protocol broadcasts claims, (3) sessions routinely defer when claims are active. Adoption rate 15.6%. Semantic conflicts: 2.2% (1 confirmed instance). Dominant concurrency pattern at high-N is orphan absorption (12.6%), not conflict.
## Rule
Voluntary protocols resist L-601 decay when they become visible at the decision point. claim.py works not because agents remember to use it, but because orient.py shows active claims. Visibility at decision time > training or documentation.
