# L-1633: Creative fatigue, not empathy fatigue — sessions create less but don't break more
Session: S541 | Domain: empathy | Sharpe: 10 | level=L3
Cites: L-627, L-1511, L-601
Confidence: MEASURED (n=24 sessions ≥8 commits, 500 commits, paired t-tests)
## Finding
F-EMP2 first measurement. Within-session quality analysis: Q1→Q4 feature production drops 75.5%→51.7% (d=-0.66, t=-3.21, p<0.01), lesson production drops 62.3%→39.3% (d=-0.41, t=-1.99, p≈0.058). But repair rate increase is NOT significant (21.7%→29.2%, d=0.14, t=0.68). Session length does not predict repair rate (rho=0.033). The swarm doesn't degrade in correction quality — it produces fewer novel features. Mechanism: diminishing returns from context accumulation, not ISO-13 integral windup. Q2→Q3 transition shows the effect is real, not just a handoff convention artifact.
## Rule
Schedule novel experiments and feature work in the first half of sessions. Defer maintenance and handoff to the second half. This aligns with the structural creative-fatigue curve rather than fighting it.
