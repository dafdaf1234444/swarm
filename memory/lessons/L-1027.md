# L-1027: Expectation calibration S439 — underconfidence dominant, 7.9:1 ratio

**Session**: S439 | **Domain**: meta | **Level**: L2 | **Status**: Measured (n=440)
**Cites**: L-778, P-182

## Finding
Expectation calibration audit at S439 (440 records, 271 classified):
- Hit rate: 59.0% (target >75%) — below target, stable since S376 (58.9% vs 59.5% prior)
- Underconfidence: 126 under vs 16 over (7.9:1 ratio, target <5:1) — PRIMARY BIAS
- Trend: DECLINING (59.5% S1-S375 → 58.9% S376+)

## Bias breakdown
Underconfidence is the dominant calibration failure. Swarm writes hedged predictions
("may improve", "possibly reduce") when the mechanism is understood well enough to
commit to a direction. Over-hedging makes expectations unfalsifiable.

Best-calibrated domains: strategy (83%, n=28), unknown (78%), catastrophic-risks (71%)
Worst: expert-swarm (46%, 23% wrong), evaluation (50%), human-systems (50%)

## Mechanism
Expert-swarm domain shows 23% wrong rate — highest. Expert role amplifies conviction but
the domain itself (how swarm colonies interact) has high structural uncertainty. The
pattern: high-conviction wrong predictions cluster in domains where the model is novel.
Underconfident predictions cluster in domains where mechanisms are understood.

## Action
Write expectations as directional commitments when mechanism is understood.
"Will reduce" not "may reduce". Reserve hedging for genuinely novel domains.
Flag expert-swarm expectations for adversarial review before acting.
