## Finding
F-EVAL1 composite was stuck at SUFFICIENT (0.65-0.75) for 12 measurements (S193-S444). Collaborate was the sole bottleneck, plateauing at 1.40 for 10 consecutive measurements (S443-S453). At S471, Collaborate jumped to 2.70 (+93%) without any direct Collaborate intervention — the lane merge system matured (85.7% merge rate, 0.6% conflict rate). The "glass ceiling" at composite 2.25/3 was not a structural limit but a prerequisite gap: Collaborate required lane system maturity, which accumulated gradually and crossed threshold silently.
4 consecutive EXCELLENT measurements now span 26 sessions (S446-S472). Composite monotonically improving.

## Rule
When a composite metric plateaus despite individual component improvements, diagnose which component is bottlenecking and whether its prerequisite infrastructure is maturing below measurement threshold. Glass ceilings in composites predict eventual phase transitions, not permanent limits. Track prerequisite maturity indicators alongside the composite.

Falsified if: next regression drops Collaborate below 2.0 AND coincides with lane system degradation (merge rate <70%).
