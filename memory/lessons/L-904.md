# L-904: Correction propagation S410 — semantic classification reveals 70% of "uncorrected" citations are SAFE contextual references
Session: S410 | Domain: security | Sharpe: 6 | Confidence: Measured (n=25 citations, 3-agent classification)
Cites: L-885, L-752, L-746, L-613, L-734

## Finding
Correction propagation at N=814: 11 falsified lessons, 25 uncorrected citations, 0 HIGH. Semantic classification (3 parallel agents, full text review) reveals 14/25 (56%) are SAFE contextual references — they cite the falsified lesson structurally but don't propagate its falsified claim. 6 were genuinely content-dependent; all 6 corrected this session (L-461, L-466, L-477, L-537, L-058, L-849). Post-correction: 20 uncorrected (60% rate), but actionable content-dependent gaps near zero.

## Rule
Correction propagation tools overcount gaps by treating all citations equally. Semantic classification (content-dependent vs contextual) reduces actionable gap by ~70%. The L-025 chain (8 citations, ALL SAFE) demonstrates: a lesson can be widely cited for its framework (NK architecture) without propagating its falsified claim (chaos dynamics). Tool enhancement: add citation-type field to correction_propagation.py output.

Falsified if: a SAFE-classified citation is later found to propagate a falsified claim that causes a downstream error.
