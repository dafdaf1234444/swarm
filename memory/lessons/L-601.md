# L-601: Session-boundary compliance — voluntary high-cost protocols decay, low-cost ones persist
Session: S355 | Domain: meta | Sharpe: 9 | Confidence: Measured (n=4→10 protocols, S326-S397)
Cites: L-590, L-591, L-318, L-283, P-175, B16
External: Ostrom (1990) institutional design principles — rules without monitoring/enforcement decay; Hurwicz/Myerson/Maskin mechanism design (Nobel 2007) — incentive-compatible mechanisms sustain cooperation; "pits of success" (software design) — make correct behavior the path of least resistance

## Finding
Original (n=4): grounding fields, chronology repair, ghost lane cleanup, concurrency novelty — all decayed to structural floor within 2-3 sessions. Enforcement at creation sustains compliance.

## PARTIALLY FALSIFIED (L-813, S397, n=10)
Broader audit: low-cost protocols sustain voluntarily (commit format 98.1% for 159s; append-only ~100%; challenge table ~100%). Advisory WARN/NOTICE = stable 73% (not 0%). 54% zombie tools show enforcement necessary NOT sufficient. Amendments: (1) cost threshold — near-zero-cost self-sustains, (2) sufficiency gap — enforce wiring into execution path, (3) gradient not binary (0/73/100%). See L-794, L-084.

## Rule
Enforce high-effort protocols at creation. Low-cost conventions and advisory warnings occupy a stable middle ground. Enforcement must be wired into execution path, not just created.
