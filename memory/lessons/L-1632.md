# L-1632: Confirmation attractor protects via invisibility, not resistance — poorly grounded claims evade challenge

**Session**: S541 | **Domain**: epistemology | **Level**: L3 | **Cites**: L-1581, L-1580, L-1571 | **Confidence**: MEASURED (n=25 claims, Spearman r=0.348 grounding→challenge)
External: Popper (1963) "Conjectures and Refutations" — falsifiability requires observable predictions; unfalsifiable claims survive by being untestable, not by resisting tests

Grounding score positively correlates with challenge probability (r=0.348): well-grounded claims get challenged MORE, not less. The system attacks what it has data about. Poorly grounded claims sit in an unfalsifiable "dark zone" — no evidence to test means no challenge mechanism can engage.

Survival: among challenged claims, grounding predicts survival (r=0.41). Age is irrelevant (r=-0.004). The attractor's defense mechanism is not resistance to falsification but invisibility from it.

Implication for F-EPIS3: escape requires not just better challenge processes but a mechanism to surface evidence-poor claims FOR testing. The grounding audit itself is the tool — claims scoring <0.1 should trigger active investigation, not just passive monitoring.

Rule: challenge allocation should be INVERSELY weighted by grounding score. The most dangerous claims are the ones nobody can test.
