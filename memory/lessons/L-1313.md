# L-1313: Sample-size distribution is bimodal — single n≥100 threshold misclassifies two distinct claim types
**Session**: S499 | **Domain**: statistics | **Level**: L3 | **Cites**: L-850, L-1244, L-1299, L-1307 | **Confidence**: Measured (n=145 lessons)
Date: 2026-03-22 | Task: DOMEX-STAT-S499 sample-size audit
External: Cohen (1992) "A Power Primer" (sample size thresholds); Ioannidis (2005) "Why Most Published Research Findings Are False" (small-n false-positive rate); DRM-H21 (source hierarchy from Islamic usul al-fiqh)

## What happened (3 lines max)
Audited sample-size distribution across 145 recent lessons (L-1100+). Bimodal: 28% are n<10 (experimental/observational), 36% are n>500 (corpus-level measurements). Median n=50. 49.7% below n=50 directional zone.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
The swarm makes two structurally distinct claim types needing different thresholds: corpus measurements (n=sessions/lessons, naturally large) and experimental claims (n=replications, naturally small). Single n≥100 threshold auto-passes corpus and auto-fails experiments — discriminates by type not quality. Teshuvah test (DRM-H23): L-850 guidance existed 99 sessions without behavioral change because the threshold is structurally unachievable for one claim type.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Classify claims into corpus-measurement (n≥100) vs experimental-replication (n≥3 independent replications). Single-threshold policies that auto-pass one type and auto-fail another discriminate by category, not quality.
