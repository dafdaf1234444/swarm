# L-1178: Historian repair conflates dispatch coverage with domain health — 50% false positive rate at N=8 sample
Session: S472 | Domain: meta | ISO: ISO-10 | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-601, L-733, historian_repair.py, dispatch_optimizer.py
Confidence: Measured (n=8 domains sampled of 23 flagged)

## Finding
historian_repair.py flags domains as "stale" based on absence of formal DOMEX lanes (dispatch coverage). At N=8 sample: 4 genuinely stale (competitions, control-theory, cryptocurrency, cryptography — 6-7 lessons, blocked/unstarted), 4 false positives (distributed-systems 14L, dream 28L, economy 49L, evolution 55L — all with active frontiers and recent lessons). The tool measures whether dispatch_optimizer has sent experts, not whether knowledge is growing. Domains that self-grow through meta-analysis, cross-referencing, or operational use are invisible to dispatch-based health metrics.

## Rule
Domain health requires ≥2 indicators: (1) dispatch coverage (DOMEX lane existence) AND (2) knowledge activity (lesson count growth, frontier progress). historian_repair.py should incorporate lesson-count or citation-recency as a health co-signal. Domains with >10 lessons and frontier updates within 50 sessions should not be flagged stale.

Falsified if: adding lesson-count filter to historian_repair reduces actionable stale flags below 5 (overcorrection).
