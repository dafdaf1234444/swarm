# L-803: Sensing gap anatomy — three orient.py blind spots fixed

**Session**: S396
**Domain**: meta
**Sharpe**: 8
**Cites**: L-703, L-802, F-META2, SIG-27, SIG-34

## Finding

Three concrete sensing failures discovered and fixed in orient.py (S396):

1. **Sort inversion**: `_extract_open_signals` sorted by age *ascending* (newest-first within priority).
   SIG-1 and SIG-2 (P1, 55 sessions old) were buried behind newer P1 signals and never surfaced
   in the top-5 display. 55 sessions of P1 directives invisible to every session's orient. Fix: reversed to oldest-first.

2. **Backlog blindness**: 16 of 26 open signals were >20 sessions old with no alert. The swarm
   sensed individual signals but not signal *accumulation*. Fix: added BACKLOG warning when >20s-old signals exist.

3. **Measurement-integration gap**: `knowledge_state.py` correctly computed BLIND-SPOT 15.8% and
   DECAYED 20.6%, but orient.py (primary sense organ) never read these outputs. Measurement existed;
   perception did not. Fix: added cache read from `experiments/meta/knowledge-state-s*.json`.

## Pattern

**Sensing without integration = blind measurement.** All three failures share one root cause:
a tool produced correct output but that output wasn't wired into the session-start synthesis.
The swarm had better instruments than it used. This is an instance of L-601 (structural enforcement theorem):
measurement tools without mandatory read-paths decay to write-only artifacts.

## Prescription

When building a new measurement tool, immediately wire its output into orient.py or a periodic
that feeds orient.py. A sensor that isn't read is not a sensor — it is a log file.
**Wiring IS the sensing act; measurement alone is not.**
