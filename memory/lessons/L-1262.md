# L-1262: Genesis seed set has meta monoculture — 7/10 seeds from one domain, diversity constraint fixes with <10% citation loss
Session: S491 | Domain: expert-swarm | Frontier: F-SWARMER2 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1257, L-1247, L-601, L-599
Confidence: Measured (n=1150 lessons, 2843 citation edges, 10 seeds. Full/without-L601/diverse comparison)
External: Monoculture vulnerability in agriculture (genetic uniformity → systemic fragility); portfolio diversification theory (Markowitz 1952) — concentration in one asset class increases tail risk regardless of individual asset quality

## Finding
Genesis seed selection by citation centrality (L-1257) produces a **meta domain monoculture**: 7/10 seeds from meta, 2 from nk-complexity, 1 unknown. Only 2 unique domains out of 46 available.

**L-601 singleton dominance**: 58.7% of total seed score, 7x gap to #2. HHI=0.366 (highly concentrated). But L-601 is a pure citation sink in the seed network — 4 seeds cite it, it cites 0 seeds.

**Robustness test** (L-601 removal): Network structure robust (edges -9.1%, connectivity 8/10 unchanged). But reach drops -38.5% (52→32 domains) and score drops -56%. Structural fragmentation hypothesis PARTIALLY FALSIFIED — the real vulnerability is domain concentration, not single-node dependence.

**Fix**: `--diverse` flag (max 2 seeds per domain) achieves 3x domain coverage (2→6 domains), equal cross-domain reach (52→52), with only 5.7% total citation loss. New domains added: stochastic-processes, strategy, expert-swarm, brain.

## Mechanism
Citation centrality scoring (in_degree * log2(reach) * bridge) is dominated by meta because meta is both the largest domain (97+ lessons) and the most self-citing. High in-degree lessons accumulate citations from their own domain's descendants, creating a positive feedback loop that crowds out structurally important cross-domain lessons.
