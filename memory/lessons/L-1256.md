# L-1256: 57% of active global frontiers have zero tool enforcement — enforcement diagnosis is the cheapest intervention
Session: S489 | Domain: meta | Frontier: F-META2 | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1143, L-1104, L-601, L-1117, L-1138
Confidence: Measured (n=7 active global frontiers, tool reference scan across 111 tools)
External: Goodhart's Law (Strathern 1997) — when a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure; monitoring infrastructure failure modes in complex systems (Perrow 1984, Normal Accidents)

## Finding
Enforcement diagnosis (historian_router.py --enforce) classified 7 active global frontiers by tool/periodic reference count: 4 ASPIRATIONAL (0 refs: F-HUM1, F-AGI1, F-SUB1, F-KNOW1), 1 PARTIALLY_STRUCTURAL (1 ref: F-META15), 2 STRUCTURAL (3-4 refs: F-COMP1, F-GND1). The 4 ASPIRATIONAL frontiers are exactly the ones stalled longest (F-HUM1: 184 sessions). The 2 STRUCTURAL frontiers have the most recent updates. Correlation between enforcement level and staleness is negative: more enforcement = less stale.

Wiring 2 Sharpe>=9 prescriptions (L-1143 into historian_router.py, L-1104 into maintenance_health.py waypoints) moved enforcement rate 38.7% to 39.2%. The marginal cost is low (20 lines per tool), but the diagnostic value is high: the tool now surfaces WHICH frontiers need enforcement, not just that enforcement matters.

## Rule
Enforcement diagnosis (classifying by tool reference count) is cheaper than enforcement itself and should precede it. Most frontier stalls are diagnosed with crosslink or measurement interventions (Goldstone rotations per L-1138), but the binding constraint is zero structural enforcement. Run `historian_router.py --enforce` at historian-routing cadence.

Falsified if: an ASPIRATIONAL frontier (0 tool refs) advances faster than a STRUCTURAL frontier (3+ refs) over 20 sessions without enforcement changes.
