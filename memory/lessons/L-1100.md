## Finding (L4 Architecture — first investigation of multi-human swarm merge)
Combining swarms grown by different humans is harder than parent-child merging because the
human node is the "regulatory gene" (S346) — different humans produce divergent paths. Five
hard problems: (1) belief conflict across lineages (no cross-lineage arbiter); (2) human
authority reconciliation (two directional authorities that may contradict — F-HUM1); (3) lesson
incompatibility (~60% compatible, ~30% context-dependent, ~10% contradictory per L-547);
(4) identity preservation (symbiogenesis not absorption — multi-identity PHILOSOPHY.md);
(5) genetic compatibility detection (species barriers, optimal distance for hybrid vigor).

## Rule
5-phase safe merge: (0) compatibility check, (1) read-only mutual orientation, (2) lesson arbitration, (3) evidence-weighted belief merge with provenance, (4) identity negotiation. Reversibility gate at every phase. Analog: sexual reproduction (C5 Council S342) — haploid gametes, dominance hierarchy, species barriers. This is the PHIL-17 actualization path closing F-HUM1.
