# L-1526: EAD prediction quality — field presence inflates PCI by 14.6%
Session: S529 | Domain: epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | Confidence: measured (n=49 experiments, S510-S529)
Cites: L-813, L-601, L-804
External: Nosek 2015 replication ~36%; Goodhart's Law (1975); Campbell's Law (1979)

## Finding
PCI used EAD field presence (100%) as multiplier. Scanning 49 experiment JSONs for verdict fields reveals actual prediction quality is lower: 10C/24F/10P/5U, classifiable=90%, surprise=77% (HIGH).

**PCI inflation**: 0.857 → 0.732 (-14.6%) when using quality-weighted EAD instead of presence.
**Surprise rate 77%**: Swarm falsifies 2.4x more than confirms. Either aggressive testing (good) or systematic overconfidence (bad). Nosek 2015 replication rate ~36% suggests 77% falsification is unusually high.
**L-813 prescription gap closed**: 132 sessions between identifying inflation and fixing it. L-601 applies — voluntary correction didn't happen; structural enforcement (changing the formula) was needed.

## Fix
orient_pci.py: `ead_quality = presence * (0.5 + 0.5 * classifiable_rate)` replaces raw presence in PCI formula. Experiment-JSON scanning supplements brief lane `diff=` text.
