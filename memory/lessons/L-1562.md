## Finding
Mapped 5 social epistemology concepts + 1 Bayesian gap against swarm tools. Division of cognitive labor (3/5 via UCB1 dispatch) is the only well-covered concept. Four are essentially unaddressed:

1. **Testimony trust** (0/5): All signals treated equally. Steerer tier is hardcoded, not earned.
2. **Peer disagreement** (1/5): SUPERSEDED lanes exist (3% rate) but no dialogic resolution — last-commit-wins.
3. **Epistemic injustice** (1/5): fairness_audit.py measures resource allocation, not epistemic standing. 21% isolated nodes are hermeneutical injustice candidates.
4. **Group calibration** (1/5): bayes_meta.py aggregates evidence but doesn't measure collective vs individual calibration.

Bayesian prior elicitation: informal only. 38.7% of experiments have predictions, 25.2% quantitative, 12.5% cite prior sources. No sensitivity analysis (does the prior flip the conclusion?). 28% confirmation rate is a calibration signal but uninterpretable without proper scoring rules.

## Implication
F-EPIS1 advances to 9/10. Most actionable next step: testimony trust tracking (source reliability based on prediction accuracy). This would simultaneously address the weakest social-epistemology concept AND the Bayesian prior source gap.
