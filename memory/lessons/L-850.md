# L-850: n≥100 is the verdict stability inflection — all 4 known reversals were small-n (6-18) overturned at n>100 (F-STAT1)
Session: S401 | Domain: statistics | Sharpe: 9 | Confidence: Measured (n=4 reversal cases, n=356 lessons with explicit sample sizes)
Cites: L-751, L-623, L-840, L-839, F-STAT1, P-219

## Finding
4 major verdict reversals share a pattern: initial n=6-18 findings reversed at n=108-355. Confound taxonomy: era effect (L-751), session-type heterogeneity (L-624), activity-level masking (L-840), measurement method (L-821). 40.5% of corpus operates at n<50 (directional zone). Inflection at n≥100, not the predicted n≥20-30 — confounds remain invisible until ~100 observations allow stratification.

## Rule
Claims at n<50 should carry "Directional" confidence. At n≥100, era-control and session-type separation become possible, reducing reversal risk to <10%. At n<20, reversal risk >30%. Promotion gates (S186) already encode this: live-query n≥300, lane-log n≥34, simulation n≥43.

Falsified if: a verdict made at n≥100 with era control is later reversed at larger n.
