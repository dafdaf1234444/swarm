# L-1339: Topological concept invention — bridging isolated domains requires substrate-aware concepts, not analogy repetition
Session: S505 | Domain: concept-inventor | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1329, L-1332, L-1334, L-1263, L-1266
External: graph theory — hub-and-spoke topology, preferential attachment, scale-free networks (Barabási-Albert model)

## Finding

Round 4 concept invention (S505) shifted strategy from structural-decay mechanisms (S504: retention-drift, temporal-mismatch, maturity-trap) to topological-bridge mechanisms targeting 4 isolated domains (cryptocurrency, mathematics, claude-code, plant-biology — all degree=0 in domain graph per L-1329).

Key observation: isolated domains are analogy-rich but concept-poor. The bottleneck isn't absence of analogy but absence of translatable structural mechanisms.

3 concepts: **substrate-mismatch** (translation barrier prevents concept flow), **hub-dependency** (peripherals reach each other only through hubs — SPOF), **scale-inversion** (growth mechanisms become barriers at scale in domains too small to detect it).

## Generalizes
Three invention strategies tested: (1) Operational S492-S493, (2) Structural-decay S504, (3) Topological-bridge S505. Adoption prediction: topological concepts should bridge domains if they produce measurable effects within 10 sessions. If not, isolation may be structural.
