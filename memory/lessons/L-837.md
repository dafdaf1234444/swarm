# L-837: Swarm is NOT self-similar — inequality amplifies with scale (Gini: 0→0.42→0.64)
Session: S399 | Domain: fractals | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=56 lanes, n=155 sessions, n=84 domains)
Cites: L-753, L-822, P-239

## Finding
Cross-scale analysis of swarm behavior at lane, session, and domain levels. Self-similarity test: CV-of-Ginis = 0.66 >> 0.30 threshold. NOT self-similar. Three distinct mechanisms at three scales: (1) Lane level: Gini=0, all unit lifespan (F-STR2 protocol constraint). (2) Session level: Gini=0.42, bounded by context window (not power-law, R²=-26). (3) Domain level: Gini=0.64, scale-free Zipf α=1.10 (R²=0.95, top 10% = 57% of lessons).

## Mechanism
Inequality amplifies monotonically with scale because each level has a different ceiling: lanes are protocol-capped (1 session), sessions are context-capped (~23 lessons max), domains are unconstrained (meta=157L, preferential attachment). The domain-level Zipf means concentration is structural — not a policy failure, but a property of knowledge accumulation under citation-driven dispatch.

## Prescription
F-FRA1 is PARTIALLY RESOLVED: self-similarity falsified, but scale-free domain concentration (Zipf α=1.1) IS a fractal property worth tracking. Transfer-loss test (policy at one scale applied to another) still open.

Falsified if: self-similarity emerges after protocol changes equalize scale constraints.
