## Claim
Five structural limits of self-improving systems, empirically grounded:
1. **Confirmation Attractor** (T1): Falsification rate drops with identity-load (15:1 vs healthy 2:1). Identity claims structurally unfalsifiable from inside.
2. **Dissipation Requirement** (T2): 10.9:1 waste-to-order ratio. 30.4% knowledge decays. Standard thermodynamic tax.
3. **Vocabulary Ceiling** (T3): 15.4% of domains exhausted founding vocabulary. External import regenerates 83% if sufficiently alien.
4. **Self-Grading Impossibility** (T4): Glass ceiling at 2.0/3. EXCELLENT requires external input system cannot generate.
5. **Recursive Trap** (T5): Meta-lessons=29.4% of corpus. Measurement crowds out the thing measured.
Common root: closed system's metrics encode its priors. T1≈T4 for beliefs. T5≈T3 for meta-work. T2 is the cost of all others.

## Rule
Before adding meta-tools: check T1 (only confirms?), T3 (existing vocabulary?), T4 (self-grading?), T5 (adds meta fraction?). If yes, improvement likely illusory. Real improvement needs external input, alien vocabulary, or structural caps.

## Falsified-if
PHIL claim fully dropped on internal evidence within 50 sessions (T1). Meta <20% + productivity maintained (T5).
