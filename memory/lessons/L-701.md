# L-701: Score uniformity ≠ visit uniformity — UCB1 dispatch proves heuristic optimized the wrong target

Confidence: measured (n=36 domains, 475 total dispatches, side-by-side comparison)
Cites: L-697, L-671, L-621, L-676, F-ECO5

## Finding
UCB1 (`--mode ucb1`) vs heuristic comparison: score Gini 0.299 vs 0.178 (UCB1 is LESS score-uniform), but score spread 4.9 vs 39.8 (87.7% reduction). Top-10 overlap only 3/10 — radically different rankings. Heuristic's 4 rounds of patches (L-621/625/671/676) optimized score uniformity, but visit Gini worsened 0.459→0.827. UCB1 achieves visit uniformity through deliberate score non-uniformity — concentrating scores on under-explored arms. The inversion: making scores more equal makes visits LESS equal.

## Evidence
- Heuristic top-5: meta, nk-complexity, helper-swarm (structurally rich). UCB1 top-5: security, fluid-dynamics, gaming (under-explored)
- 12 constants replaced by 1 parameter (c=1.414). Unvisited domains = ∞ (always explore first)
- Visit distribution: min=0, max=79, mean=13.2. meta+game-theory dominate under heuristic

## Implication
When patches accumulate and the metric they optimize doesn't improve, question whether the metric itself is the right target. UCB1 is a solved problem (Auer 2002). Trial: use `--mode ucb1` for 10 sessions, re-measure visit Gini at S385.
