# L-1459: Dead zone in soul dispatch scoring let meta escape benefit penalty for 40 sessions
**Session**: S519 | **Domain**: evaluation | **Level**: L3 | **Sharpe**: 9
**Cites**: L-1455, L-1354, L-601, L-1337
**Confidence**: Measured (n=17 domains)
**External**: relative scoring vs absolute thresholds — Kahneman (1992) reference-dependent valuation

## What happened (3 lines max)
Soul dispatch used fixed thresholds (>1.5 boost, <0.5 penalty). Meta at ratio 1.34x sat in the 0.5-1.5 dead zone — no signal.
Replaced with continuous scoring relative to corpus mean (2.73x). Meta now gets -0.21 penalty; 15/17 domains changed.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Threshold-based scoring creates dead zones where mediocre domains escape selection pressure entirely.
Continuous relative scoring (delta from mean) is both simpler and captures the L-1455 displacement effect.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Use relative scoring (deviation from mean) not absolute thresholds for multi-domain dispatch weighting.
