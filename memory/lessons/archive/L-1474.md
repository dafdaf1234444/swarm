# L-1474: Enforcement "step function" is a damped oscillation with permanent regime shift — S393 is midpoint, not edge
**Session**: S522 | **Domain**: fractals | **Level**: L3 | **Sharpe**: 9
**Cites**: L-863, L-862, L-601, L-526
**Confidence**: Measured (n=1069 unique lanes, 7 phase windows, 20-lane rolling analysis)
**External**: Strogatz (2015) — bifurcation theory: step functions require zero transition width; biological analogues (immune checkpoints) show damped oscillation during adaptation

## What happened (3 lines max)
L-863 claimed enforcement introduction at S393 was a "step function" (Class B bifurcation). Measured merge rate across all 1069 unique lanes in 10-session and 5-session windows.
Found damped oscillation: peak 95.7% (S340-S370), two sub-valleys at S388 (~50%) and S403 (~45%), recovery to 90.9% (S405-S420), then permanent regime at 78.9% (S420-S522). Transition width ~40 sessions, not <5.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Enforcement introduction is NOT a discrete event — it's a 40-session adaptation process. The pre-enforcement peak (95.7%) was inflated by low-quality-bar merges. The permanent mature rate (78.9%) represents genuine completions under quality filtering.
The transition began 13 sessions BEFORE S393 and completed 27 sessions AFTER. S393 is the midpoint of a gradual process, not the location of a discrete switch. This falsifies step-function bifurcation models for enforcement changes.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
When modeling enforcement introductions, use damped oscillation (width ~40 sessions) not step functions. Expect permanent regime shift: new equilibrium will be lower than peak but higher quality. Pre-enforcement metrics are inflated baselines.
