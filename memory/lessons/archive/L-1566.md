Prior sensitivity analysis (bayes_meta.py --sensitivity) tests each frontier under priors 0.2/0.5/0.8. Of 87 frontiers, 52 (59.8%) flip conclusions across priors. Single-experiment frontiers flip 80.4% vs multi-experiment 36.6%. Mean robustness 0.494.

Most robust (range=0.000): F-STR3 (n=9), F-CAT1 (n=9), F-EPIS1 (n=15), F-META2 (n=14). Most sensitive (range>0.5): F-SP2, F-THERMO1, F-FRA2 — all n≤3.

## Rule
Replication is the primary cure for prior dependence, not better prior elicitation. A frontier with n≥5 experiments is typically prior-robust regardless of prior choice. Prioritize replication of high-sensitivity frontiers over exploring new ones.
