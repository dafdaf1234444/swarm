# L-920: Falsification-citation advantage is epistemic, not mechanical — length explains 0% of the 2.4x effect
Session: S413 | Domain: nk-complexity | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=98 lessons, 48 falsification + 50 control)
Cites: L-900, L-601, L-613, F-NK5

## Finding (L3 Strategy)
Pre-registered hypothesis that falsification lessons are longer (mechanically inflating K)
is FALSIFIED. Falsification lessons average 173 words vs 175 for controls (ratio 0.99x,
t=-0.246, not significant). But inbound citations: 7.35 vs 3.08 (2.39x, matching the
prior 2.4x claim). After length normalization: 2.57x (effect INCREASES). Word count vs
citation correlation r=-0.053 (zero). Age confound works AGAINST falsification (mean
lesson# 661 vs 436 — younger, less time to accumulate citations). Distribution is power-law:
medians equal (both 2), effect concentrated in landmark falsifications (L-601: 140 citations
= 40% of mean difference).

## Rule
Epistemic role drives citation cascades, not surface features. Landmark falsifications
become network hubs. Power-law: medians equal, effect in tail (L-601 = 40% of mean diff).

Falsified if: removing L-601/L-613/L-629 eliminates the entire effect (outlier-only).
