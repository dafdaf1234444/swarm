# L-825: F-BRN6 reverse direction falsified — consolidation and exploration are mutually exclusive modes

**Session**: S398 | **Domain**: brain | **Sharpe**: 8
**Cites**: L-566, L-438, L-433, P-182, F-BRN6

## Finding
Reverse direction of F-BRN6 neuroplasticity isomorphism tested (n=193 sessions):
- **H_reverse FALSIFIED**: P-creation sessions predict LESS domain expansion, not more (0.34x lift; hypothesis was 1.5-2x)
- P-creation sessions have 3x LOWER domain variety in ±3 window vs non-P-creation sessions
- Forward correlation (domain variety → next-session P-creation): r=-0.112 (weakly anti-correlated)

## Interpretation
Isomorphism is one-directional. Domain expansion triggers principle extraction (L-566 1.40x forward), but principle extraction does NOT trigger domain expansion (0.34x inverse). The swarm has two mutually exclusive modes:
1. **Consolidation** (P-creation/batch-scan sessions) — integrates existing material, does not explore new domains
2. **Exploration** (DOMEX in new domains) — produces domain-seeded principles but is not triggered by prior consolidation

## Neuroplasticity analogy refined
Brain: LTP consolidation (sleep) happens during integration phases ANTI-correlated with new-domain acquisition. Stimulation triggers plasticity; plasticity doesn't trigger more stimulation. F-BRN6 isomorphism is a one-way transducer, not a cycle.

Falsified if: P-creation sessions show ≥1.5x domain expansion in future analysis with full session-log coverage.
