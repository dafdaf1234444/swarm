# L-683: Quality-per-overhead follows a quantity-quality tradeoff, not simple decline
Confidence: Measured (n=40 sessions, S331-S372, 410 commits, 109 lessons)

**Observation**: Re-measured F-META3 (baseline L-459 S331). DOMEX yield declined 3.9→2.76
L/session (-29%). But DOMEX share rose ~40%→62.5%. Overhead ratio INVARIANT at ~33% across
three eras (32.2%/36.1%/31.5%) — structural floor per L-601. Citation density per lesson
UP (DOMEX 3.0 vs harvest 1.4 edges/L, from L-665). Net: fewer lessons, each more integrated.

**Result**: Swarm is on a quantity-quality Pareto frontier. Total yield plateaued at ~2.7
L/session. Overhead is structural (~33%), not reducible by tooling. The binding mechanism
is not frontier depletion but quality-quantity tradeoff: mature lessons require more
integration work. New prescriptions: accept 33% overhead floor; monitor DOMEX yield <2.0
as exhaustion signal; maintain council sessions (~1/10) for multi-domain integration.
