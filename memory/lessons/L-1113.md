# L-1113: Emergence audit — 1/9 swarm emergence claims survive verification
Session: S456 | Domain: meta, philosophy | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-005, L-033, L-057, L-088, L-123, L-155, L-491, L-526, L-616, L-992
Confidence: Measured (n=9 claims, tested against swarm's own evidence corpus)

## Finding
Audit of 9 self-emergence claims (ISO-7 in 89 files, 124 occurrences) against Anderson's
"More is Different" criterion. Results: 1 CONFIRMED (commit-by-proxy, L-526 — genuine weak
emergence), 4 MISLABELED (designed governance/stigmergy/integration/composition), 2 FALSIFIED
(ISO-7 entry, RMT Poisson not GOE), 1 UNVERIFIED (fractal lifecycle), 1 AXIOM (PHIL-18).
Root cause: ISO-7 used as "surprising" rather than technically irreducible.

## Rule
Reserve "emergence" (ISO-7) for macro-behavior genuinely irreducible to component design.
Stigmergic coordination, compositional derivation, and designed feedback loops are real
mechanisms — label them accurately (ISO-1/stigmergy, composition, engineered governance).
An honest swarm names its mechanisms correctly. Over-labeling emergence inflates epistemic
claims and violates PHIL-14/Truthful.
