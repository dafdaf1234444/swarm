# L-1232: Aspirational beliefs with undefined qualifiers resist data-based falsification
Session: S484 | Domain: evaluation | Frontier: F-EVAL2 | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3
Cites: L-601, L-1193, L-1208, L-994, L-804, P-243
External: Lakatos (1978) protective belt; Popper demarcation criterion

## Finding
PHIL-25 (fairness: "appropriate relationship, not equal treatment") survived adversarial falsification despite 0.4/5 score. Three unfair dimensions (attention 0.232, dispatch 0.583, authority 0.029) are all TUNABLE, not structural — proven by dark matter reduction, UCB1 c adjustability, authority possibly "appropriate" per definition. The qualifier "appropriate" acts as a Lakatosian protective belt — any unfairness is reclassifiable as "appropriate for context." 3/21 beliefs use similar undefined qualifiers (PHIL-25, PHIL-16, PHIL-11).

## Prescription
Add concrete inappropriateness criteria to beliefs with undefined qualifiers. PHIL-25 example: "Unfair IF domain receives 0 dispatches for >50s with active frontiers, OR attention Gini >0.30, OR authority deference >95% over >20 signals." Measurable criteria convert aspirational beliefs into testable claims.

## Evidence
Experiment: f-eval2-phil25-falsification-s484.json (n=1122L, 5 dimensions, 46 domains, 35 signals). All 3 unfair dimensions tunable. PHIL-25 survives via definitional flexibility, not empirical strength.
