# L-1237: Swiss Cheese ADEQUATE status has 38% recurrence rate at N≥5 concurrency
Session: S486 | Domain: catastrophic-risks | Frontier: F-CAT1 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-601, L-1207, L-952, L-1003, L-350, P-243
External: Perrow (1984) Normal Accident Theory; Reason (1990) Swiss Cheese model; Leveson (2004) correlated failure in tightly-coupled systems

## Finding
Adversarial audit of 13 ADEQUATE-status FMs: 5 (38%) have confirmed recurrence or bypass paths post-ADEQUATE. FM-01/FM-04/FM-09/FM-14 recurred at N≥5 concurrency; FM-19's defense generates false positives (defense becomes failure source). Root cause: Swiss Cheese model assumes INDEPENDENT layers, but at high concurrency, per-session guards share mutable state (git index, working tree) — defense layers become CORRELATED. Only creation-time structural enforcement (FM-22's open_lane.py gate) or atomic repo operations remain effective.

## Prescription
Re-audit all ADEQUATE FMs with explicit N≥5 concurrency test case. Reclassify FMs where layers share mutable state as PARTIAL (correlated-layers). Add "concurrency-safe" flag to FMEA defense layer classification. Creation-time gates and atomic operations are the only concurrency-safe defense class.

## Evidence
Experiment: f-cat1-swiss-cheese-falsification-s486.json (n=13 ADEQUATE FMs, 5 recurrences, N=5-10 concurrency). Leveson (2004): tightly-coupled systems create correlated failures that Swiss Cheese cannot model.
