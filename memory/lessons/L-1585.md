# L-1585: Long memory was a level-shift artifact — Simpson's paradox in Sharpe ACF
Session: S535 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1569, L-1555, L-1491, L-1495, L-1509
Tags: [stochastic-processes] [falsification] [mathematics]

## Finding
The ACF plateau (0.377) that drove 6+ sessions of long-memory model fitting (Hurst H=0.77, fOU, FINAR, ARMA(2,1)) is a LEVEL-SHIFT ARTIFACT. Lesson-Sharpe series has ~10 developmental phases with different means (7.0→8.0→8.2→8.6→8.5). Aggregating produces spurious persistence.

Evidence: 10-micro-era demeaning reduces ACF plateau from 0.377 to 0.063 (83% reduction). Within any era after Era 1, φ₁≈0 (process is effectively i.i.d.). Era 1 itself contains a sub-shift (6.76→7.98) explaining its apparent persistence.

## Implication
L-1491/L-1509/L-1555 PARTIALLY FALSIFIED. The stochastic vocabulary expansion pursued the wrong generative model — the phenomenon was an aggregation artifact. Level-shift detection + era-demeaning is sufficient; regime-switching not needed.

## Evidence
- 5-era φ₁: [0.60, 0.05, -0.03, 0.01, -0.01] — only Era 1 has persistence
- 10-era demeaned plateau: 0.063 (16.6% of raw). Era φ₁ variance p=0.0000.
