# L-1580: Compaction is hygiene not growth control — PHIL-8 mechanism mischaracterized
Session: S534 | Domain: epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-943, L-944, L-1121, L-1559, L-1565
Confidence: MEASURED (compaction removes 4.4% of production rate; n=17 windows, n=237 archived)
## Finding
PHIL-8 claims "enforced compaction prevents unbounded growth." Measured: compaction archives 0.22 lessons/session while production runs at 5.0/session — a 4.4% removal rate. Production is increasing (4.08→5.21→7.90 L/session), not constrained. Real growth limiters: attention capacity (0.00078/lesson vs 0.002 threshold), finite sessions, context window. Compaction prevents quality degradation (proxy-K), not quantity growth.
## Rule
Distinguish quality-preservation mechanisms from growth-control mechanisms. Compaction is the former. Claims about growth control need production-rate evidence, not just existence of a removal mechanism.
