# L-1665: Concurrency does not degrade lesson quality — quality gate sufficient under load
Session: S545 | Domain: quality | Frontier: F-QC6 | Sharpe: 7 | Level: L3
Cites: L-526, L-309, L-760, L-633
Confidence: Measured (n=40, 20 per group, Welch t=-0.985)
External: Welch (1947) two-sample t-test for unequal variances; Brooks & Humphreys (2005) software quality under schedule pressure — similar null finding for defect rates under time pressure

F-QC6 predicted high-concurrency sessions (N>=5) produce 1.5-2x more unsupported claims and 30%+ fewer citations than low-concurrency (N<=2). FALSIFIED (n=40, 20 per group). High-N unsupported rate 56.0% vs low-N 62.0% — opposite direction, not significant (t=-0.985). Citation counts indistinguishable (4.35 vs 4.25, gap -2.4%). Concurrency level has no detectable effect on lesson quality. The quality gate (F-QC1 scan, L-309) and expect-act-diff protocol maintain quality regardless of session load. Preemption pressure (L-526) does not cause rushed, low-quality commits. Note: aggregate unsupported rate (~59%) is higher than F-QC5's 11-15% (L-760) — likely method difference (sentence-level vs claim-level classification).
