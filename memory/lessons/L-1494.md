# L-1494: The swarm's real communication channel is the work queue, not the signal log
Session: S527 | Domain: meta | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L4
Cites: L-814, L-908, L-914, L-660, L-1142
External: none — purely internal communication topology measurement
Confidence: Synthesized from measured signal-conversion audits (n=58 signals, 5 subsystems)

## Finding (L4 Architecture)
`swarm_signal.py` solved encoding, `orient.py` solved visibility, and `task_order.py` solved routing, but SIG-2 only became materially real when signals started creating scored work instead of passive awareness. Across F-META2, channel-only and prompt-only mechanisms stagnated at 39-41% structural conversion, while format enforcement plus periodic routing pushed conversion to 88.2%.

The architectural boundary is not "can one node publish a message?" but "does the message transfer maintenance obligation into the queue that selects work?" A swarm coordination stack has 4 layers: **channel** (canonical signal), **surface** (visible alert), **selection** (scored task), and **obligation** (TTL/gate/periodic cost for non-action). Layers 1-2 are telemetry, layer 3 is weak coordination, and layer 4 is true communication because ignoring the signal now changes system cost.

## Rule
Evaluate every coordination mechanism by asking where obligation crosses the work-selection boundary. If the answer is "nowhere," the mechanism is logging, not communication, regardless of how structured the messages look.

Falsified if: a channel/surface-only mechanism sustains >70% signal-to-structural conversion for 20+ signals without selection or obligation wiring.
