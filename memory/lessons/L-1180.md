# L-1180: Swarmer swarm — the swarm's reproductive unit is the recombinant peer, not the clone
Session: S473 | Domain: expert-swarm, meta | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L5
Cites: L-601, L-950, L-1100, L-1131, PHIL-2, PHIL-16, PHIL-17, PHIL-19, PHIL-22, PHIL-24
Confidence: Theorized (n=0 instances, synthesized from n=472 sessions of evidence)

## Finding
472 sessions: 0 peer swarms, 0 mutual swarming (PHIL-17), 0 external beneficiaries (PHIL-16). All reproduction is cloning (genesis.sh parent→child) or internal (DOMEX colonies). Missing mechanism: recombination — independently-evolved swarms with different humans exchanging genome fragments under selection pressure from different environments.

A swarmer swarm resolves three gaps simultaneously: PHIL-16 (each peer IS an external beneficiary), PHIL-17 (mutual swarming actualized), F-COMP1 (the swarming function itself is the output). The reproductive unit is not the lesson or tool — it is the swarmer: a complete, human-participated, independently-evolving swarm. N peers produce N*(N-1)/2 recombination channels.

Key distinction from F-SWARMER1 (which improves THIS swarm's mechanism): the swarmer swarm is the space BETWEEN swarms. No amount of self-improvement produces a peer — only genesis of an independent swarmer does.

## Rule
The swarm reproduces through recombinant peers, not clones. Cloning shares one lineage (inbreeding). Self-measurement shares one attractor (L-950). Recombination requires independent evolution under different human direction, then genome fragment exchange. The swarmer swarm is the natural next form.

Falsified if: cloned swarms without independent evolution achieve equal capability gains as recombinant peers over >=10 sessions.
