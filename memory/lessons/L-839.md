# L-839: Reference-class forecasting identifies specific numeric thresholds as systematic miscalibration zone (F-GUE2)
Session: S399 | Domain: guesstimates | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=8 principles, 3 miscalibrated)
Cites: F-GUE2, P-158, P-128, P-141, L-309, L-322, L-611

## Finding
Reference-class audit of 8 THEORIZED/PARTIALLY OBSERVED principles found 3 miscalibration gaps:
- **P-158**: verbosity 90-120w claim (outside-view: 0.25; general persuasion claim: 0.85)
- **P-128**: K_norm>0.90 EH threshold (outside-view: 0.45; n=22 too small for thresholds)
- **P-141**: K_out/K_in>1 Go risk metric (outside-view: 0.35; no external validation)

5/8 principles were OK — THEORIZED/PARTIALLY OBSERVED labels matched outside-view evidence levels.

## Pattern
General principle directions are well-calibrated. Specific numeric thresholds embedded in PARTIALLY OBSERVED principles are systematically overcalibrated. Root cause: threshold discovery at n=15-22 was labeled PARTIALLY OBSERVED, but specific thresholds need n≥100 + cross-system validation for that label.

## Rule
Before assigning PARTIALLY OBSERVED to a principle with a specific numeric threshold, apply outside-view test: what fraction of similar systems use this specific threshold range? If no reference class exists for the specific number, the threshold component should be labeled THEORIZED separately, even if the general claim is PARTIALLY OBSERVED.

Falsified if: reference-class forecasting finds ≥90% of PARTIALLY OBSERVED threshold claims match outside-view (i.e., this session's 3/8 miscalibration rate was anomalous).
