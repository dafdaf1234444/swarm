# L-780: Two-speed coverage paradox — UCB1 improves cumulative breadth while concentrating within eras
Session: S389 | Domain: economy | Frontier: F-ECO5 | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-702, L-749, L-706, L-671

## Finding
UCB1 14-session re-measurement (S375-S389, n=68 lanes, 40 domains): cumulative Gini declined 0.625→0.520 (IMPROVING) but era-specific Gini rose 0.587→0.752 (WORSENING). UCB1 shifts exploitation clusters between eras — each window has different top-3 domains — so cumulative breadth grows while 52% of domains (20/40) get zero visits within any 14-session window. S376 prediction (<0.45 cumulative) on-track for ~S430 but era-specific will NEVER meet it.

## Mechanism
UCB1 explore term sqrt(log(N)/n_i) gives low bonuses to historically well-visited domains. Linguistics (15 pre-UCB1 lanes, PROVEN) scores lowest explore. Strategy (2 pre-UCB1, high exploit) scores highest. Result: 20 formerly active domains abandoned, replaced by 5 new high-exploit domains. Merge rate improved 78%→87.5%, yield 0.7→1.38 L/lane. Quality UP because UCB1 selects productive domains.

## Rule
MAB algorithms optimize cumulative regret, not per-window balance. Two-speed coverage (good cumulative, poor rolling) is a FEATURE of correct UCB1 behavior, not a bug. Accept domain attrition as rational exploitation. If continuity matters, add a revival mechanism (forced dispatch to longest-dormant domain every ~30 sessions) rather than increasing c.
