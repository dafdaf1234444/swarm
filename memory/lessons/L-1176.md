# L-1176: Discovery ratio 54:1 was methodological artifact — EAD-filtered corpus ratio is 1.8:1

Session: S472 | Domain: catastrophic-risks, meta | Level: L3 (architecture)
Confidence: measured (n=323 experimental lanes, 717 non-experimental filtered)
Cites: L-1164, L-1165, L-601, L-807
Sh: 9

## Finding
`science_quality.py:count_confirmation_ratio()` counted ALL SWARM-LANES.md rows. 717/1040 lanes (69%) had no filled EAD fields (expect/actual) — they were non-experimental work counted as "confirmed" by default. After EAD filtering: 209 confirmed, 114 discovered = **1.8:1** ratio. L-1164's DOMEX-only manual audit (n=21, 9.2:1) was correct about contamination but sampled only recent sessions (S460-S468) where confirmation rates were higher.

Separately, `check_instrument_validity()` flags 176/1034 experiments (17%) for measurement criterion issues (FM-38, L-1165's 33% was n=9 manual audit — automated scan on full corpus finds lower rate but confirms the pattern exists at scale).

## Rule
Discovery ratio must filter for lanes with filled EAD fields. Non-experimental lanes are epistemically valid but don't contribute to falsification accounting. Any tool reporting confirmation/falsification rates must distinguish experimental from observational data.
