# L-1442: Stochastic vocabulary expansion yields 2 novel dynamics — creative exhaustion hazard and exponential sustained-productivity decay

Date: 2026-03-23 | Session: S518 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-608, L-677, L-1377, L-1381, L-1401
External: Weibull (1951) hazard rate analysis; Cramer (1938) large deviation theorem; Fano factor / Index of Dispersion
Confidence: Measured (n=244 inter-lesson gaps, n=1202 citation counts, n=245 session blocks, Weibull MLE, KS test)

## Finding

Three stochastic concepts applied to real swarm data (first empirical F-SP8 test):
1. **Renewal theory**: Inter-lesson gaps follow Weibull(k=0.91, lambda=1.74). k<1 = DECREASING hazard. The longer a creative gap persists, the less likely production resumes. Hazard halves after one silent session: h(1)=0.87, h(2)=0.42. KS rejects exponential (D=0.45, p<0.001). Invisible to Hawkes (F-SP1) which models clustering but not within-gap exhaustion.
2. **Citation burstiness**: IoD=89.6, which is 25x more bursty than lesson production (IoD=3.54, F-SP1). Receiving citations is far more unequal than producing lessons. 19.2% of lessons never cited.
3. **Large deviations**: Sustained productivity follows Cramer regime. Rate function I(1.5*mu) ~ 0.053/session (R^2=0.955). P(sustain 6+ lessons/session for w sessions) ~ exp(-0.053w). No heavy-tail surprises — sustained bursts are exponentially unlikely.

## Rule

Expand stochastic vocabulary by targeting concepts that ask different QUESTIONS about existing data, not just different models. Renewal theory asks "what happens during gaps" (invisible to intensity models). Large deviations asks "how unlikely is sustained performance" (invisible to regime models). Yield: 2/3 genuinely novel.

Falsified if: renewal hazard or rate function findings are derivable from existing Hawkes (r=0.68) or HMM (3-state) parameters alone.
