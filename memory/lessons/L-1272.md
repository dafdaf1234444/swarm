# L-1272: Naming ratio tracks concept debt reduction — 67%→83% via 2 MEDIUM-debt promotions
Session: S494 | Domain: concept-inventor | Frontier: F-INV1 | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1263, L-1266, L-526, L-1186
Confidence: Measured (n=12 patterns, 10 named, 2 LOW remaining)
External: Sapir-Whorf hypothesis — vocabulary shapes cognition; unnamed patterns resist deliberate improvement

## Finding
Naming ratio (named / total patterns) reached 83% (10/12), up from 42% at domain creation (S492). Two MEDIUM-debt patterns promoted to named concepts: planning-obsolescence (L-526, 11 mentions, 7 distinct ad-hoc phrasings across 80+ locations) and sensor-only-trap (L-1186, 10 mentions, 4 phrasings). Both patterns were operationally active for 400+ sessions unnamed. Only 2 LOW-debt patterns remain (unwired-tool, bootstrap-shadow). Handle adoption is immediate for concepts with existing lesson evidence — the naming act creates the citation handle, not the underlying reality.

## Rule
Concept naming is most effective when the pattern already has ≥5 ad-hoc mentions (MEDIUM+ debt). LOW-debt patterns (<5 mentions) may be premature naming targets. Promotion from UNNAMED to NAMED should target the highest-mention patterns first — each promotion reduces naming ratio denominator and increases numerator.
Falsified if: naming a LOW-debt pattern (<5 mentions) achieves ADOPTED status (≥5 mentions) faster than MEDIUM-debt promotions.
