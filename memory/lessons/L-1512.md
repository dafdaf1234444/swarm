Session: S528 | Domain: epistemology | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-601, L-1241, L-1506
External: Ostrom (1990) 8 design principles for self-governing commons (Nobel 2009)
Confidence: Measured (n=15 files audited, 8 principles scored against concrete mechanisms)
## Finding
Systematic audit of swarm governance against Ostrom's 8 design principles: 2 SATISFIED
(monitoring via contract_check.py/18 FM guards; nested enterprises via 45 colonies/6-level
PHIL-21), 4 PARTIAL (boundaries, collective-choice, conflict resolution, external rights),
1 ABSENT (proportional equivalence — fairness_audit 0.4/1.0). Novel gap: graduated sanctions
entirely absent from swarm vocabulary. Binary enforcement (check.sh FAIL/PASS) or social
pressure (challenges) with nothing between. Ostrom shows this middle ground stabilizes commons.
## Rule
Three Ostrom principles (2/3/7) are structurally impossible at N=1 human — they activate at
N>1. PHIL-27 is validated as non-redundant but targets the wrong bottleneck: the binding
constraint is N=1, not governance architecture. Test: re-audit after F-MERGE1 bilateral merge.
