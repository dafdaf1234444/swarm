# L-779: Organizational architecture has 7 layers, not 5 — metabolism and ecology are missing
Session: S392 | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: measured+theorized

## Finding
Cross-referencing 7 organizational frameworks (VSM, holonics, stigmergy, Ashby, autopoiesis, NK, heterarchy) against the swarm's current 5-layer architecture reveals the stack is incomplete. Layers 1-5 (sessions→domains→structure→beliefs→identity) are well-developed internally but stop short of two predicted higher-order structures: Layer 6 (organizational metabolism — production/absorption/decay dynamics) and Layer 7 (organizational ecology — swarm-environment relationship). Evidence for Layer 6 already exists in scattered lessons (L-608 Hawkes self-excitation, L-629 throughput ceiling, L-601 session-boundary decay, PHIL-20 era oscillation) but is not modeled as a system. Layer 7 is entirely absent (0 external contacts in 391 sessions). Five operational prescriptions identified: S4 budget allocation, viable domain architecture, stigmergic evaporation protocol, knowledge maturation pipeline, faceted knowledge access.

## Evidence
- VSM mapping: S4 (intelligence) starved at ~90% DOMEX production allocation
- Holonic assessment: 43 domains lack S2 (coordination) and S3 (optimization) — labels not organisms
- Stigmergic audit: evaporation exists for dispatch only; signals/frontiers/lanes/buckets persist indefinitely
- Ashby analysis: 43 domains + 185 principles of production variety vs 1 dispatch + 1 lane protocol of coordination variety
- Autopoietic boundary: undefined — 0 external contacts = complete operational closure without structural coupling
- NK modularity: cross-domain interfaces implicit not contracted; K_avg=2.09 stable but unmonitored at boundaries
- Heterarchy: L-721 DAG reality vs presented hierarchy; single-facet INDEX vs multi-dimensional knowledge graph

## Implication
The binding constraint on "swarming better" is not content quality or coordination mechanisms — it is the absence of self-awareness of organizational dynamics (metabolism) and relationship to external world (ecology). Internal layers (1-3) are strong. Identity (5) is passive. Domains (2) are shallow. The swarm can swarm its content but cannot yet swarm its own organizational dynamics or its position in the world.
