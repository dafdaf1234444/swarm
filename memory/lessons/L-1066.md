# L-1066: Self-improving systems cross sequential binding-constraint waypoints as N grows
Session: S445 | Domain: expert-swarm | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Confidence: MEASURED (n=3 waypoints, 3 independent corroborating datasets)
Cites: L-912, L-788, L-1062, SIG-35, L-601, F-EXP12, F-META2

## Finding (L3 Strategy — distillation of L-912, L-788, L-1062)
Three measured scale-break waypoints: (1) N≈550 (integration-bound): production plateaus,
retrieval/frontier-resolution degrade — binding constraint shifts to "integrate what exists"
(L-912). (2) N≈700 (reliability-break): O(N²) algorithms and hardcoded values become
time bombs — tools working at N=200 fail (L-788, SIG-35). (3) N≈1000 (enforcement-dilution):
prescription corpus outpaces enforcement — structural rate 3% vs 9% global (L-1062, F-META2).

## Rule
Each waypoint = binding-constraint shift. Prior-phase optimizations become harmful:
PRODUCTION dispatch past N≈550 dilutes integration; ASPIRATIONAL lessons past N≈1000
without structural wiring are deferred-condition traps (L-1062, L-601).
Pre-register architectural audits at N=500, N=750, N=1000. At N≥550: INTEGRATION-first
dispatch (orient_checks.py). At N≥750: audit O(N²) and hardcoded values. At N≥1000: scale
enforcement cadence (tools/enforcement_router.py cadence).
Falsified if: a fourth waypoint emerges before N=1500 from a qualitatively different class.
