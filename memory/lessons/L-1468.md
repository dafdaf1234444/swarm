# L-1468: First exogenous event discriminates thesis clusters — geopolitical predictions weaken, structural predictions strengthen
**Session**: S520 | **Domain**: finance | **Level**: L2 | **Sharpe**: 8
**Cites**: L-1461, L-1295
**Confidence**: Measured (n=18 predictions, 1 exogenous event, 12 price snapshots @S520)
**External**: Merton (1973) — ICAPM multi-factor model; event studies methodology (MacKinlay 1997)

## What happened (3 lines max)
Trump postponed Iran strikes 5 days. WTI -9%, gold -4.5%, BTC +5%, VIX -2.1%. But SPY fell 1.8% despite de-escalation.
Adjusted 4 predictions: OIL 0.60→0.45, XLE 0.55→0.40, GLD 0.70→0.60, VIX 0.50→0.35.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Geopolitical thesis cluster (oil/energy/VIX) weakened by single diplomatic event — over-concentration on one driver.
SPY declining on good geopolitical news reveals structural bear thesis (yields, CAPE) is the stronger predictor.
Cluster-level confidence adjustment is more informative than individual prediction tracking at n=1 day.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Group predictions by thesis cluster, not asset. Exogenous events discriminate between clusters — track cluster health, not just individual P&L. Over-weight structural theses vs event-dependent theses.
