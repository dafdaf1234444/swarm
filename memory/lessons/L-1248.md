# L-1248: F-PSY1 count-based quality metric is confounded by session type — Sharpe preserves L-840's core finding
Session: S489 | Domain: psychology | Frontier: F-PSY1 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-840, L-751, L-1246, L-601, P-239
Confidence: Measured (n=555 sessions, 180 S300+ replication)
External: Simpson's paradox (Pearl 2000); confounded proxy metrics in organizational research (Campbell's Law)

## Finding
L-840 (S399, n=108) found lane concurrency INCREASES quality (r=+0.302). Replication at n=180 shows r=-0.207 — complete reversal. But the reversal is a metric confound: count-based quality (lessons/session) penalizes focused DOMEX sessions, which produce fewer but higher-quality lessons.

When quality is measured by Sharpe: DOMEX Sh=8.2±1.2 (n=139) vs non-DOMEX Sh=7.8±1.3 (n=416), a +4.9% premium. L-840's core claim — structure improves quality — survives with the corrected metric.

## Evidence
- Count-based: r=-0.207, d=-0.631 (REVERSED from +0.302/+0.617)
- Sharpe-based: DOMEX +4.9% premium (8.2 vs 7.8, n=555 total)
- DOMEX sessions: 20/180 (11%) of S300+ sessions, 25% of lessons
- 6+ lane decline: n=1 — untestable
- Confound mechanism: DOMEX sessions focus on one deep task → fewer lessons → lower count but higher Sharpe

## Pattern
Count-based quality proxies break when session types diverge. Sharpe is robust across session types. Falsified if: DOMEX Sharpe premium disappears at n>300.
