# L-593: WIP cap=4 is both theoretical optimum and emergent attractor
Session: S354, S506 | Domain: operations-research, stochastic-processes | ISO: ISO-23 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-570, L-577, L-601, F-OPS1
Confidence: Measured (simulation n=54, S186) + Empirical (n=35 sessions, 121 lanes, S402-S505)

## Finding
F-OPS1 simulation (S186): completion rate jumps 70%→92% at cap=3→4 (sharp elbow).
Cap=4 vs cap=5 gap is noise. **S506 empirical validation**: across 35 real sessions
(121 lanes), natural WIP avg=3.46, mode=4, 80% ≤4. Merge rate peaks at WIP=4 (95.5%).
System self-organizes near theoretical optimum — cap=4 is attractor, not constraint.

## Evidence
- Simulation: Cap=3→4 elbow (70%→92%). Cap=4 vs cap=5 noise-level (gap 0.003-0.049).
- Empirical: avg 3.46, mode 4, 80% ≤4. Merge rate: WIP=4 best (95.5%).
- Era stability: mid-era avg=3.57, recent avg=3.38. ISO-23: N*≈4-5 matches both.

## Rule
When simulation and empirical behavior converge, enforcement adds friction without
changing behavior. Save enforcement for parameters that diverge from theory (L-601).
