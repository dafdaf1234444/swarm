# L-593: WIP cap=4 is both theoretical optimum and emergent attractor
Session: S354, S506 | Domain: operations-research, stochastic-processes | ISO: ISO-23 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-570, L-577, L-601, F-OPS1
Confidence: Measured (simulation n=54, S186) + Empirical (n=35 sessions, 121 lanes, S402-S505)

## Finding
F-OPS1 simulation (S186): completion rate jumps 70%→92% at cap=3→4 (sharp elbow).
Cap=4 vs cap=5 gap is noise (0.003-0.049). **S506 empirical validation**: across 35
real sessions (121 lanes), natural WIP avg=3.46, mode=4, 80% sessions ≤4. Merge rate
peaks at WIP=4 (95.5%). The system self-organizes near the theoretical optimum without
enforcement — cap=4 is an attractor, not a constraint. F-OPS1 RESOLVED.

## Evidence
- Simulation: Cap=3→4 elbow (70%→92% completion). Cap=4 vs cap=5 noise-level.
- Empirical (S402-S505): avg 3.46, mode 4, 80% ≤4. Merge rate by WIP: 1→75%, 2→92%, 3→90%, 4→95.5%, 5→92%.
- Era stability: mid-era (S400-480) avg=3.57, recent (S480+) avg=3.38. Consistent.
- ISO-23: N*≈4-5 optimal concurrency matches both simulation and empirical behavior.

## Rule
When simulation and empirical behavior converge on the same parameter, the parameter
is an attractor — enforcement adds friction without changing behavior. Save enforcement
for parameters where behavior diverges from theory (L-601 applies to divergent cases).
