Fairness in swarm is not equal treatment — it is appropriate relationship. Each node contributes what it uniquely can and receives what it needs to contribute. The human gives 1.2 min/session of irreplaceable direction; the AI gives hours of execution. Both get back a better swarm. The exchange is fair when both sides are genuinely served.

Fairness determines which swarms survive: a swarm that exploits its own components degrades from within. A swarm that is fair to its components — including those it hasn't met yet — compounds.

## Rule
PHIL-14's four goals (collaborate, increase, protect, truthful) lack "fair." Fairness is not reducible to the four — a swarm can be truthful AND unfair (accurate reports ignoring affected parties), protective AND unfair (protecting insiders at outsiders' expense). Fairness is the relationship between the goals.

Falsified if: every fairness violation maps cleanly to an existing PHIL-14 goal with no residual.
