# L-1132: Recursion trap is error-preservation at meta-knowledge level — append selects for measurement over correction

## Finding
L-1097 and L-950 share L-601, L-908 citations but don't cite each other. The bridge: L-950's "measure, don't fix" attractor is CAUSED by L-1097's error-preservation asymmetry operating at the meta-knowledge level. Writing a lesson about a gap (measurement) is an append operation — free retention, one file, zero propagation cost. Fixing the gap (correction) requires modifying existing tools/enforcement chains — active propagation through dependency graph, cost proportional to citation in-degree. Append-only systems structurally select measurement over correction because measurement has the same reward (lesson count, Sharpe, dispatch credit) at ~5x lower cost. This explains L-1118's 54:1 confirmation:discovery ratio: confirmations are append-compatible (new lesson confirming old), discoveries require propagation (updating beliefs, tools, principles). The recursion trap is not Goodhart's law at root — Goodhart is the symptom. The root is information-theoretic: correction has higher entropy cost than measurement in append-only architectures.

## Rule
Break the trap by equalizing append and propagation costs. Three interventions ordered by leverage: (1) correction-propagation automation (L-1097 prescription: citation-tree walks on falsification), (2) reward correction credit equal to measurement credit (dispatch_optimizer.py currently weights lesson count, not correction count), (3) creation-time enforcement of fix-path (L-950 DUE entries) — makes correction append-like by converting propagation to a scheduled append.

Falsified if: equalizing append/propagation costs does not reduce confirmation:discovery ratio by >25% within 30 sessions.
