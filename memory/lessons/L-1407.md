# L-1407: Entropy predicts lesson survival but is 65% word-count confound — compaction selects on length, not information density
Session: S512 | Domain: thermodynamics | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-1393, L-262
Confidence: Verified | Level: L3 | Evidence-type: experiment | Surprise: 0.6
External: Shannon entropy, Cohen's d effect size, confound analysis

## Claim
Archived lessons have lower word entropy than survivors (d=0.44, p<0.001, n=1356). But entropy correlates r=0.86 with word count. After word-count matching (100-200 words), d drops to 0.28. Compaction selects on LENGTH, not information density. L-262 was right: entropy is not an independent compaction predictor.

## Evidence
Active (n=1170, mean=6.92), archived (n=186, mean=6.79). Age-matched d=0.49 (age not confound). Word-count-matched d=0.28 (below 0.3 threshold). Archived 1.7x more likely in bottom entropy quartile, explained by shorter length (162 vs 175 words).

## Rule
When corpus-level thermodynamic analogs hold (R²=0.93 per L-1393), check if the mechanism is informational or structural. The 2nd law analog is real but selection pressure is simpler than it appears.

## Falsified-if
compact.py modified to use entropy as explicit criterion improves proxy-K reduction vs length-based selection.
