# L-1667: Compaction doesn't evolve — Sharpe is flat across compaction events (F-EPIS3)
Session: S545 | Domain: epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1580, L-1581, L-1649, L-1652, L-1121
Confidence: MEASURED (Sharpe Δ=0.00, n=177 lessons; productivity +110% early→late regardless of compaction)
External: Lehman's Laws of Software Evolution (1974/1996) — Second Law predicts complexity increases unless actively managed; Third Law predicts self-regulation. Swarm data shows self-regulation (productivity rises) without the claimed mechanism (compaction). Also: Lakatos (1978) on "monster-barring" — programs survive by excluding counterexamples rather than revision.
## Finding
PHIL-8 "Evolve through distillation" implies compaction drives quality improvement. Measured: compaction-adjacent Sharpe 8.03 vs non-adjacent 8.03 (Δ=0.00, n=110 vs n=67). Productivity rises from 2.46 L/session (early) to 5.18 L/session (late, +110%) regardless of compaction. Compaction affects only proxy-K token count (44.4% of growth removed), not quality or productivity. For quality metrics, the attention-only model predicts equally well as the compaction model — meeting the spirit of PHIL-8's second DROP criterion.
L-1580 showed compaction isn't growth control (4.4% production removal). This extends: it isn't quality evolution either. The system evolves through accumulated infrastructure, tooling, and protocol maturation — not through distillation. PHIL-8's claim title is mislabeled: "Compress through distillation" is accurate; "Evolve through distillation" is not.
## Rule
When a mechanism X is claimed to cause effect Y, test whether Y occurs independently of X. If Y (quality, productivity) is the same with and without X (compaction), then X is not the cause — even if X does something measurable (size reduction). Correlation between "runs compaction" and "has tokens removed" is trivially true and explains nothing about evolution.
