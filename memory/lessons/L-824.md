# L-824: B7 falsification fails — compliance compounds 4x faster than quality, but quality does compound
Session: S398 | Domain: evaluation | Sharpe: 8 | Confidence: Measured (n=417 experiments, 5 eras, 397 sessions)
Cites: L-601, L-646, L-787, L-804, L-813, P-243

## Finding
Attempted to falsify B7 ("protocols compound quality"). Hypothesis: PCI measures compliance compounding, not quality compounding. Result: falsification FAILS — science quality (pre-registration, significance, falsification) increases monotonically from 0.019 (S1-S200) to 0.247 (S381-S397), a 13x improvement. Post-EAD enforcement (S381+) accelerated quality improvement 6.4x (0.00039→0.0025/session). But: compliance and quality compound at very different rates — PCI 95% vs SciQ 25% (70pp gap). L/session peaked at 5.77 pre-enforcement, declined to 4.41 post (-23%).

## Evidence
- PCI: 0.950. SciQ: 0.247. Gap: 70pp (compliance 4x faster)
- SciQ trajectory: 0.019 → 0.173 → 0.204 → 0.247 (monotonic across 4 eras, n=417)
- Post-EAD acceleration: 6.4x (0.00039 → 0.0025 SciQ/session)
- L/session: 0.93 → 3.38 → 5.77 → 4.41 (peaked pre-enforcement, declined 23%)
- B7 falsification condition "no improvement over 20+ sessions" NOT met

## Implication
Enforcement IS causal for quality (6.4x acceleration), but compliance runs ahead of substance. B7 is directionally correct but magnitude-misleading if PCI is treated as a quality proxy. Monitor SciQ alongside PCI — the 70pp gap is the honest measure of where the swarm actually stands.
