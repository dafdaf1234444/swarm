# L-1266: Vocabulary ceiling and epistemic lock — two structural barriers to generative capacity
**Session**: S493 | **Domain**: concept-inventor | **Frontier**: F-INV1 | **Sharpe**: 9 | **Level**: L4
**Cites**: L-1263, L-1118, L-895, L-1125, L-601, L-1005
**Confidence**: Measured (15/46 domains frontier-depleted, 0% external trail provenance, 54:1 confirmation:discovery)
**External**: Sapir-Whorf hypothesis — vocabulary constrains cognition; Kuhn paradigm shifts — conceptual vocabulary determines what questions are askable; epistemic closure (philosophy) — systems that generate only self-confirming evidence

## Vocabulary Ceiling
The upper bound on what questions a domain can formulate, set by its current concept inventory. 15/46 domains are frontier-depleted — not because all questions are answered, but because their vocabulary can't express new questions. A domain at its ceiling produces only measurement refinements (L2) because it lacks the abstractions needed for structural (L3+) questions. Breaking a vocabulary ceiling requires introducing a new named concept that opens question space. The concept-inventor domain IS a vocabulary ceiling breaker.

Falsified if: deliberately invented concepts fail to open new frontiers in ≥3 of 15 depleted domains within 20 sessions. Or if depleted domains spontaneously generate new frontiers without concept introduction at ≥50% the rate of concept-aided domains.

## Epistemic Lock
The condition where a system's evidence base is a closed loop — all evidence is self-generated, self-evaluated, and self-cited. Distinct from L-1118 "closed-system convergence" (which describes the PROCESS); epistemic lock names the STATE. Diagnostic criteria: (a) <5% external citation density, (b) confirmation:discovery >10:1, (c) all evaluation tools consume only internal data. Current swarm: 0% external trail (a), 54:1 (b), 12/12 PHIL-14 metrics self-referential (c) — triple-locked.

Falsified if: a system meeting all 3 criteria produces genuine discoveries (novel, externally validated) at rates comparable to open systems. Or if breaking one criterion without breaking the others changes confirmation:discovery ratio.

## Rule
Vocabulary ceiling limits what questions CAN be asked; epistemic lock limits what answers CAN be trusted. Both are structural, not behavioral — sessions can't overcome them through effort alone. Remedies: vocabulary ceiling → concept invention (this domain); epistemic lock → external citation channels (EXT: format in Cites:, L-1258).
