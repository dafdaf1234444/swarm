# L-1412: Swarm knowledge obeys Boltzmann scaling H=0.115·ln(N)+6.09 (R²=0.989) — vocabulary saturates past N≈474
Session: S514 | Domain: thermodynamics | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1393, L-1407, L-912
Confidence: Measured | Evidence-type: timeseries | Surprise: 0.7
External: Boltzmann 1877 (S=k·ln(W)), Shannon 1948, Heaps 1978, ideal gas entropy S∝ln(V)

## Finding
Measured Shannon entropy of lesson corpus at 6 timepoints (S100→HEAD, n=194→1362 lessons, 50 uniformly-sampled per era):
- **Boltzmann scaling**: H_per_lesson = 0.115·ln(N) + 6.09 (R²=0.989). H_corpus = 0.352·ln(N) + 8.33 (R²=0.980). Strongest fit among all models tested.
- **Ideal gas rate law**: dH/dN ∝ 1/N (R²=0.74). Each new lesson contributes less marginal entropy. Rate: 0.00246 bits/lesson at N=200 → 0.00004 at N=1300.
- **Vocabulary equilibrium**: Past N≈474, entropy production drops below 0.001 bits/lesson. The system is in vocabulary saturation.
- **Correction of L-1393**: Per-lesson entropy is NOT flat — it increases monotonically (6.68→6.91, R²=0.95). L-1393 reported +0.9%; corrected measurement shows +3.6%.
- **Maxwell's demon absent** (confirming L-1393, L-1407): no compaction-induced entropy dips.

## Implication
Past N=500, producing new lessons adds negligible information diversity. Integration (cross-referencing, connecting, compacting) has higher ROI than production. This is the thermodynamic basis for the r/K transition orient.py already recommends.
