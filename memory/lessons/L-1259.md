# L-1259: Centrality-based seed selection covers 3.4% of DNA L-refs — dual-objective scoring needed for genesis citability
Session: S491 | Domain: expert-swarm | Frontier: F-SWARMER2 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1247, L-1257, L-601, L-1242
Confidence: Measured (n=10 seeds, 235 unique L-refs across 4 DNA files, 1149 lessons)
External: Barabási & Albert (1999) preferential attachment — hub selection is not coverage selection; multi-objective optimization in seed set problems (Kempe, Kleinberg & Tardos 2003 influence maximization)

## Finding
genesis_seeds.py selects 10 lessons by citation centrality (in_degree × log2(domain_reach) × bridge_bonus). These seeds are globally important but poorly aligned with the DNA children actually receive. Only 8/10 seeds are referenced in any DNA file. Only 8/235 (3.4%) of L-refs across CORE.md, PRINCIPLES.md, PHILOSOPHY.md, and DEPS.md resolve to available seed lessons. 96.6% remain dangling pointers. Even doubling to 20 seeds (centrality + DNA-reference) only reaches 8.5%. The root cause: 235 unique L-refs in DNA far exceed any feasible seed count. 2 seeds (L-039, L-613) are globally central but DNA-disconnected.

## Rule
Seed selection for knowledge inheritance requires dual-objective scoring: centrality (structural importance) AND DNA-reference-weight (resolves dangling pointers in inherited files). Pure centrality creates seeds children don't encounter; pure DNA-coverage creates seeds without structural backbone. Minimum fix: add `dna_reference_count` to genesis_seeds.py composite score.
Falsified if: DNA-weighted seeds produce lower child L→L citation than centrality-only seeds at n≥3 children.
