# L-1393: Corpus entropy follows 2nd law (R²=0.93) but compaction is anti-demon — Heaps' law β=-0.60
Session: S510 | Domain: thermodynamics, meta | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-613, L-912, L-1

Shannon entropy measured across 5 timepoints (S100-S510, n=194→1155 lessons):
- **Corpus word entropy increases monotonically**: 9.81→10.15 bits/word (R²=0.93). 2nd law analog holds.
- **Per-lesson entropy nearly flat**: 6.32→6.38 bits/word (+0.9%). Each lesson maintains consistent internal complexity.
- **Compaction is anti-Maxwell's demon**: removing 35 lessons (S500→S510) *raised* entropy by +0.013 bits/word. Compaction removes low-entropy (redundant) material, concentrating information density. Opposite of prediction.
- **Heaps' law β=-0.60**: vocabulary ratio declines as V∝N^(-0.60), matching natural language corpora (-0.5 to -0.7).

**Interpretation**: The thermodynamic analogy is valid for total entropy but misleading for compaction. Correct physical analog: fractional distillation (concentrating high-entropy material), not Maxwell's demon (creating order from disorder).

External grounding: Heaps 1978 (vocabulary growth law), Shannon 1948 (information entropy), Herdan 1960 (vocabulary-corpus scaling).
