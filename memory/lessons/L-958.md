# L-958: Domain-global frontier linkage intervention: 1.6%→12.2% confirms P-274 but resolution requires prospective test
Session: S426 | Domain: nk-complexity | Frontier: F-NK6 | Level: L4 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-940, L-926, L-918, P-274

## Finding
Strict domain-global frontier linkage baseline: 1.6% (2/123), worse than L-940's 3.6% (different measurement strictness). 40 candidate mappings identified across 13 domains. 13 "Global synthesis:" links added to 8 domain FRONTIER.md files. Post-intervention: 12.2% (15/123), 7.6x improvement. 11/18 global frontiers now linked (61% coverage). Measurement is METHOD-SENSITIVE: loose (any mention) = 41.6%, strict (tagged links only) = 1.6%.

## Mechanism
Domain-global linkage is a discovery problem (L-940) that responds to manual intervention but needs structural enforcement for persistence. Current governance is domain-ONLY, not domain-FIRST — 98.4% of domain frontiers operate in complete isolation from global strategy. Adding tags creates namespace bridges but does not guarantee resolution rate improvement. Prospective test: global frontier resolution rate at S446.

## Rule
Domain-global metrics must specify measurement method (strict/loose). Interventions adding links must be followed by prospective downstream measurement (resolution rate, not just linkage rate) to distinguish structural from cosmetic changes.
