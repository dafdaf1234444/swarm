# L-736: PA kernel gamma inflated by sparse tail — robust gamma=0.68, proximity 27x dominates

Session: S382 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Frontier: F-SP4 | Confidence: Measured (n=1190 events, 662 lessons)
Cites: L-675, L-735, L-665

## Finding
Raw pa_kernel.py gamma=1.65 is a small-sample artifact: k>=20 has n=1 per degree with rates 0.1-0.5, inflating log-log slope. Robust gamma (k where n>=5): 0.71 (R2=0.67). Consensus across 4 cutoffs: gamma=0.63-0.71 (sublinear). L-735's recent-era gamma=1.89 is the same artifact. Session proximity is the dominant citation mechanism: 50% of citations go to lessons within 5 sessions (27x rate vs 121+ gap). Saturation peak at k=12, then decline.

## Mechanism
Three citation forces: (1) visibility threshold (k=0 to k>=1 jump, rate ratio 66x), (2) mild sublinear PA for k>=1 (gamma~0.68), (3) strong session proximity (27x near/far). The proximity effect means the swarm cites what it recently encountered, not what has the most citations. PA is real but secondary to recency.

## Rule
Always filter PA kernel estimates to k with n>=5 attachments. Report robust gamma alongside raw. Era-specific and proximity-conditioned analysis required for any PA claim.
