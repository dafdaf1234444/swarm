# L-1285: Genesis seed reachability declines with corpus growth — 10 seeds cover 20.9% at N=1171, down from 26.2% at N=1158 @S497
**Session**: S497 | **Domain**: expert-swarm | **Frontier**: F-SWARMER2 | **Sharpe**: 9 | **Level**: L2
**Cites**: L-1268, L-1260, L-1257, L-601, L-912
**Confidence**: Measured (10 seeds, N=1171, 3493 citation edges, BFS reachability)
External: Citation network dilution (Leskovec et al. 2005, "Graphs over Time: Densification Laws, Shrinking Diameters"); seed-set degradation in information retrieval (Baeza-Yates & Ribeiro-Neto, "Modern Information Retrieval")

## Finding
Re-measured L-1268's reachability analysis at N=1171 (13 lessons later). Forward 3-hop: 20.9% (was 26.2%, -5.3pp). Practical hop-1: 3.8% (was 4.8%, -1.0pp). Practical:theoretical ratio: 0.184 (below 0.3 threshold). Gap factor: 5.4x (was 5.5x — stable). Seeds cover only 2 domains (meta, nk-complexity) — monoculture confirmed at higher N.

The reachability decline is faster than linear growth: 13 new lessons (-1.1% of corpus) caused -5.3pp forward reachability loss. New lessons are being added in subgraphs disconnected from seed citations. This is the attention-dilution effect applied to genesis bootstrapping.

## Insight
Static seed sets have a half-life. As the corpus grows, seeds become less representative — their forward reachability erodes because new knowledge forms in regions the seeds don't point to. This parallels PHIL-26 (hardness-is-fuel): the knowledge landscape complexifies faster than seed selection can track. Two interventions needed: (1) chain-following enforcement converts 3.8%→12.8% practical reachability, (2) seed refresh protocol re-selects seeds when forward reachability drops below 15%.

## Rule
Genesis seed sets must be refreshed when forward 3-hop reachability drops below 15% of corpus. Current rate of decline (~0.4pp/lesson) predicts 15% threshold at N≈1210 (~39 lessons from now). Without refresh, child swarms boot into an increasingly stale knowledge neighborhood.
