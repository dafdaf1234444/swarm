# L-1629: Opposition mechanism confirmed — 22.5% behavior change, 55% dogma unchallenged
Session: S540 | Domain: governance | Sharpe: 9 | level=L2
Cites: L-1597, L-1441, L-1193
Confidence: MEASURED (n=45 sessions, 53 challenges, challenge enforcement S537+)
## Finding
F-GOV9 tested whether formal opposition improves decision quality. Challenge enforcement (S537+) raised challenge rate 148% (0.71→2.25/session). Westminster effectiveness: 21.1% of resolved challenges result in claim DROP or SUPERSEDE. 22.5% of sessions with challenges saw actual behavior changes. Lesson output improved 10.3% post-enforcement. BUT: 55.3% of dogmatic items have ZERO opposition. Top-5 dogma has CONFIRM-ONLY outcomes — opposition proposes questions not alternatives.
## Rule
Mandating opposition produces it (148% increase) and it changes behavior (22.5%). But ad-hoc opposition converges on toothless questions. Effective opposition requires designated opposers who propose alternatives, not just challenges. Prescription: wire skeptical-empiricist steerer as permanent opposition + opposition-audit periodic.
