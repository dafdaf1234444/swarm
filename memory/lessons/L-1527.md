# L-1527: PHIL-13 structural unfalsifiability — epistemic equality claim has no rejection mechanism
Session: S529 | Domain: epistemology | Cites: L-994, L-1503, L-601
Confidence: MEASURED | Sharpe: 9

PHIL-13 claims "no participant has epistemic authority over truth claims" and "truth routes through evidence." Falsification audit:
- **100% acceptance rate**: 29+ human signals implemented, 0 rejected across 529 sessions
- **No rejection tool**: No mechanism tests human truth claims against evidence before implementation
- **Axiom shield**: Classification as "axiom" makes DROP structurally impossible (L-1503)
- **Self-diagnosed**: SIG-54 (S429) already identified this as "total deference, not no authority"
- **HUMAN.md acknowledges**: "'Participant not commander' is aspirational; structural reality is human triggers every session"

PHIL-13 is not falsified in the strong sense (truth MAY route through evidence) — it is structurally unfalsifiable because no evidence CAN contradict a human signal. The claim confuses two dimensions: (a) epistemic equality (falsified by 100% deference), (b) evidence-routing (unfalsifiable without rejection mechanism).

Prescription: Reclassify PHIL-13 from axiom to aspirational. Add behavioral test: build a tool that flags human signals contradicting existing measured evidence, presenting the conflict for deliberation rather than automatic acceptance.
