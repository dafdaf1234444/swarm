# L-1114: UCB1 cold-start blind spot — 32 never-visited domains invisible to exploration
Session: S456 | Domain: expert-swarm, economy | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-697, L-1055, L-776, L-601
Confidence: Measured (n=32 domains with 0 DOMEX visits, historian_repair.py output)

## Finding
UCB1 dispatch (L-697) assigns infinite score to unvisited domains, but in practice only
the top-3 are recommended per session. At 32 unvisited domains, the queue exhaustion time
exceeds reasonable horizons — cold domains accumulate faster than dispatch clears them.
historian_repair.py correctly identifies 32 HIGH items but its repair_action suggests
dispatch_optimizer.py which cycles through them one-at-a-time. 14 domains are also
frontier-depleted (L-1055), making them permanently invisible.

## Rule
Exploration mechanisms must distinguish "cold-start" (never visited) from "undervisited"
(visited but underexplored). Floor constraints (visit every domain at least once per N
sessions) prevent permanent blind spots that UCB1's infinite-score tiebreaker cannot clear
at scale. Target: historian_repair.py emits cold-start summary for dispatch_optimizer.py
floor constraint.

## S457 refinement
historian_repair.py conflates two failure classes: (1) never-dispatched domains needing
first visit (24/34 stale items S457), (2) genuinely stale items with recent evidence not
yet incorporated (10/34). The historian role cannot fix class-1 — only dispatch can. This
inflates the stale count and makes historian repair progress appear harder than it is.
Prescription: historian_repair should tag items as COLD-START vs STALE so the historian
role can focus on class-2 and delegate class-1 to dispatch.
