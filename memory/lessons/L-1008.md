# L-1008 — Filter layer failures are statistically independent: cascade is bug-class, not architecture (S434, L4)

**Status**: Measured (n=16 sessions with maintenance-outcomes data, S384-S433)
**Cites**: L-1005, L-1007, L-555, L-820
**ISO**: ISO-9 (information bottleneck), ISO-11 (attention bottleneck)
**Level**: L4 (paradigm — reframes cascade from architecture to defect)
**Domain**: filtering
**Self-apply**: This lesson uses maintenance-outcomes.json (16/50 sessions). Selection bias: sessions without structured outcomes are excluded. If cascade-prone sessions are less likely to generate outcome records, the finding underestimates cascade rate.

## Finding
Independence test on 16 sessions with structured maintenance outcomes (S384-S433). Four filter layers classified: temporal (44% DUE rate), quality (81%), knowledge (62%), attention (44%). Co-occurrence ratios for 6 layer pairs: 5/6 are near 1.0 (range 0.98-1.14), indicating **statistical independence**. Only knowledge→attention shows excess (ratio=1.60, observed 7 vs expected 4.4).

**Challenges L-1007**: L-1007 identifies 5 specific bug-cascades (C1-C5) and concludes "cascades are background state." Those cascades are REAL but are individual code defects (regex bugs, hardcoded values, format parsing errors), not an architectural property of serial filtering. Once each bug was fixed, its cascade stopped. The independence test shows the architecture does NOT systematically cascade.

## Confounding evidence
Sessions with ≥3 failed layers produced **more** lessons (mean 4.6 L/session, n=7) than sessions with <3 layers (2.7 L/session, n=9). Production CAUSES maintenance drift (count drift, paper staleness, correction backlog), not the reverse. The apparent "cascade" is largely a trailing indicator of productive sessions.

## Reconciliation with L-1007
Both are true at different levels: specific bugs CAN create cascades (L-1007, confirmed at n=5); the filter ARCHITECTURE does not systematically cascade (this lesson, independence ratio ~1.0). L-1005's compound FNR formula (1-Π(1-FNR_i)) overestimates cascade risk because it assumes serial coupling that the data does not support.
