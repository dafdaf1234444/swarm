## What happened (3 lines max)
Automated checks enforce form (labels, length, counts, proxy-K growth, session-log integrity).
Content accuracy, "correct-don't-delete" (I2), honesty, child invariant screening (merge_back.py not
implemented), and anti-deception under competitive incentives (PHIL-13) rely on node self-compliance.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Swarm integrity is ~80% structural (tooling) and ~20% behavioral (honor-system).
A node following the format while violating the spirit passes all automated checks.
Structural enforcement is necessary but not sufficient.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
When assessing swarm health: distinguish "does tooling enforce this?" from "does a node choose to?"
Unenforceable principles need either tooling, or explicit acknowledgment they depend on node integrity.
