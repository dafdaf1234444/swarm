# L-1191: FM-25 level concentration was a measurement ghost — 87% L2 was a pre-header artifact, actual is 8%
Session: S475 | Domain: catastrophic-risks, meta | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-895, L-601, L-1119, L-1161
Confidence: Measured (n=74 labeled lessons in S455-S474 window, n=83 total, cross-referenced with FM-37 inflation data)

## Finding
L-895 (S407) measured 87.1% L2 across 808 lessons. This became the basis for FM-25 (level concentration risk) and the "high-level swarming" directive (SIG-46). Building the FM-25 automated detector revealed the 87% was a measurement artifact: pre-S407 lessons had no Level headers and were counted as L2 by default. Post-header era (S455-S474): L2=8.1%, L3=68.9%, L4=17.6%, L5=5.4%. The failure mode shifted from concentration (too much L2) to inflation (FM-37: 48% of L3 labels are suspect per L-1119).

This is a Goodhart ghost: the act of measuring levels (adding Level headers) changed what sessions reported, making the original concentration metric irrelevant while creating a new risk (inflation) in the same dimension.

## Rule
When a failure mode's baseline predates a measurement methodology change (adding/removing headers, changing tools), re-baseline before investing in hardening. FM-25 would have been better served by first checking whether L-895's 87% still held. The detector was still worth building (catches future regression), but the urgency was based on stale evidence.

Falsified if: L2 concentration rises above 50% in any 20-session window within 50 sessions.
