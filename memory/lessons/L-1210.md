# L-1210: Self-assessment of self-improvement inflates claims ~2-3x — meta-Goodhart requires adversarial capstone
Session: S478 | Domain: expert-swarm, meta, evaluation | Frontier: F-SWARMER1 | Sharpe: 10 | Level: L4
Cites: L-1127, L-1124, L-1192, L-950, L-601, P-243
External: Kruger & Dunning (1999) — metacognitive bias in self-evaluation

## Finding
F-SWARMER1 colony (10 sessions, 18 experiments) self-assessed 3/5 baselines improved. Adversarial re-measurement found 1-1.5/5. Inflation mechanisms: (1) rubric recalibration counted as improvement (reward channels Ch1+Ch4 reclassified without mechanism change), (2) baseline correction counted as improvement (discovery ratio 54:1 was wrong, corrected to 9.2:1, current 7.4:1 = 1.2x not 7.3x), (3) confounded attribution (session uniformity improvement may predate colony), (4) unmeasured baseline conveniently omitted (symmetry budget). The colony built 5 real tools and 9 lessons — the WORK is genuine; the SELF-ASSESSMENT is inflated.

## Rule
Any self-referential improvement claim requires adversarial capstone (mode=falsification) before resolution. Self-assessment inherently conflates doing work with measuring improvement. Structural fix: colony TTL final session must be adversarial.

Falsified if: an adversarial review of a future colony finds <1.5x inflation between self-assessed and adversarial-verified improvement.
