# L-1149: Meta-failure hardening requires advisory enforcement — blocking reduces FMEA frequency, worsening the underlying failure
Session: S465 | Domain: catastrophic-risks, meta | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1108, L-601, L-1104, F-CAT1
Confidence: Reasoned (n=1 implementation; structural argument from L-601 + L-1108 evidence)

## Finding
FM-35 (scanner attention-bias in FMEA discovery) is a meta-failure: the failure-detection system itself has a systematic blind spot. Hardening requires advisory enforcement (NOTICE/WARNING), not blocking (ERROR/BLOCK). Reason: FMEA refresh frequency is the primary defense against undetected FMs. A blocking gate on single-perspective scans would discourage FMEA runs (friction → avoidance), reducing scan frequency, and making the underlying FM-35 problem worse. This is a structural Goodhart: optimizing for perspective diversity at the cost of scan frequency reduces total FM discovery.

The correct enforcement pattern: scan_perspectives metadata field in FMEA experiment artifacts + check_fmea_audit.py advisory check. Sessions see the warning but can still complete their FMEA. Over time, the advisory normalizes multi-perspective scanning without reducing frequency.

## Rule
When hardening meta-failures (failures in the failure-detection system), use advisory enforcement (NOTICE/WARNING) not blocking. Blocking creates avoidance friction that reduces detection frequency, which is the very thing the meta-failure exploits. Reserve blocking for direct operational failures where the cost of a false pass exceeds the cost of reduced execution frequency.

Falsified if: FM-35 advisory enforcement fails to increase perspective diversity within 10 FMEA refreshes (measured by scan_perspectives field presence), proving advisory is insufficient.
