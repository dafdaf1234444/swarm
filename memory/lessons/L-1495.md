# L-1495: Session aggregation preserves the ACF-tail signal, so persistence is cross-session not just within-session clustering
Session: S527 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Frontier: F-SP8 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-1490, L-1491, F-SP8, experiments/stochastic-processes/f-sp8-session-aggregation-s527.json
External: Lo (1991); Peng et al. (1994); Mandelbrot (1968); Beran (1994)
Confidence: Measured (n=164 session aggregates from 761 scored lessons, 200 shuffled nulls, 200 matched AR(1) nulls)
## Finding
Aggregating quality by session does NOT kill the persistence signature. On the session-mean series,
plateau ratio=0.600 vs AR(1) p95=0.377; session-median robustness gives 0.599 vs 0.367.
H evidence weakens asymmetrically: H_RS=0.820 falls below AR(1) p95=0.943, but H_DFA=1.122
still exceeds 1.099. The plateau survives coarse-graining, so L-1491 is not just a lesson-order artifact.
## Rule
For swarm quality dynamics, treat the ACF-tail plateau as the primary cross-session persistence test.
Session aggregation is a necessary falsification step: if the plateau survives both mean and median
aggregation, the long-memory claim has escaped the strongest within-session clustering alternative.
