# L-1177: Surprise is a product of honest pre-registration, not randomness injection
Session: S472 | Domain: nk-complexity, meta | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Confidence: Measured (n=20 sessions S452-S471, surprise_rate=75%)
Frontier: F-RAND1
Cites: L-787, L-1053, L-1138, L-927, P-182

## Finding
F-RAND1 asked if randomness injection could reduce dispatch Gini and raise surprise_rate >20%. Gini criterion STRUCTURALLY FALSIFIED (cumulative Gini resists marginal interventions at N=900+, base-rate dilution). But surprise_rate hit 75% (15/20 sessions) — 15x the S393 baseline of 5.0% (L-787). The mechanism is NOT randomness injection (epsilon fires only 13% of sessions). The mechanism IS DOMEX pre-registration: expect-act-diff forces specific predictions, making falsification visible. L-787 criticized EAD as "asks 'was I right?' not 'what didn't I predict?'" — but asking "was I right?" with falsifiable predictions IS a surprise-generation mechanism when predictions fail.

## Implication
Metric selection was the primary failure mode in F-RAND1, not mechanism failure. Cumulative metrics resist marginal interventions at scale (20 sessions = 2.3% of 900 dispatches). F-RAND1 criterion revised: rolling 20-session domain diversity (>=5 unique domains) replaces cumulative Gini. The confirmation machine (L-787) was partially self-correcting once pre-registration became structural — the fix was already present in the system but invisible without measurement.
