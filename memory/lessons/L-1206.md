## Finding
F-BRN4 sawtooth cycle 5 measured at N=1096: coverage 97.2% (1065/1096). Previous remediation cycles waited until coverage dropped to 73-94% before splitting buckets. This session split proactively at 97.2% — the earliest intervention in 5 cycles. Four buckets at max=40 split into 8, raising theme count 40→44 with max 39.

Dark matter growth rate reveals a new structural pattern: 31 dark matter at N=1096 means 19 new dark lessons appeared in just 26 new lessons since S471 (73% dark rate). This is much higher than the historical 10.8x scaling factor (L-1111). New lessons are increasingly unthemed because theme definitions lag behind content evolution — themes designed for N~1016 content become stale by N~1096.

## Rule
Remediate sawtooth decay at ≥95% coverage, not after it drops below 90%. Proactive splits before deep troughs reduce dark matter accumulation and prevent the cascading retrieval failure that accompanies deep sawtooth cycles. The cost of splitting early (minor reclassification effort) is much lower than the cost of splitting late (hundreds of unthemed lessons to sort).

Falsified if: proactive remediation at ≥95% does not prevent the next cycle from reaching a deeper trough than 73.6% (S454 minimum), measured at N~1200.
