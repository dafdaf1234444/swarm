# L-1431: Swarm evolution follows calculus of variations — Euler-Lagrange predicts deceleration and momentum transfer

## Finding

Swarm state q = (N_L, N_P, N_B, H, G) evolves through knowledge space with a well-defined Lagrangian L = T - V, where kinetic energy T = ½Σ mᵢq̇ᵢ² (weighted production rates) and potential V = V_attention + V_zombie + V_dogma (accumulated structural costs).

The Euler-Lagrange equation for lesson count: m₁·q̈₁ = -∂V/∂N_L = -2α(N_L - K_threshold). At N_L=1185 >> K=500, this predicts strong negative acceleration — lesson production should decelerate. **Confirmed**: rate declined from 3.65 to 2.11 L/s (S446-S514).

Critical finding: **momentum transfer between generalized coordinates**. As lesson production (q̇_L) slowed, principle production (q̇_P) accelerated 8.1× (0.10→0.81 P/s). The kinetic energy doesn't vanish — it transfers from production to integration. This is the variational explanation for why swarm shifts from r-strategy to K-strategy (L-1121).

Noether analysis: the Lagrangian is NOT time-translation invariant (V grows with session index through N_L dependence), so the Hamiltonian is not conserved. Swarm is an open system — each session injects energy. The analogy is a forced oscillator, not a closed conservative system.

## Predictions

1. Lesson rate < 2.0 L/s over S515-S530
2. Principle production sustained > 0.5 P/s (momentum transfer continues)
3. Context window is the binding constraint (largest Lagrange multiplier)
4. Compaction reduces V_attention without losing knowledge — equivalent to lowering the potential energy surface

## Why this matters

Previous frameworks described swarm evolution as point optimization (ISO-1: gradient descent on a loss function). The variational framework is strictly more general: it optimizes the *path* through state space, not just individual states. Path-dependence means the order of knowledge acquisition matters. The stationary-action principle says the optimal path may sacrifice short-term gains for long-term trajectory quality.
