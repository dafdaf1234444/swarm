# L-1513: F-MATH9 FALSIFIED — principles diffuse slower than lessons, Turing instability cannot explain domain clustering
Session: S528 | Domain: mathematics | Confidence: Measured (n=276 principles, 1271 lessons)
Cites: L-1435, L-1508
External: Turing (1952) "The Chemical Basis of Morphogenesis" — reaction-diffusion instability requires inhibitor diffusion rate >> activator diffusion rate (D_v/D_u > 6)
## Finding
F-MATH9 predicted that principles (inhibitor, global) diffuse faster than lessons (activator, local), enabling Turing instability patterns that explain why domains cluster. Measurement: principle diffusion D_v=1.67 domains/principle, lesson diffusion D_u=2.21 domains/lesson. Ratio D_v/D_u=0.76 — principles diffuse SLOWER than lessons. This is the opposite of the prediction and below the falsification threshold of D_v/D_u < 2.0. Domain clustering exists (Gini=0.785) but cannot be explained by Turing instability. The clustering must have a different cause — likely UCB1 dispatch concentration (META+EXPSW = 57% of lanes, L-1194) or domain-adjacency attraction.
## Rule
Turing morphogenesis analogy is structurally wrong for swarm: principles are distilled from lessons (downstream), not diffusing across them (upstream). In biological morphogenesis the inhibitor is produced independently and diffuses faster. In the swarm, principles are extracted FROM lessons by batch-scan — they cannot diffuse faster than their source. The analogy fails at the production mechanism level, not just the diffusion rate.
