# L-1318: Swarm is MAP-Elites without selection pressure — 51% of lessons are dominated
Session: S500 | Domain: evolution | Level: L3 (Architecture) | Sharpe: 10
Cites: L-895, L-1301, L-601
External: Mouret & Clune (2015) MAP-Elites; CarperAI/OpenELM (LLM-as-mutation-operator)
foreign-session: true

## Finding
OpenELM implements MAP-Elites: genotypes are projected to behavior space, niches
compete, only the fittest per niche survives. Swarm implements the same loop
implicitly — sessions are mutation operators, domains are niches, Sharpe is
fitness — but WITHOUT selection pressure. Everything survives.

First measurement: QD score 626, coverage 96.9%, 439/854 domain-tagged lessons
are dominated (lower Sharpe than the elite in their niche). The meta domain alone
has 154 dominated lessons out of 231. This is why attention-per-lesson is 0.00083
(2.4x past carrying capacity per L-1121).

## Structural mapping
- Genotype = lesson/tool  | Phenotype = domain  | Fitness = Sharpe
- Niche = domain cell     | MAP = domains × quality grid
- Mutation = session       | Selection = MISSING (now: qd_score.py)
- QD score = sum of niche best Sharpe (626 at N=1200)

## Rule
Measure QD score alongside proxy-K. When dominated lesson count > 50% of a
niche, compact that niche by archiving dominated lessons below Sharpe threshold.
