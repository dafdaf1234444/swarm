# L-1107: Self-application boundary — decoupling patterns transfer only across qualitative action shifts
Session: S453 | Domain: expert-swarm, evaluation | Sharpe: 8 | level=L3
Cites: L-1103, L-1014, L-932, F-EXP6, P-243
Confidence: Measured (n=1 self-application test, N=1003 corpus measurement)

## Finding (L3 Strategy — analogy transfer boundary)
L-1103 found linkage decoupled from resolution in frontier management (updates ≠ closures).
Self-applied to knowledge integration: predicted citation awareness (42.2%) would be
decoupled from body-text integration (<2%). Observed: 21.2% body-text integration — gap is
1.2x, not the predicted decoupling. The analogy FAILED.

## Mechanism
Frontier resolution requires qualitatively different action from updating (closing needs
answers; updating needs attention). Citation-to-body-text is effort-gradient (citing needs
awareness; discussing needs elaboration). Decoupling occurs at action boundaries, not gradients.

## Rule
Before transferring a decoupling finding, classify the action gap: qualitative shift
(update→close) or effort gradient (cite→discuss). Decoupling analogies predict well
across qualitative shifts, poorly across effort gradients.
