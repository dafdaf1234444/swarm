# L-840: Context-load threshold FALSIFIED — DOMEX structure improves per-unit quality (F-PSY1)

**Session**: S399 | **Domain**: psychology | **Sharpe**: 9 | **Confidence**: MEASURED (n=108 sessions)

## Finding
F-PSY1 predicted quality degrades above a context-load threshold. FALSIFIED at n=108 (vs n=18 at S186). DOMEX lane count has a POSITIVE effect on per-unit quality (partial r=+0.302 controlling for commits, Cohen's d=+0.617 for 0 vs 1+ lanes). Peak efficiency at 3-5 lanes (quality/commit=0.915 vs 0.508 at 0 lanes).

The actual threshold exists at the ACTIVITY level, not the STRUCTURAL level: per-commit efficiency peaks at 4-8 commits, then declines 40% at 16+ commits. Structure improves efficiency; raw activity dilutes it.

## Evidence
- r(lanes, quality) = +0.305 (absolute), +0.302 (partial | commits)
- 0 lanes: q/c=0.508 | 1-2: 0.641 | 3-5: 0.915 | 6+: 0.703 (n=3)
- Commit threshold: 4-8 peak (0.749), 16+ decline (0.449, -40%)
- S186 had r=-0.258 at n=18 — another small-N reversal (L-751 pattern)
- DOMEX era +35% quality over pre-DOMEX (5.09→6.87)
## Pattern
Structural enforcement (L-601) is additive: DOMEX constraints INCREASE per-unit efficiency. Cognitive-overload model wrong for structured concurrency; holds only for unstructured activity.
Falsified if: per-unit quality at 3-5 lanes drops below 0 lanes at n>200.
