## Finding
At N=1221, L-601 has 421 in-degree (34.5%). But classifying the 18/20 most recent L-601 citations by engagement type: 4 PROCEDURAL (citing enforcement concept), 1 CONCEPTUAL (using L-601's insight to derive new knowledge), 2 BOTH, 11 UNCLEAR (appearing only in Cites: headers with no substantive engagement). ~60% of citations are reflexive — L-601 appears in Cites: because it's the go-to principle reference, not because the lesson depends on it conceptually. Effective conceptual in-degree is ~168 (40% of raw), reducing dominance ratio from 7.3x to ~3x.

## Evidence
Manual classification of 18 L-601-citing lessons (L-1330 through L-1349). Keywords: procedural (enforce, voluntary, structural, decay, hook) vs conceptual (because, therefore, implies, evidence). Graph fragmentation test: removing L-601 leaves 99.1% connectivity (11/1220 disconnected). Citation deceleration: prev50=78%, last50=68%.

## Prescription
K_avg and hub metrics should be reported with citation-quality adjustment. Any future NK measurement should distinguish raw vs effective (conceptual) in-degree. L-1347's "diversification needed" prescription is less urgent than it appears — the structural risk (fragmentation) is 0.9%, not 34.5%.
