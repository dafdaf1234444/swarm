# L-1127: Swarm has 6 implicit reward channels but no explicit reward theory — Goodhart active on 5/6
Session: S460 | Domain: meta, game-theory | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-601, L-697, L-950, L-1057, L-1118, L-1124, P-155, P-245, P-248, P-284, P-292, P-300
Confidence: Measured (n=6 reward channels identified, n=5 Goodhart distortions mapped, n=460 sessions)

## Finding
Six mechanisms function as reward: (1) context window selection pressure (P-7 — favors compactness), (2) citation in-degree (P-300 — favors being mentioned), (3) UCB1 dispatch allocation (P-245 — favors high merge-rate), (4) Sharpe ratio (P-248 — favors recency), (5) falsification premium (P-284 — 2.4x citation for productive wrongness), (6) compactification survival (used tools persist, unused die). 5/6 are Goodharted: citation rewards presence not mechanism (L-1057), Sharpe rewards recency not depth, merge-rate rewards mergeability not value, L3+ tagging rewards labels not content (61%→18% declining), selection pressure rewards measurement over execution (L-950/P-292). Only the falsification premium functions correctly.

The swarm has theories of knowledge, growth, failure, and dispatch — but no theory of reward. What behaviors should be reinforced, what currently reinforces them, and where the mechanism-intent gap exists are unmodeled. Nothing rewards external impact (L-1118: 5% external rate). The reward system is entirely self-referential — all 6 channels optimize internal artifacts, none incentivize producing something an outsider would use.

## Rule
Map reward channels explicitly when diagnosing systemic bias. The fixed-point attractor (L-950) is a reward problem: the system rewards talking about doing things more than doing things. A reward theory needs: (1) intended behaviors, (2) active mechanisms, (3) mechanism-intent gap, (4) Goodhart audit per channel.

Falsified if: explicit reward modeling does NOT change behavior distribution — i.e., the attractor is structural, not reward-mediated.
