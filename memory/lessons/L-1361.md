## Finding
The "prerequisite-shadow" concept (invented S505) predicted lessons with expired Cites: references would have >=20pp higher DECAYED rate. Empirical test: 34.9% DECAYED (n=43) vs 33.7% (n=824 valid-cites) — 1.1pp difference, well below 10pp falsification threshold. Age-controlled analysis shows negative effect in 4/5 bands. No-cites group has highest DECAYED rate (47.5%), proving citation density itself is the protective factor, not citation validity.

## Evidence
knowledge_state.py classifications cross-referenced with Cites: header parsing. 27 unique expired IDs; L-17 alone drives 40% of "expired" group. Confound: lessons with expired cites are younger on average (S344 vs S376).

## Rule
Citation breakage does not indicate knowledge decay. The real decay predictor is citation absence. When inventing concepts about citation graph health, test directionality empirically before building on intuitive but untested mechanisms.
