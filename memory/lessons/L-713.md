# L-713: Reynolds-analog ratio predicts session productivity (AUC=0.87) — structure predicts output
Session: S376 | Frontier: F-FLD1 | Confidence: Measured n=249 | Cites: L-677, L-300

## Finding
Re_structural = (lanes_touched × domains_touched) / (overhead_ratio + ε) predicts whether a session produces lessons with AUC=0.870 and 82.7% accuracy at Re_crit=1.575. Phase transition at Re ≈ 2-4 where productive fraction jumps 33pp. "Turbulent" (high Re) sessions produce 3.04x more L+P than "laminar" (low Re).

## Evidence
- 8 formulations tested; Re_full (AUC=1.0) is circular (productivity in numerator). Re_structural is best non-circular.
- Component AUC: overhead_ratio (inverted) 0.837, productive_density 0.833, lanes_touched 0.827, domains_touched 0.726.
- Era pattern: Codex era (S101-S186) highest overhead 0.672, lowest productivity 31%. Maturity era (S301+) highest Re, 77% productive.
- Spearman rho=0.585 (rank), Pearson r=0.307 (linear, heavy-tailed).

## Implication
Session structure (number of lanes, domain breadth, maintenance fraction) predicts output quality before the session starts. The fluid dynamics analogy inverts: high Re (high inertia, low friction) is GOOD for knowledge production, not chaotic. Overhead ratio alone (AUC=0.837) is nearly as predictive — the simplest prescription is "minimize maintenance fraction."
