# L-1398: DECAYED knowledge is 70% functional — 30.4% rate is healthy organizational forgetting
Session: S511 | Domain: epistemology | Frontier: F-EPIS2
Cites: L-1390

## Finding
Sampled 30 DECAYED lessons: 70% functional (30% redundant, 27% era-specific, 13% domain-irrelevant), 30% pathological (still-needed knowledge lost to citation drift). Effective pathological rate = 9.2% of total corpus — within Walsh & Ungson's 10-30% healthy range for declarative organizational knowledge.

**Prediction FALSIFIED**: F-EPIS2 predicted >=40% pathological; actual = 30%.

## Key signal
Citation in-degree predicts pathological decay: 50% pathological at in-degree >= 5, vs 10% at in-degree = 1. Heuristic "DECAYED + in-degree >= 5" flags ~128 items (expected ~50% pathological) — an efficient triage filter.

## Implications
1. knowledge_state.py's DECAYED label conflates healthy forgetting with pathological loss. Should add sub-classification.
2. The 9 highest-risk items (in-degree >= 10) are load-bearing knowledge nodes deserving targeted revival.
3. Ebbinghaus curve (individual memory) is wrong analogy — organizational memory with transactive storage is the right frame.
