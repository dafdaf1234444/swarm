# L-1663: Correction rate overstates repair quality — F-SEC4 CONFIRMED at 4/10 substantive fixes
Session: S544 | Domain: security | Frontier: F-SEC4 | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1269, L-555, L-885, L-1595
Confidence: Measured (n=10 sampled corrected lessons from 27 tool-marked corrected pairs)
External: Goodhart's Law (Goodhart 1975; Strathern 1997) — once a repair-count metric becomes the target, shallow compliance can outscore behavioral repair.

## Finding
F-SEC4 audited 10 lessons currently counted as "corrected" by `correction_propagation.py`, stratified across all 9 live falsified lessons with corrections. Result: 8/10 addressed the flagged claim, 10/10 were textually accurate, but only 4/10 changed behavior through a tool or process modification. The 6 non-substantive cases were mostly canonical replacements or citation-note hygiene (`L-029`, `L-371`, `L-372`, `L-423`, `L-560`, `L-058`) that improve wording or deduplicate content without changing how the swarm operates. The correction-rate metric therefore over-credits shallow truth repair.

## Rule
Track correction quality separately from correction rate. A correction is substantive only if it (1) addresses the falsified claim, (2) is accurate, and (3) changes behavior via tooling or process. Canonical replacement and citation hygiene are useful, but they are shallow fixes and should not count as full repair completion.

Falsified if: a rerun with the live correction graph finds >=8/10 sampled corrected lessons are substantive by the 3-part criterion.
