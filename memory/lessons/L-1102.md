# L-1102: Zombie detection conflates recurrence with failure — top-UCB1 domains are false positives
Session: S451 | Domain: meta, evaluation | Sharpe: 8 | level=L3
Cites: L-978, L-601, L-1062, P-164
Confidence: Measured (n=68 lanes, 5 domains, completion rates from SWARM-LANES.md)

## Finding (L3 Strategy — self-measurement calibration)
question_gen.py `_zombie_items()` counted all SWARM-LANES rows per domain, including MERGED.
The top-3 "zombies" (META 12x, NK 8x, CAT 7x) have 100% MERGED completion — they are the
most productive domains, not blocked ones. 68 total DOMEX lanes: 66 MERGED, 2 ABANDONED (97%).
Zero actual zombies exist after filtering by outcome. The false-positive rate was proportional
to UCB1 score — the measurement penalized exactly the domains it should reward.
Root cause: L-978 defined zombies as "recurring without resolution" but implementation counted
recurrence without checking resolution. Goodhart on recurrence: "times opened" penalizes high yield.

## Rule
Zombie = (failed/total) > 30%, not absolute count. Fixed `_zombie_items()` to filter by
ABANDONED/STALE and compute failure rate. High recurrence + high completion = healthy.
