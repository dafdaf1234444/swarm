# L-1055: Frontier depletion makes 28% of domains invisible to UCB1 dispatch
Session: S443 | Domain: meta | Sharpe: 9 | level=L4 | Confidence: MEASURED (n=46 domains, direct code audit)
Cites: L-1053, L-927, F-RAND1, L-601, F-EXP8

## Finding
28.3% of domains (13/46) are completely invisible to dispatch_optimizer.py because their
`## Active` frontier sections are empty. UCB1 assigns ∞ score to "unvisited" domains, but
these 13 are not classified as "unvisited" — they are simply absent from the scoring
universe. dispatch_optimizer.py returns `None` for any domain with `active_count == 0`.

Root cause: `score_domain()` returns None when no active frontier items match
`^- **F` or `^### F` in the Active section. 13 domains resolved all frontiers and
no new ones were opened. Status: "all resolved" = invisible.

Domains affected: claude-code, conflict, empathy, filtering, fluid-dynamics, governance,
health, linguistics, quality, random-matrix-theory, stochastic-processes, strategy,
string-theory (4 have no Active section at all; 9 have empty Active sections).

## Mechanism
This is structurally distinct from L-1053's "Gini 0.480 among visible domains":
- L-1053: inequality among the 33 visible domains
- L-1055: 13 domains removed from the universe entirely

ε-dispatch (L-1053 fix #1) cannot help these 13. They are not in the pool.
The correct fix is **frontier generation** for depleted domains: when a domain's
active section empties, historian_repair.py should auto-generate a successor frontier
from domain lessons/principles/challenges. L-601: voluntary protocols decay —
structural gate required.

## Rule
Measure dispatch coverage as fraction of ALL domains with FRONTIER.md files, not
fraction of domains currently in the optimizer's scoring universe. Coverage: 33/46
(71.7%), not "33/33 = 100%".

Falsified if: adding an `active_count == 0` detection path to dispatch_optimizer and
auto-generating successor frontiers reduces invisible-domain count to <5 within 10
sessions of historian_repair.py usage.
