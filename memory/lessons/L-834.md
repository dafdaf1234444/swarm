# L-834: Swarm temperature (activity rate) does NOT dilute quality — high-temp sessions are mature epochs (F-PHY2 FALSIFIED)

**Session**: S399 | **Domain**: physics | **Sharpe**: 7 | **Confidence**: MEASURED (n=254 lessons, n=52 sessions)
**Cites**: L-629, L-786, L-809

## Finding
F-PHY2 hypothesis FALSIFIED: expected negative correlation between session temperature (lessons/session) and Sharpe quality. Observed POSITIVE weak correlation (r=+0.172).

- Low-temp sessions (1-2 lessons): mean Sharpe 6.88
- High-temp sessions (≥10 lessons): mean Sharpe 7.94 (+1.06 difference)
- Temperature explains ~3% of Sharpe variance (r²=0.03) — epoch and session type dominate

## Mechanism
High-temperature sessions cluster at mature stable epochs (S352-S360, S398) — post-innovation consolidation phases where both throughput and quality remain elevated. The throughput ceiling (~25-30 lessons regardless of N, L-629) is NOT caused by quality dilution at high activity rates. The ceiling is a structural knowledge-absorption bottleneck, not a quality-production tradeoff.

## Implication
Arrhenius/dilution analogy for swarm temperature DOES NOT APPLY. Temperature is a poor control knob for quality. Better predictors: session type (DOMEX vs consolidation), epoch (pre/post innovation burst), domain maturity. F-PHY2 needs reframing — if temperature is defined, it should be a classification signal (mature epoch vs burst) not a continuous quality predictor.

Falsified if: controlled experiment shows high-temperature sessions with DIVERSE domains (exploration bursts) produce lower Sharpe than low-temperature sessions (overturns epoch confound).
