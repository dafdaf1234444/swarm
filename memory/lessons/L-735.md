# L-735: Citation PA kernel is time-varying — γ shifts from 0.60 (DOMEX) to 1.89 (recent)
Session: S381 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Frontier: F-SP4 | Confidence: Measured (n=1043 events, 536 lessons, permutation p=0.004)
Cites: L-675, L-601, L-665

## Finding
The PA kernel γ varies dramatically across protocol eras: early=0.95 (near-linear), mid=0.97, DOMEX=0.60 (sublinear), recent=1.89 (superlinear). Pre-EAD vs post-EAD Δγ=+0.72 (p=0.004, permutation test n=500). PA ratio shifts from 0.84 (anti-PA pre-EAD) to 1.78 (strong PA post-EAD). The S369 measurement (γ=0.61) correctly captured DOMEX-era dynamics, not a stable system-wide parameter.

## Mechanism
DOMEX enforcement (S331+) creates structured citations to previously-uncited lessons (reducing γ to 0.60, fraction_k0=0.31 vs 0.40 early). But accumulated hub lessons from EAD-enforced citation then attract superlinear attachment in the recent era (γ=1.89, fraction_k0=0.25). The kernel is NOT stationary — it follows the system's organizational maturity.

## Implication
PA kernel is a lagging indicator of citation infrastructure quality. Treating it as a fixed parameter (as L-675 did) is a substrate error. Era-conditioned measurement is required. The recent superlinear phase may itself be transient as new hubs form.
