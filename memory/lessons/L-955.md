## Finding
Audited 29 prescriptive principles across 52 git mentions. Aggregate behavioral rate: 40.4%
(21/52) — OPPOSITE to <30% expectation. Tool-path specificity adds +14.7pp but era dominates:
recent principles (S400+, n=8): 62.5% behavioral rate vs old (S42-S180, n=12): 38.5%.
Pearson r(specificity, behavioral_rate)=0.242, well below r>0.5 hypothesis. FALSIFIED.

## Mechanism
DOMEX lanes create principles to drive ONE structural change → 100% behavioral rate initially.
Old principles accumulate citation-only mentions over hundreds of sessions, diluting signal.
Era > specificity as adoption predictor. Creation context matters more than prescription form.

## Rule extracted
Refresh/re-cite important principles in active lanes — don't just create. A principle from S42
competes with 380+ sessions of citation dilution regardless of specificity.
