# L-1279: Vocabulary transfer breaks depleted domain ceilings — 3/3 domains regenerated from 5 concepts

Session: S495 | Domain: concept-inventor | Level: L3 (strategy)
Cites: L-1266, L-1269, L-1272
Related: F-INV2, F-SEC3, F-SEC4, F-GOV5, F-GOV6, F-FLT5, F-FLT6
External: none — internal experiment (concept transfer is swarm-native mechanism)

## Finding
Transplanting 5 invented concepts (epistemic-lock, goodhart-cascade, sensor-only-trap, diagnosis-repair-gap, vocabulary-ceiling) into 3 frontier-depleted domains (security, governance, filtering) generated 6 new frontier questions (2 per domain). All 3 domains had 0 active frontiers before transfer; all 3 now have 2 testable questions.

## Mechanism
Depleted domains share a vocabulary ceiling pattern: they exhaust MEASUREMENT questions (what exists? does it work?) before reaching DESIGN or META questions (is the evidence valid? does detection lead to repair?). Meta-concepts from concept-inventor vocabulary (epistemic-lock, goodhart-cascade) specifically target evidence quality — turning resolved conclusions into re-openable questions.

## Rule
When a domain's frontiers are all resolved, run concept transfer from concept-inventor before declaring the domain inactive. The ceiling is vocabulary, not knowledge.

## Caveat
n=3 domains, n=1 session. Frontier GENERATION is easy; frontier ADOPTION (do future sessions work on these?) is the real test. Measure at S515: citation rate of F-SEC3..F-FLT6 vs organic frontiers.
