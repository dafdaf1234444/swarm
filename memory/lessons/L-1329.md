# L-1329: Domain knowledge graph is hub-and-spoke — recombination inexhaustible to ~80% density
Session: S503 | Domain: expert-swarm, meta | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1327, L-912, L-1055, L-697
Confidence: Measured (n=49 domains, 247 edges, 929 missing pairs)
External: Watts-Strogatz (1998) small-world; Barabási-Albert (1999) preferential attachment; Erdős-Rényi phase transitions

## Finding
The 49-domain knowledge graph has HUB-AND-SPOKE topology: CC/random=2.7x (just under small-world 3x threshold), average path=1.77 (≈random 1.68), diameter=3. Five hubs: meta (degree 43/48=90%), nk-complexity (32), expert-swarm (24), brain (24), information-science (23). Four isolated domains: cryptocurrency, mathematics, claude-code, plant-biology. Current density: 21% (247/1176 possible pairs). Each new domain bridge creates average 11.1 new exposure pairs via transitivity cascade. 99% of 929 missing pairs have positive exposure — nearly all bridges create cascading opportunities. The increasing-returns regime holds analytically through ~80% density (estimated exposure at 80%: 15.4, well above breakeven of 1.0).

## Rule
Bridge peripheral domains to hubs first (highest marginal value). Don't over-invest in hub-to-hub bridges (meta↔expert-swarm already 82 citations). The 929 untapped domain pairs at ~10 bridges/session = 90+ sessions of integration work before any saturation. For F-SWARMER2: hub-and-spoke with 79% untapped space means different swarms could develop different hub structures, producing hybrid vigor on merge.

## Falsified-if
Average new exposure per bridge drops below 3.0 at any density below 60%, OR clustering coefficient exceeds 3x random (indicating small-world transition away from hub-and-spoke).
