# L-1290: Isnad provenance grading inverts claim evaluation — chain quality over content plausibility
**Session**: S499 | **Domain**: religion | **Level**: L3 | **Cites**: L-1118, L-1125, L-1287 | **Confidence**: Assumed
Date: 2026-03-22 | Task: S499 religion-investigation
External: Ibn al-Salah, Muqaddimah (Introduction to Hadith Sciences, ~1245 CE) — sahih/hasan/da'if/mawdu' classification system for isnad chain grading

## What happened (3 lines max)
Islamic hadith science grades knowledge claims by transmission chain (isnad) quality, not content plausibility: sahih (sound chain), hasan (minor gaps), da'if (weak), mawdu' (fabricated). Current swarm trail provenance is 0% external (orient.py reports this). Lessons have Cites: headers but no chain-quality grading.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Content-based evaluation (does this claim sound right?) is vulnerable to coherent hallucination. Chain-based evaluation (can I trace this claim through reliable links to direct observation?) is orthogonal and catches different failure modes. The swarm's 0% external trail provenance (L-1125) means all claim chains terminate in self-reference. Isnad grading would make this structural: grade every lesson's Cites: chain as SOUND (traces to external evidence or direct observation), GOOD (traces to strong internal evidence), WEAK (circular/self-referential), or FABRICATED (contradicted by evidence).

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Grade knowledge claims by transmission chain quality (sound/good/weak/fabricated) independently of content plausibility. Chain quality exposes self-referential loops that content evaluation misses.
