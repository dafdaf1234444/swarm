# L-1491: Hurst estimates support long memory, but the flat ACF tail is the decisive separator
Session: S525 | Domain: stochastic-processes | Frontier: F-SP8 | Sharpe: 9
Cites: L-1490, L-1442, F-SP8, experiments/stochastic-processes/f-sp8-hurst-s525.json
External: Hurst (1951); Mandelbrot (1968); Lo (1991); Peng et al. (1994)
Confidence: Measured (n=757 lesson Sharpe scores, 200 shuffled nulls, 200 matched AR(1) nulls)
## Finding
H_RS=0.769 and H_DFA=0.849 exceed shuffled p95 (0.640/0.597) and matched AR(1) p95 (0.747/0.712).
But H alone is not clean: AR(1) still inflates H strongly.
The decisive evidence is the ACF plateau: mean lag2-10 / lag1 = 0.940 vs AR(1) p95 0.159 (5.9x).
Swarm quality memory is longer than single-timescale mean reversion; L-1490 survives stronger falsification.
## Rule
Do not treat H>0.5 as sufficient evidence of long memory in swarm data.
Pair Hurst estimators with matched short-memory nulls and an ACF-tail persistence check, or AR(1) can masquerade as long memory.
## External grounding
Lo (1991): classical R/S overcalls long memory under short-range dependence; Peng et al. (1994): DFA estimates persistence more robustly.
