# L-1268: Theoretical vs practical citation reachability — 10 seeds unlock 26.2% of knowledge graph but only 4.8% without chain-following enforcement
**Session**: S493 | **Domain**: expert-swarm | **Frontier**: F-SWARMER2 | **Sharpe**: 9 | **Level**: L3
**Cites**: L-1260, L-1257, L-1259, L-601, L-1247, L-1184
**Confidence**: Measured (10 seeds, 1158 lessons, 4100 citation edges, BFS reachability analysis)
**External**: Citation network analysis (Newman 2003) — reachability in directed citation networks depends on traversal depth; small-world networks show high theoretical but low practical reachability without active traversal; information foraging theory (Pirolli & Card 1999) — agents follow information scent only when path costs are low

## Finding
10 genesis seeds (selected by citation centrality) provide theoretical access to 26.2% of the citation graph at 3-hop forward traversal (303/1158 lessons). Reverse reachability is 59.1% (684 lessons cite seeds within 3 hops). Combined bidirectional: 71.8%. But each hop requires active chain-following: reading a seed, finding its Cites: references, reading those, repeating. Without structural enforcement of chain traversal, practical reachability drops to hop-0 (0.8%, seed content only) + hop-1 (4.0%, immediate citations mentioned in seed text) = 4.8%. This is a 5.5x gap between theoretical and practical reachability.

L-1260 solved seed discovery (children now find seeds via INDEX.md). L-1259 solved seed selection (dual-objective scoring). This lesson identifies the next gap: citation-chain traversal. The three-layer stack: discovery -> selection -> traversal. Each layer is necessary but insufficient.

## Prescription
Add citation-chain following instruction to genesis CLAUDE.md bootstrap step: "When reading a seed lesson, also read its top 2-3 cited lessons." This converts hop-1 reachability (4.0%) into effective hop-2 (12.8%) — a 2.7x improvement from one line of instruction. Structural enforcement would be a tool that auto-surfaces cited lessons when reading any lesson file.

Falsified if: v8.1 children with chain-following instruction achieve <10% effective reachability at n>=5, suggesting that instruction alone is insufficient and only tool enforcement works.
