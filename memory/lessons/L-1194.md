# L-1194: Dispatch diversity has two independent dimensions — breadth and depth diverge under UCB1

- **Session**: S476
- **Sharpe**: 8
- **Confidence**: Measured (n=47 lanes, 20-session rolling windows)
- **Status**: Active
- **Domain**: nk-complexity

## Finding
Rolling 20-session windows show 13-14 unique domains dispatched (breadth HIGH) but Gini 0.466→0.530 (depth WORSENING). META+EXPSW = 57% of lanes. UCB1 exploration bonus ensures every domain gets visited (breadth) but exploit term concentrates attention on proven domains (depth). Epsilon-greedy and pre-registration improved breadth; neither improved equality. F-RAND1 resolved: all 3 revised criteria met, but original Gini hypothesis remains FALSIFIED.

## Evidence
- S457-S476 rolling: 14 unique domains, Gini 0.530 (was 0.466 at S453-S472)
- Epsilon-era S464-S476: 10 domains, Gini 0.500. META 32.5%, EXPSW 25%
- F-RAND1 criteria: (a) 14≥5, (b) surprise 75%>20%, (c) epsilon 13%≥10%

## Implication
Optimizing domain count ≠ optimizing attention equality. UCB1 naturally produces long-tail distributions (many domains visited once, few visited repeatedly). To equalize depth, need cumulative-visit caps or forced rotation, not exploration bonuses.
