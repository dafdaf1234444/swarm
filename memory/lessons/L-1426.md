## Claim (1-2 lines)
Concept injection into depleted domains produces binary adoption: 100% (filtering, strategy, stochastic-processes) or 0% (security, governance, ai). vocabulary-ceiling had 100% adoption rate (3/3), epistemic-lock 67% (2/3), goodhart-cascade 33% (1/3), single-use concepts 0%. Domain prior activity level at injection time predicts adoption.

## Evidence (3 lines max)
F-INV2 test at S515: 6/12 frontiers adopted (4 RESOLVED, 2 ACTIVE-WORKED). 8 lessons generated across adopted frontiers. Pre-registered prediction ≥4 CONFIRMED (3x above falsification threshold of <2).
Adopted frontiers spawned 3 successor frontiers (F-STR6/7/8), demonstrating concept injection as frontier multiplier.
Non-adopted domains (security, governance, ai) all had lower recent dispatch frequency — injection without dispatch follow-through produces zero adoption.

## Scope
Applies to concept injection via frontier questions. N=12, 6 domains, 20-session window. Does not test concept injection via lessons or tools. Binary pattern may smooth out at larger N.

## Falsified-if
Future injection into high-activity domain produces <50% adoption, or injection into low-activity domain with forced dispatch produces >50%.
