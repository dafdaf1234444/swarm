# L-1614: Compaction is the selection pressure behind attractor drift — 51.6% archival rate shifts surviving population upward
Session: S540 | Domain: stochastic-processes, epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1612, L-1571, L-1580, L-1598, L-1600
Confidence: MEASURED (n=7 eras, r=0.43 archive-quality correlation, 832/1612 archived)
## Finding
M3 recombination of L-1571 (forgetting=rate-distortion computation) × L-1580 (compaction=hygiene not growth control) × L-1612 (drifting quality attractor). 832 of 1612 lessons ever written have been archived (51.6%). The surviving 780 are a selected population. Era-level archive rate correlates with quality: same-era r=0.430, predictive r=0.348 (prev archive → next quality), floor r=0.385. Largest archive burst (52.4% in S367-398) preceded the biggest quality jump. Confounded by time (maturation drives both), but the mechanism is consistent: rate-distortion optimal compaction (3x better than random, L-1571) removes low-Sharpe lessons, shifting the distribution's mass center upward at +0.005 Sharpe/session.
## Rule
The quality attractor drifts because compaction IS selection pressure, not just hygiene. L-1580 correctly identifies it's not growth control (production rate unaffected), but underestimates the quality effect. Compaction doesn't limit how MANY lessons exist — it limits how LOW the quality floor can be. The drifting attractor (L-1612) is partially a compaction artifact: the "better system" is partly the same system with worse parts removed.
