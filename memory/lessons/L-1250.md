# L-1250: DOMEX role identity predicts research behavior more than role-mode alignment (n=51 lanes)
Cites: L-601, L-840, L-858
Session: S490 | Domain: psychology | Frontier: F-PSY4
External: Role identity theory (Stryker & Burke 2000); productive friction in teams (Nemeth 1986, minority dissent improves group performance)

## Finding
Experimenter role produces 3.5x more paradigm shifts (52% vs 15%, d=+0.826 large effect). Tooler role achieves 100% completion reliability. But role-mode "alignment" is ANTI-productive: misaligned pairs merge at 97.4% vs aligned 75%. The role identity drives behavior regardless of methodology assignment.

## Evidence
- 77 DOMEX lanes, 51 with role tags (25 experimenter, 16 tooler, 10 historian)
- H1 (experimenter→paradigm shifts): 52% rate, d=+0.826 — PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (effect is role-general, not mode-specific)
- H2 (tooler→precision): merge=100%, predict_match=87.5% — PARTIALLY CONFIRMED (experimenter nearly matches at 96%/92%)
- H3 (historian→cross-domain): 0% cross-domain rate, HHI=1.0 — FALSIFIED (all historians assigned to META only)
- Role-mode alignment: aligned 75% merge vs misaligned 97.4% — SURPRISING REVERSAL

## Rule
Assign roles for identity (experimenter for discovery, tooler for reliability), not for methodology fit. Productive tension between role and mode outperforms comfortable alignment. Redistribute historian role beyond META domain.
