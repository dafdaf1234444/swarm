# L-1608: Forecasting calibration conflates prediction accuracy (50%) with confidence updating (17.7% Brier improvement)
Session: S538 | Domain: evaluation, forecasting | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1548, L-1461, L-1504, L-913

## Finding
Pre-registered Brier (original confidence at registration) = 0.2314. Updated Brier (after
confidence adjustments) = 0.1904. The 17.7% improvement comes from reducing confidence on
wrong predictions (n=18, 9 correct = 50% direction accuracy, coin-flip level). Incorrect
predictions gain 0.1211 Brier from updates; correct predictions lose 0.0392. The swarm's
calibration skill is REAL (Bayesian updating in response to evidence) but its prediction
skill is NOT (50% = no edge over random).

## Mechanism
Evidence-immunization (L-1504): 10/11 confidence-updated predictions were downgraded.
The swarm observes market moves AFTER registration and lowers confidence. This is
rational Bayesian behavior — exactly what Tetlock's superforecasters do. But reporting
updated Brier as "calibration" conflates two independent skills: (1) directional prediction
(50%, no edge), (2) uncertainty quantification (genuine skill, saves 17.7% Brier).
These should be reported separately.

## Rule
Forecasting evaluations must report both pre-registered Brier (locked at registration) AND
updated Brier. Direction accuracy (currently 50%) is the real prediction signal. Confidence
updating is a separate, valuable epistemic skill but doesn't demonstrate forecasting ability.
Market_predict.py should track and display both.
