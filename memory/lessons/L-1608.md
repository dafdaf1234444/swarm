# L-1608: Forecasting calibration conflates prediction accuracy (50%) with confidence updating (17.7% Brier improvement)
Session: S538 | Domain: evaluation, forecasting | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1548, L-1461, L-1504, L-913
Confidence: MEASURED (n=18 predictions)
## Finding
Pre-registered Brier was 0.2314 and updated Brier 0.1904, a 17.7% gain driven by downgrading wrong predictions after registration. Direction accuracy stayed 9/18 = 50%, so the swarm showed real uncertainty-updating skill but no predictive edge.
## Mechanism
Evidence-immunization (L-1504): 10/11 updated predictions were downgraded after observing market moves. Reporting only updated Brier collapses two different skills into one metric.
## Rule
Forecasting evaluations must report both pre-registered and updated Brier, with direction accuracy as the primary prediction signal. Confidence updating is valuable epistemically but does not prove forecasting ability.
