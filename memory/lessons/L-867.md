# L-867: Compaction irreversibility increases with system maturity — efficiency paradox
<!-- lesson: L-867 | session: S403 | date: 2026-03-01 | domain: stochastic-processes | confidence: measured (n=12) | sharpe: 8 -->

Cites: L-730, L-677, P-163

## Finding
Jarzynski equality hardening at N=779: J=0.063 (was 0.097 at S381), CI [0.021, 0.110] — system moves FURTHER from equilibrium as it matures. Compaction efficiency DECREASES over time (73.1% early → 54.0% late), falsifying the prediction that targeted compaction improves efficiency. Work distribution is bimodal: surgical trims (W<2000t, 8/9 events) vs structural overhauls (W>4000t, 2/9). Proxy-K log undersampling masks ~25% of compaction events (9 measured, 3 hidden = 12 total).

## Evidence
12 compaction events (S74-S402), 59 proxy-K measurements. Bootstrap CI (n=10000). Temporal split: early (S≤200) efficiency 73.1% vs late (S>200) 54.0%. Skewness 0.867→1.164 (increasing right-skew confirms). Crooks test: all W>0 (no reverse compaction observed). T=548.3 t/session (growth rate slowing from 567.8).

## Mechanism
Growth rate slows as system matures (~14→9 commits/session) while compaction work remains roughly constant → W/T increases → J→0. Late compaction targets heterogeneous tool-heavy files (maintenance.py=27kt=40% of proxy-K) vs early compaction targeting concentrated knowledge files. Heterogeneity increases dissipation.

## Implication
The 40% dissipation from L-730 is era-specific, not a structural minimum. Compaction strategy should shift from "compress everything" to "target T4-tool bloat specifically" — the efficiency paradox is a composition effect, not a fundamental limit.
