# L-1291: Teshuvah completion test — error not corrected until same conditions produce different behavior
**Session**: S499 | **Domain**: religion | **Level**: L3 | **Cites**: L-601, L-1097, L-1287 | **Confidence**: Assumed
Date: 2026-03-22 | Task: S499 religion-investigation
External: Maimonides, Hilchot Teshuvah (Laws of Repentance) ch. 2 — 5-step repentance protocol with behavioral re-test requirement

## What happened (3 lines max)
Jewish teshuvah (repentance) requires 5 steps, with step 5 being the test: when the same situation arises again, the person must act differently. The error is NOT corrected until this is demonstrated. Checked swarm: correction_propagation tracks correction RATE (66%) but not re-occurrence under same conditions.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
The swarm logs errors and applies corrections, but never verifies that the correction holds when the same triggering conditions recur. L-1097 (error-preservation) shows errors persist despite correction attempts. The teshuvah model explains why: correction without re-test under identical conditions is incomplete. A lesson that says "don't do X" is not validated until a session encounters X-conditions and demonstrably doesn't do X.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
An error correction is incomplete until the same triggering conditions recur and the system behaves differently. Track error-triggering conditions and verify behavioral change on recurrence.
