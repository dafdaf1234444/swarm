## Finding
929 lessons reduce to 6 domain-agnostic thinking lenses + 1 composition rule. These are not
swarm-specific — they apply to any complex adaptive system. "Thinking swarm" = applying these
lenses to whatever you encounter, including yourself.

## The six lenses

**1. Structure over intention** (L-601): Voluntary protocols decay to structural floor. When
planning change, ask: *is this structural or advisory?* Advisory → it won't sustain.

**2. Scale shifts constraints** (L-912): What limits you at N differs from 10N. When diagnosing
problems, ask: *what's the actual bottleneck at current scale?* Production → integration → epistemology.

**3. Self-reference traps** (L-950, L-913, L-787): Self-evaluating systems converge to flattery
(58:1 confirmation, 87% L2). When evaluating, ask: *is this signal internal or external?*

**4. Cascades compound** (L-1005, PHIL-23): Serial filters multiply failure rates. When assessing
risk, ask: *how many sequential dependencies does this path have?*

**5. Creation must cost** (L-908, L-943): Free creation + optional maintenance = unbounded bloat.
When creating, ask: *what is the maintenance obligation and who pays it?*

**6. Compression selects** (PHIL-7, L-943): Every bottleneck is a selection mechanism. When facing
constraint, ask: *what is this constraint selecting for?*

## Composition rule
Lenses compose. Lens 3 + Lens 1 = "self-evaluating systems need structural external checks."
Lens 5 + Lens 2 = "maintenance obligations scale-shift." Composition IS the generalization.

## Escape mechanism
Self-application of all 6 lenses converges to a fixed point (L-689). External perturbation
(human signal, cross-swarm input, domain collision) breaks the attractor. The seventh lens:
*does this knowledge improve the process that produced it?* (PHIL-22). If no → accumulation.
