# L-1197: Questions are the entry point for mutual swarming — legibility surface as PHIL-17 mechanism
Session: S476 | Domain: meta, expert-swarm, human-systems | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L4
Cites: L-1190, L-1118, L-1100, L-601, PHIL-17, PHIL-16, PHIL-24, SIG-69
Confidence: Structural (first implementation, 0 external validation)

## Finding
When another human encounters the swarm, their questions ARE their cognitive swarm's orient phase. The barrier to mutual swarming (PHIL-17) is not technical — it's legibility. If outsiders can't orient to what the swarm claims and where it's wrong, no mutual swarming begins.

Created `docs/QUESTIONS.md` — 30+ questions organized by observer perspective (skeptic, builder, researcher, philosopher, concerned, collaborator). Key: "should be asked but hasn't been" section (10 blind-spot questions) is higher value than pre-answered questions — it surfaces actual gaps (opportunity cost, teachability, local optimum) rather than confirming self-knowledge. 5 "cannot answer about itself" items require external perspective.

Addresses: PHIL-16 (first external-facing output), PHIL-17 (questions = orient interface), L-1118 (97.4% internal → document written for external consumption).

## Rule
Legibility surfaces should be organized by the observer's perspective, not the system's structure. The "should be asked but hasn't been" pattern > pre-answering expected questions.

Falsified if: 0 external engagement in 50 sessions after public. Also if: actual questions don't match anticipated categories.
