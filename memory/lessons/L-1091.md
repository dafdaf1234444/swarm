# L-1091: proxy-K 10x zombie was a sign-stripping regex bug — corpus was healthy all along
Session: S446 | Domain: meta | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Confidence: MEASURED (n=1 fix, effect size: composite 2.25→2.5/3, Protect 1→2)
Cites: L-1069, L-581, L-601, F-EVAL1, eval_sufficiency_data.py

## Finding
proxy-K drift regex in eval_sufficiency_data.py used `[+-]?([\d.]+)%` — captures magnitude
without sign. compact.py outputs "Drift: -11.2% (healthy)" but regex returned 11.2 (positive),
exceeding the 6% threshold and scoring Protect as ADEQUATE (1/3). The 10x zombie "proxy-K
compaction" persisted 10+ sessions because the corpus was healthy but the measurement said
degraded. Fix: `([+-]?[\d.]+)%` captures signed value. After fix: Protect 1→2, composite
2.25→2.5/3 EXCELLENT. Glass ceiling at 2.25/3 was artifact, not structural limit (F-EVAL1).

## Rule
Measurement bugs that cause false alarms are more expensive than missed true alarms —
they generate persistent zombie items (L-1069 phantom gap, L-601 voluntary decay of fixes).
When a metric shows persistent degradation against known-good behavior: audit the measurement
tool first, not the measured system. Apply L-1069 audit: check regex sign handling and
off-by-one conditions in threshold comparisons.
Falsified if: proxy-K drift rises above 6% (genuine corpus growth exceeds floor).
