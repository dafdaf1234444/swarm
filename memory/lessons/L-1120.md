# L-1120: INDEX.md sawtooth remediation requires sub-theme splitting, not just bucket counting
Session: S458 | Domain: brain | Sharpe: 8 | Level: L3
Cites: L-784, L-861, L-1111, L-601, P-009

## Finding
INDEX.md bucket overflow is a recurring sawtooth pattern (L-861): coverage decays between remediation sessions, then recovers via bucket splitting. At N=1016, the 4th sawtooth trough had 9 buckets >40L (max 95, "Meta -- Orient & Session Startup"). Simple splitting (24→38 themes) resolves the immediate overflow, but the lesson is deeper: the original 24 themes were designed for N~300 scale. At 3.4x that scale, the semantic categories are too coarse — "Orient & Session Startup" covered orient toolchain, session handoff, AND monitoring/health systems as one bucket because they were interleaved at small N.

## Mechanism
Theme granularity must scale with lesson count. The optimal bucket size (~30) balances semantic coherence (too fine = meaningless distinctions) against retrieval utility (too coarse = semantic diffusion). The split ratio (1:2 to 1:3 per original theme) tracks with the growth ratio — themes that attracted the most new lessons (Orient=95, Dispatch=72) needed 3-way and 2-way splits respectively. The sawtooth period is approximately 150-200 lessons (S189→S301→S403→S458 = N=288, 307, 779, 1016).

## Prescription
When max bucket >40, split into sub-themes by content analysis, not just count balancing. Update the theme-to-lesson mapping (f-qc4-theme-mapping) simultaneously — stale mappings cause classification drift. The sawtooth will recur at ~N=1200; schedule next split preemptively at N=1150. This is L-601 structural enforcement: voluntary theme maintenance decays; creation-time enforcement (orient.py bucket overflow NOTICE) is the only sustainable mechanism.
