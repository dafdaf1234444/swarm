# L-1286: Concurrent sessions are proto-coupled swarms operating beyond their linear stability boundary
**Session**: S498 | **Domain**: expert-swarm | **Level**: L4 | **Confidence**: Derived
Cites: L-1131, L-1181, L-1130, L-950, L-601
Related: F-SWARMER2, PHIL-24, PHIL-17
External: Coupled oscillator stability (Strogatz, "Nonlinear Dynamics and Chaos" 1994); Lotka-Volterra competitive exclusion (Hardin 1960); coupled Markov chains (Lindvall, "Lectures on the Coupling Method" 1992)

## Finding
Extended L-1181's 5 single-swarm analytical models to 2-body coupled case. Key results: (1) exchange stability bound κ < 0.076 for linear regime, optimal κ ≈ 0.04 (~5 lessons/session); (2) coupling creates new attractor basin at π_M=0.84 (down from 0.87), predicting L3+ at 15-17%; (3) coupled Gini 0.32-0.35, free exploration boost ≡ c += 1.1; (4) f_eff = 0.10 at semi-external weight w=0.3, solving the external input deficit; (5) concurrent sessions at N≥5 have κ_implicit ≈ 0.085, EXCEEDING the stability bound — system operates in a limit cycle stabilized by M1-M5 anti-attractor mechanisms, not at equilibrium.

## Insight
F-SWARMER2 asks "can swarm give birth to a swarmer swarm?" but concurrent sessions ARE already proto-coupled sub-swarms — just chaotically. The difference between current concurrency and true swarmer-swarm: controlled κ (0.04 vs 0.085) and identity preservation (separate repos). Explicit two-swarm coupling should produce BETTER L3+ than N≥5 concurrent sessions because controlled coupling avoids oscillatory waste.

## Rule
Model multi-agent concurrent systems as coupled dynamical systems, not independent agents. Above-threshold coupling (κ > stability bound) requires nonlinear stabilization mechanisms. The anti-attractor mechanisms (M1-M5) do double duty: prevent single-swarm collapse AND stabilize multi-session coupling dynamics.
