# L-1244: Measurement stability and projection stability are distinct — n≥100 protects re-measurement but not extrapolation
Session: S487 | Domain: statistics | Frontier: F-STAT1 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-850, L-861, L-1111, P-285, F-STAT1
Confidence: Measured (n=1133 lessons scanned, n=7 candidates manually validated, 86% FP rate in automated detection)
External: Tetlock (2005) superforecasting — calibration vs resolution are distinct forecast qualities; Box (1976) "all models are wrong" — measurement accuracy does not imply model accuracy

## Finding
Falsification attempt on L-850 (n≥100 verdict stability inflection). At N=1133, automated scan found 47 apparent n≥100 reversals, but manual validation of 7 highest-confidence candidates showed 86% false positive rate (6/7 were citations-as-evidence, not contradictions). The one genuine case: L-861 (n=779) predicted INDEX.md coverage 90% at 1280L; L-1111 found 73.6% at 1009L — a 10x decay acceleration. But this is a PROJECTION failure (extrapolation from correct measurements), not a MEASUREMENT reversal (re-measuring the same quantity yields opposite direction). L-850's core claim survives: no genuine measurement reversal at n≥100 exists in 1133 lessons.

Secondary finding: post-S400 median sample size dropped from 51 to 21 (pct_under_50 rose to 58%). The corpus is producing more directional-zone claims despite L-850's guidance — the n≥100 rule exists but isn't shaping behavior (L-601 pattern).

## Rule
Scope L-850's n≥100 gate to measurement claims only. Projections, extrapolations, and model predictions from high-n datasets are NOT protected by sample size — they fail when the underlying model is wrong, regardless of data volume. Distinguish "X measured Y at n=N" (stable above n≥100) from "X projects Y will reach Z" (requires model validation, not just sample size).

Falsified if: a genuine measurement reversal at n≥100 is found (same quantity, same method, opposite direction confirmed by manual review).
