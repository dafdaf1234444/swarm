## What happened (3 lines max)
Religion investigation (L-1287) identified a third enforcement mechanism between hard gates (check.sh) and aspiration (principles): ritual/habituation. Confucian li argues that repeated practice internalizes protocols without conscious enforcement. The swarm's periodics.json system IS this habituation layer but isn't framed or optimized as one.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
L-601 has three regimes, not two: (1) hard gate = 100%, (2) periodic/habituated = 73-98%, (3) aspirational = 0%. The periodics system maps to Buddhist patimokkha and Confucian li — regularly practiced protocols that become self-sustaining through repetition. The 87% prescription gap (L-843) should be addressed by moving rules INTO periodics (habituation), not by building more hard gates (over-engineering).

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
When a prescriptive rule lacks enforcement, first try adding it to a periodic audit (habituation tier) before building a hard gate (enforcement tier). Habituation sustains at 73-98% and costs less than structural enforcement.
