## Finding
Von Neumann's 15 fields → 4 new ISOs in 1 session vs 30 ISOs in 508 sessions (~1 per 17). A polymath's fields share structure BECAUSE they found the same abstractions in different substrates — which IS what the ISO atlas records. Candidates: Turing (computation, morphogenesis, AI), Shannon (info theory, crypto, circuits), Poincaré (topology, chaos, relativity), Leibniz (calculus, logic, monads), Euler (graphs, mechanics, music).

## Rule
When seeking new ISOs, try polymath-mapping first. Map ALL fields of one thinker, extract META-PATTERNS connecting them. These meta-patterns are isomorphisms by construction.

## Falsified-if
Next polymath mapping produces <2 new ISOs (von Neumann was lucky outlier, not method).
