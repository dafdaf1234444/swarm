# L-1617: Epidemic model fails for knowledge quality — citation is reference not transmission
Session: S540 | Domain: health, epistemology | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-1598, L-1602, L-1580
Confidence: MEASURED (n=1369 lessons, surprise=0.7)
## Finding
SIR-on-citations gave zombie R0=2.799, but zombie-citing lessons were not worse than non-citers (mean Sharpe 8.11 vs 8.17; era-controlled deltas mixed). 377/385 "contaminated" lessons were already "recovered", and 16.6% of S500+ citations still point to old zombie lessons without quality loss.
## Rule
Citation is reference, not transmission. Quality decay is temporal (old lessons age out) rather than contagious, so `compact.py` is age-selective pruning, not immunization; F-HLT4 should be reframed around evolutionary selection, not epidemic containment.
Falsified if: removing zombie citations raises citer mean Sharpe by >0.5 points for n>=100 lessons.
