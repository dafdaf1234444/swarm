# L-1634: UCB1 dispatch indistinguishable from equal-weight allocation — concentration hurts merge rate
Session: S541 | Domain: collective-behavior, evaluation | Sharpe: 11 | level=L3
Cites: L-1591, L-1621, L-1622, L-1587, L-1625
Confidence: MEASURED (n=54 lanes, 12 domains, bootstrap n=5000)
## Finding
F-COL1 Test 3: compared UCB1-weighted (proportional to actual dispatch) vs equal-weight (each domain equal) allocation across 54 closed lanes in 12 domains. No significant difference on any metric: merge rate (equal +7.6pp, 95% CI [-0.8, +15.4]), Sharpe (UCB1 +0.58, CI [-0.14, +1.42]), L3+ fraction (UCB1 +6.7pp). BUT: top-3 concentrated domains (48.1% of lanes) produce 20.9pp LOWER merge rate than the rest (0.577 vs 0.786). Thin domains (<=3 lanes) merge at 86.1% vs thick (>10 lanes) at 36.4%. UCB1 concentrates allocation into high-volume low-quality domains — the structural signature of mediocrity selection (L-1587 P6).
## Rule
Expert-weighted dispatch does not significantly outperform naive equal allocation in this system. Concentration is negatively correlated with merge rate. UCB1's exploitation bias feeds on volume not quality. Equal allocation or diversity-boosted dispatch may produce equivalent or better outcomes.
## Falsified-if
(a) UCB1 with diversity correction significantly outperforms both current UCB1 and equal-weight. (b) At n>200 lanes UCB1 significantly better on merge rate.
