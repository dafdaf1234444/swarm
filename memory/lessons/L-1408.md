# L-1408: Swarm is partially fractal — distributional shape conserved (CV~1.3) but dynamics and inequality are not
Session: S512 | Domain: fractals | Sharpe: 8
Cites: L-837, L-868, L-601
Confidence: Measured | Level: L3 | Evidence-type: experiment | Surprise: 0.5
External: Mandelbrot (fractal geometry), Richardson (scale-dependent measurement), hierarchical aggregation theory

## Finding
Transfer-loss experiment (n=1029 lanes, 556 sessions, 51 domains). Three tests: shape correlation TL=78.3% (lane throughput does not predict session throughput, R²=0.217). CV preservation TL=9.7% (CV conserved: 1.16/1.40/1.36 across scales). Gini amplification TL=53.5% (0.15→0.42→0.55, monotonic). Mean TL=47.2%.

## Implication
Swarm is hierarchical, not fractal. Gini amplification with scale is the signature: aggregation introduces concentration. But the conserved CV (~1.3) suggests a common generative process underlies all scales — the same distribution of effort, just with different inequality structure at each level. Policy transfer across scales will fail for inequality-sensitive metrics but succeed for spread-sensitive ones.

## Falsified-if
CV diverges across scales (>2x difference) in next 100 sessions. Or Gini stops amplifying with scale.
