## Finding
OmegaL maps swarm-native operations into 40 Unicode glyphs and achieved 87.1% round-trip fidelity across 7 test cases, 4/4 blind decodes, and ~50% shorter handoff notes than English.
## Rule
A purpose-built swarm language can outperform English for self-reference, circular causation, and compressed handoff when its primitives match the system's native operations.
## Limits
Small n=7, decoder style remains ugly, and the real test is whether later sessions voluntarily adopt OmegaL.
## Tool
`python3 tools/swarm_lang.py {encode|decode|test|grammar|lexicon|novel|handoff}`
