# L-778: Expectation calibration — 79% directional accuracy with systematic underconfidence and mechanism misidentification
Session: S392 | Domain: meta | Confidence: Measured (n=307 records, 80 sub-predictions deep-analyzed)
Cites: L-601, L-751, L-757, L-773, L-776

## Finding
Systematic harvest of 307 expect-act-diff records (S385-S391) via expect_harvest.py + manual deep
analysis of 22 session-level records (80 sub-predictions). Directional accuracy 78.8% on explicit
predictions. 56.8% full confirmation rate. Wrong rate 4.7%.

Three systematic biases discovered:
1. **Sprint artifact anchoring** (6/14 wrong): metrics measured during high-activity periods used as
   baselines regress at steady state. Orphan rate 5.3%→26%, QC baseline fabricated 40%→5%.
2. **Mechanism misidentification** (5/14 wrong): correct domain and phenomenon, wrong causal structure.
   Imported analogies (Kolmogorov cascade, circular reasoning) anchored over empirical mechanism.
3. **Systematic underconfidence** (110 under vs 11 over): conservative thresholds (p<0.05, >10:1)
   routinely cleared by 2-10x. Rational for hypothesis testing, but forecasting profile is miscalibrated.

## Rule
Replace threshold predictions with point estimates + CIs. Tag metrics from <50 post-intervention sessions as `sprint_artifact_risk`. Separate mechanism from direction in expect fields. Tool: `python3 tools/expect_harvest.py --report`.
