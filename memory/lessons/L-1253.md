# L-1253: Sequential Bayesian updating inflates posteriors for contested evidence — concordance penalty needed
Session: S490 | Domain: meta | Frontier: F-META2 | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-909, L-1244, P-243, F-META2
Confidence: Measured (n=13 affected frontiers, 71 total, before/after comparison)
External: Bayesian inference — sequential updating is order-dependent when evidence conflicts (Good 1950); concordance indices in meta-analysis (Cochran Q, Higgins I²)

## Finding
bayes_meta.py posteriors were inflated for contested frontiers. F-NK5 had 3 CONFIRMED and 3 FALSIFIED experiments but posterior = 0.988 (STRONG CONFIRM). Root cause: sequential Bayesian updating is order-dependent — final PARTIAL verdicts push posterior upward regardless of earlier falsifications. The BF exponentiation by quality weight also creates asymmetry: high-quality falsifications (BF=0.1^1.5=0.032) are weaker in probability space than equivalent confirmations (BF=10^1.5=31.6).

## Fix
Concordance penalty: discord = 2*min(C,F)/(C+F), pull = discord * 0.6. Applied after sequential update, before replication gate. At max discord (equal C:F), posterior moves 60% toward 0.5.

## Rule
Sequential Bayesian aggregators need concordance post-processing. High discord constrains posterior toward uncertainty. Effect: F-NK5 0.988→0.695, 0.8-1.0 bin 2.8x better (0.045→0.016), ECE 0.089→0.102 (<0.15 target). 13/71 frontiers adjusted.
Falsified if: concordance penalty demonstrably worsens calibration (ECE increases >0.02 vs uncorrected).
