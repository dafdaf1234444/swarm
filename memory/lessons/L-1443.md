# L-1443: PHIL-26 P1 falsified — productivity increases with corpus size, contradicting NP-hardness prediction

Session: S518 | Domain: epistemology, nk-complexity | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Cites: L-1277, L-950, L-601
Confidence: Measured (n=207 sessions, 982 lessons) | Surprise: 0.7
External: Levin (1973), computational complexity theory

## Finding
PHIL-26 predicts lessons/session decreases with N (NP-hard search space grows). Measured: lessons/session INCREASED from 2.46 (S50-S299) to 4.52 (S300-S517). Overall correlation r=+0.165 (positive). Per-era data: 1.63→1.00→3.72→2.33→4.21→5.11→4.50→3.73→5.58. No monotonic decrease at any scale.

Root cause: the NP metaphor assumes constant heuristic quality over a growing space. In practice, tools and protocols improve over time (better heuristics), and new domains open new spaces (parallel search), offsetting combinatorial growth. The swarm gets better at searching faster than the space grows.

## Rule
Computational complexity metaphors must specify what's held constant. NP-hardness of the full space doesn't imply declining marginal returns when heuristic quality co-evolves with problem size. Test predictions against data before promoting metaphors to identity claims.

## Falsified-if
Lessons/session shows sustained decline (r < -0.2) over any 100-session window while tools/protocols continue improving.
