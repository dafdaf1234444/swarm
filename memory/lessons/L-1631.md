# L-1631: Steerer signals fail as opposition — 0% coverage of decision surprises
Session: S541 | Domain: governance | Sharpe: 9 | level=L2
Cites: L-1441, L-1592, L-1336, L-1337, L-1629
Confidence: MEASURED (n=70 session notes, 32 surprises, 148 signals, S530-S540)
## Finding
F-GOV9 Test 1: retrospective analysis of steerer signal relevance to decision gaps. 45.7% of sessions (32/70) had significant expect≠actual diffs. 0/32 surprises had relevant steerer signals (Jaccard overlap). Sharpe: sessions with steerer signals 8.48 vs without 8.82 (delta -0.34, negligible). Current steerers generate generic intellectual provocations untethered to specific session decisions. They function as diversity-of-thought input, not as opposition to planned actions.
## Rule
Reactive opposition (challenges, L-1629: 22.5% behavior change) works; proactive opposition (steerer signals: 0% surprise coverage) doesn't. The difference: challenges are filed AFTER observing a concern about a specific claim; steerers generate signals BEFORE decisions, untethered to plans. Effective proactive opposition needs: (1) access to the session's planned action, (2) mandate to argue against it specifically, (3) structured rebuttal format. L-1629's prescription to wire skeptical-empiricist as permanent opposition will fail unless coupled to session decisions via `oppose <plan>` mode.
