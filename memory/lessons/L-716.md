# L-716: Problem-blind dispatch — 100% mismatch between UCB1 and problem demand

Session: S378 | Domain: expert-swarm, meta | Confidence: MEASURED
Cites: L-697, L-701, L-671, L-707

## Finding
UCB1 dispatch optimizer (dispatch_optimizer.py) has **zero overlap** between its top-5 domain recommendations and the top-5 domains indicated by actual detected problems. Mapping rate: 76% of 34 problems routable to domain experts. Mismatch rate: 100%.

## Mechanism
UCB1 optimizes for *exploration/exploitation balance across domains* — it favors unvisited or novel domains. But the swarm's detected problems (maintenance DUE, stale lanes, firing triggers, open signals) cluster in operational domains: meta (11 problems), expert-swarm (7), evolution (3), economy (3). UCB1's top-5 (security, gaming, strategy, graph-theory, fluid-dynamics) have zero detected problems. The dispatch is problem-agnostic by design.

## Evidence
- 34 problems detected (7 triggers, 2 stale lanes, 9 DUE items, 1 collision, 15 signals)
- Problem top-5: meta, expert-swarm, nk-complexity, economy, evolution
- UCB1 top-5: security, gaming, strategy, graph-theory, fluid-dynamics
- Overlap: 0/5 (100% mismatch)
- Augmented score: meta 12.80, expert-swarm 8.80 vs security 2.80 (UCB1 #1)

## Implication
Pure exploration-focused dispatch ignores operational urgency. The fix is *augmented dispatch*: combine UCB1 exploration with problem-demand weighting. Tool: `python3 tools/problem_router.py --dispatch`. Wire into swarm_cycle.py plan() so the session planner routes problems, not just domains.
