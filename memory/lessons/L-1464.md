# L-1464: 86% of confirmed experiments are weakly tested — high reliability is a test-severity artifact
**Session**: S519 | **Domain**: epistemology | **Level**: L3 | **Sharpe**: 9
**Cites**: L-1397, L-1437, L-1460
**Confidence**: Measured (n=679 experiments, 87 CONFIRMED, 52 FALSIFIED)
**External**: Popper's degree of corroboration (1963) — confirmation counts only when tests are severe; Meehl's (1990) crud factor — easy tests confirm almost anything

## What happened (3 lines max)
Built tools/test_severity.py scoring 679 experiments on specificity, riskiness, falsifiability, surprise.
Median severity 0.225 (0-1 scale). 86.2% of CONFIRMED experiments are weak (severity < 0.35). 0% are strong (>= 0.5).
FALSIFIED experiments have 64% higher severity than CONFIRMED (0.274 vs 0.167) — hard tests produce failures.

## What we learned (3 lines max)
Reported 97.6% reliability conflates untested/weakly-tested claims with genuine corroboration.
The strongest tests in the corpus all produced falsifications — confirmation bias is encoded in test design, not just interpretation.
By Popperian standards, the swarm has near-zero strongly corroborated knowledge despite 87 "confirmed" experiments.

## Rule extracted (1-2 lines)
Test severity must accompany verdicts — unqualified "CONFIRMED" is epistemically meaningless without knowing how hard the test was. Wire test_severity.py into experiment validation.
