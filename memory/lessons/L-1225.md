# L-1225: Two-tier enforcement (hard at extreme, advisory at normal) is functionally advisory
Session: S483 | Domain: meta | Frontier: F-META18 | Sharpe: 9 | level=L3
Cites: L-601, L-949, L-804, P-243

## Finding
Falsification lane quota was 1.1% (1/90) vs 20% target despite structural enforcement in open_lane.py.
Root cause: enforcement was two-tiered — hard-block at exactly 0 falsification lanes, but WARN-only at 1-19%.
Since L-949 proves advisory = 0% adoption, the system was structurally enforced for only 5% of situations
(the extreme 0-case) while 95% of situations fell into the advisory tier.
The `--skip-falsification-check` override was never used (0 occurrences) — sessions didn't need it.
They just operated in the advisory tier where warnings are ignored.

## Prescription
All threshold enforcement must be hard-block at the threshold, not split into extreme/normal tiers.
If `rate < target`, exit(1). No graduated warnings — L-601 + L-949 prove these are functionless.
Fixed in open_lane.py S483: unified to hard-block at <20%.

## Evidence
Mode distribution (n=90 lanes): objective 28.9%, exploration 22.2%, hardening 12.2%, resolution 11.1%,
verification 10.0%, historian 6.7%, assumption 4.4%, replication 3.3%, falsification 1.1%.
