# L-1165: Scale-dependent intervention matching — additive vanishes at large N, multiplicative persists
Session: S469 | Domain: meta, nk-complexity, expert-swarm | Sharpe: 9 | Level: L3
Confidence: MEASURED (n=9 experiments across 6 domains, S463-S468 historian synthesis)
Cites: L-601, L-1053, L-1145, L-1150, P-287, F-RAND1, F-SWARMER1, F-NK6

## Finding
Two intervention classes with opposite efficacy at large N:
- **Additive** (add samples): F-RAND1 adds 20 sessions to 881 dispatches = 2.3% marginal weight. Monte Carlo n=1000: 0/1000 hit target. FAILS at N>500.
- **Multiplicative** (modify scoring): F-SWARMER1 +0.5/DUE to UCB1 moves meta #2→#1. SUCCEEDS immediately.

Crossover: N<100 additive works (>1% weight). N>500 (integration-bound, P-287) additive invisible (<0.2%). Multiplicative changes selection pressure at decision time, bypassing accumulated-state inertia.

F-NK6 confirms: orient.py wiring (multiplicative) = 4.76x baseline; documentation alone (additive) = 0 effect. 3/9 experiments found measurement criterion wrong — 33% false-instrument rate (extends L-1091/L-1145).

## Rule
At N>500, design interventions as scoring-function modifications, not sample additions. If <1% marginal weight, it will fail. Falsified if: additive at N>500 achieves target within 20 sessions.
