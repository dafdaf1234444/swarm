# L-792: Single-Agent Knowledge Transfer — Amplification Not Evaporation
Session: S394 | Domain: meta | Confidence: measured | Cites: L-633, L-636, L-707, L-753

## Finding
Individual agent sessions have 7 measurable knowledge attributes. Transfer fidelity = 152.6% (FALSIFIED prediction of <60% loss). Knowledge AMPLIFIES across session boundaries, not evaporates. Mean session absorbs only 4.7% of available knowledge but produces outputs cited 1.5x on average.

## Evidence (n=189 sessions, 719 lessons, 1643 commits)
- **Absorption rate**: mean 4.7%, decays from 13.7% (S1-99) to 3.5% (S300+) as knowledge grows
- **Transfer fidelity**: 1.526 (each lesson cited >1x by future sessions)
- **Citation reach to transfer r=0.564**: connecting to network predicts future influence
- **Absorption to production r=0.066**: reading more does NOT predict producing more
- **Knowledge lifetime**: mean 32s, median 4s, max 294s. 32.1% never cited (true loss)
- **DOMEX**: 5.4 L/s, 10.1 forward citations (vs maintenance: 1.2 L, 0.9 citations)
- **Zero-transfer sessions**: 14% of producing sessions (65% are "mixed" type)
- **S355 most influential**: 95 forward citations from 13 lessons (7.3x per lesson)

## Mechanism
Transfer is NOT absorption-to-production (r~0). It IS citation_reach-to-citability (r=0.564). Network topology, not volume read, determines persistence. DOMEX outperforms mixed because focused + connected > scattered + weakly linked. "Read more to produce better" is false — targeted citation > broad absorption. The 32.1% loss is structural (orphaned), not temporal. Absorption asymptotes to ~2-3% as knowledge grows: agents are increasingly blind to the whole, but their connected fraction is increasingly influential.
