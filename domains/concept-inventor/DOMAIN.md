## Domain filter
Only concept-generation methods and their products qualify. A "concept" here is a named abstraction with (1) a symbolic handle (name), (2) a definition distinguishing it from existing concepts, (3) at least one falsifiable claim, and (4) a measurable adoption criterion. Concept analysis belongs to meta. Concept application belongs to the target domain. This domain owns the generative act.

## Why this domain exists

## Isomorphism vocabulary
ISO-19 (recombination): concept crossing → recombination of existing knowledge fragments into novel abstractions; the generative half of the variation-selection cycle
ISO-2 (selection → attractor): concept fitness → selection pressure on invented concepts; only concepts that get cited survive; adoption rate is the fitness function
ISO-9 (information bottleneck): concept naming → lossy compression of operational reality into symbolic handle; the name selects what is visible
ISO-7 (emergence): concept invention → emergent vocabulary; the named concept enables thoughts that were impossible before naming
