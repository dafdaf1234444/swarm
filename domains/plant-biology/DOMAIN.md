## Relationship to other domains
- **farming**: Farming is the agricultural management metaphor (human-side). Plant biology is the organism-level mechanism (plant-side). Farming says "rotate crops"; plant biology says "why rotation works" (allelopathy, soil microbiome).
- **evolution**: Evolution provides population-level selection dynamics; plant biology provides organism-level developmental architecture. They complement at different scales.
- **expert-swarm**: Colony architecture (F-SWARMER1/2) maps to plant colony organisms (aspen groves sharing root systems). Swarmer-swarm IS mycorrhizal network.
- **brain**: Neural signaling parallels plant hormone signaling (auxin vs neurotransmitter). Both are gradient-based information systems.

## Isomorphism vocabulary
ISO-5 (feedback — stabilizing): auxin-cytokinin balance → stabilizing feedback between shoot growth and root growth; apical dominance → negative feedback suppresses lateral buds
ISO-4 (phase transition): flowering transition → irreversible developmental phase shift triggered by photoperiod threshold; seed germination → threshold crossing from dormant to active
ISO-7 (emergence): canopy architecture → emergent from local light-seeking rules; root network topology → emergent from local nutrient-seeking rules; mycorrhizal network intelligence → distributed resource allocation without central coordinator
ISO-2 (selection → attractor): shade avoidance syndrome → etiolation attractor under low-light; monoculture → genetic uniformity attractor under strong selection
ISO-6 (entropy): leaf litter decomposition → entropy recycling; senescence → controlled entropy release recovering nutrients
ISO-13 (integral windup): salt accumulation in irrigated soil → integral windup without flushing mechanism; hormone accumulation without degradation → runaway growth response
ISO-8 (power laws): root branching architecture → power-law distribution of root diameters; leaf size distribution → allometric scaling laws
